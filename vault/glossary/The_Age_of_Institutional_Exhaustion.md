# The Age of Institutional Exhaustion

**Timeline:** 2008 - 2032

The terminal phase of the 20th-century world order. This era was defined by a profound and irreversible loss of public trust in the central institutions of society—governments, media, academia, and finance. It was characterized not by a lack of resources, but by a lack of competence and legitimacy. During this period, the mechanisms of the state continued to operate, but they had lost the ability to solve problems or command the loyalty of the citizenry. It was the "zombie stage" of the old world, walking but spiritually dead, eventually succumbing to the cascading crises of the 2030s.
