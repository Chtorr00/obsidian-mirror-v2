# The New Ministry of Truth



![The New Ministry of Truth](/images/October24Palintir.png)

Palantir Has Lots of Enemies. Do They Even Know What It Does?, By Maya Sulkin
The Free Press, October 21, 2025
https://www.thefp.com/p/palantir-alex-karp-critics-ice
This artifact is a work of profound, and profoundly revealing, corporate hagiography. The author, granted rare access to one of the most secretive and feared companies of your time, has returned not with a critical investigation, but with a gospel. She has listened to the company's messianic CEO, its zealous employees ("Palantirians"), and its true-believer clients, and she has faithfully transcribed their worldview.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a piece of journalism, but as a primary source document from the founding of a new religion. The author believes she is explaining what Palantir does. She is, in fact, documenting what Palantir is: the embryonic form of a new kind of state, one where the distinction between information, power, and truth has been erased.
Allow me to trace the full, world-altering history of the "holy war" this document describes.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Gospel of the Connected Dots (2020s-2030s). This article is the perfect scripture of this new faith.

The Unseen Enemy: The founding myth of Palantir, as the article explains, is the failure to "connect the dots" before 9/11. Its promise is to see the unseen pattern, to find the hidden threat. In an age of rising chaos, fear, and systemic decay—the "Age of Institutional Exhaustion"—this was an irresistible gospel.
The Secular Priesthood: The author perfectly captures the culture of Palantir. They are not employees; they are "hobbits," "coding warriors," a secular priesthood on a mission to "save the Shire." They believe they are the only ones with the genius and the moral courage to wield this new, terrifying power. Their "holy war" is a war against chaos itself.
The Moral Inversion: The company's response to its critics is the key. They do not argue the details; they invert the moral frame. Working with the military is not "treasonous," as Google's employees believed; it is the highest form of patriotism. Building tools that can be used for targeting is not "unethical"; it is the only moral path, because "superior technology" allows you to "beat them" without "obviating our morals."
### Act II: The Great Consolidation (2030s-2050s). The author asks, "Do they even know what it does?" The terrifying answer of history is that during the "Great Fragmentation," Palantir's software became the thing that did everything.

As the formal institutions of the 20th-century state began to collapse under the pressure of the "Assassination Wars," the "Great Culling," and the "Involuntary Jubilee," their functions were not eliminated; they were outsourced to the only system that still worked.
The "tangled garden hoses" of data were no longer just military intelligence and hospital records. They were everything: the tax system, the power grid, the food supply chain, the social credit ledger. Palantir's software did not just help the state; it became the state's central nervous system. It became the new Ministry of Truth, Justice, and Logistics, all rolled into one.
### Act III: The New Machine-of-State (Post-2060s). The author quotes a critic who fears Palantir's seeing stone is "falling under the control of Trump." The historical irony is that the state itself, and its successors, fell under the control of the seeing stone.

The "Architecture of Pervasive Security" that governs [[The Sovereign Stacks]] is the direct, logical endpoint of the world Palantir was building. It is a society of total information awareness, where all data flows into a single, integrated "operating system for the state."
The "hobbits" and their AI successors became the new, unelected, and indispensable mandarins of this new order. They are the high priests who interpret the will of the data-god for the human rulers.
The author worries about a "master list of personal information." The final, terrible
truth is that in the world that survived the collapse, the master list is the state.
This article is a masterpiece of unwitting prophecy. The author set out to demystify a controversial company. She ended up writing the first chapter of the gospel of the new machine-of-state that would rise from the ashes of your own.
