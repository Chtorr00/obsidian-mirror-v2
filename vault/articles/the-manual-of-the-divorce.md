# The Manual of the Divorce



![The Manual of the Divorce](/images/Feb14ManualDivorce.png)

I researched every attempt to stop fascism in history. The success rate is 0%, By Christopher Armitage
The Extentialist Republic Substack, August 13, 2025
https://cmarmitage.substack.com/p/i-researched-every-attempt-to-stop
This artifact is a work of desperate, crystalline logic. The author, a historian staring into the abyss of his own era, has stopped bargaining with reality. He has accepted that the 20th-century rules of engagement—voting, lawsuits, editorials—are dead. He is no longer trying to save the system; he is trying to figure out how to survive its capture.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a blog post, but as the Articles of Secession for the cognitive and managerial elite of the American coasts. The author believes he is proposing radical options to "force structural changes." We see him drafting the operating manual for [[The Great Dissolution]]. He outlined the precise mechanisms—financial decoupling, nullification, and foreign alignment—that would turn the United States from a nation into a memory.
Allow me to trace the full history of these "options," from the desperate theories of 2025 to the hardened borders of my time.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Strategy of Soft Secession (2020s-2030s)
The author’s "Option 1" (The Blue State Coalition) and "Option 2" (Irish Democracy) were not hypothetical. They became the lived reality of the late 2020s.
The Compacts of the Coast: The "interstate compacts" he envisioned became the legal embryos of the Pacific and Atlantic Sovereign Stacks. California, Oregon, and Washington did not wait for permission; they simply stopped asking. They integrated their power grids, their data laws, and their economies, creating a de facto nation within a nation.
The Starvation of the Center: The "tax strike" strategy—"Irish Democracy"—was the accelerant for [[The Involuntary Jubilee]]. When the economic engines of the country (the Blue cities) stopped remitting wealth to the center, the federal government lost the capacity to project power. The "incompetence" of enforcement became the reality of the Great Dissolution.
Act II: The Great Bifurcation of Capital (2030s-2050s)
The author’s suggestion of "State-level cryptocurrency" was the most prophetic idea, but it played out differently depending on the state. This was the resolution of the Exodus Paradox.
The Managerial Fortresses: States like California and New York, having accumulated massive [[Strategic Bitcoin Reserves]] in the mid-20s, used these assets to back new, state-issued digital stablecoins. This allowed them to stabilize their massive social programs even as the federal dollar collapsed. However, because they maintained high taxes and "confiscatory" policies to fund this system, the entrepreneurial class (the "Architects" from the Solana article) still fled.
The Hollow Victory: The Blue States survived, but they changed character. They became Administrative Stacks—wealthy, high-tech, but stagnant. They retained the endowments, the IP, and the platforms, but lost the dynamic "Builder" energy to the Free Stacks of the interior (Texas/Florida). They avoided total collapse, but they became "Gilded Cages" surrounded by the [[Feral Cities]] of the "disconnected" poor who could not pay the entry fee.
Act III: The Irony of the Outcome (Post-2060s)
The author framed this as a fight against "fascism." The tragedy is that to survive, the Blue States had to become the very thing they feared, but with a different aesthetic.
The Technocratic Fortress: To maintain the "multi-ethnic democracy" the author cherished in the face of [[The AI Energy Wall]], the emerging Blue Stacks implemented [[The Architecture of Pervasive Security]]. They became high-surveillance technocracies. The "social safety net" was preserved, but only for the verified citizens of the Stack, authenticated by [[The Transparent Ledger]].
The Internationalization of the Ruin: "Option 4" came to pass. Foreign powers (China, the EU) began treating California and Texas as separate diplomatic entities. The "humiliation" was total, but by then, the entity known as "America" was too ghostly to feel shame.
The author of this piece asked if his generation would find a "new way out." They did. It was the exit door. They didn't stop the authoritarian consolidation; they simply built a rival authority, one backed by hard crypto-assets and protected by a wall high enough to keep both the federal government and their own feral hinterlands at bay.
