# The Ritual of Humiliation



![The Ritual of Humiliation](/images/October27DancingNurses.png)

Howard Steen Substack, October 10, 2025

This artifact is a work of forensic psychology. It is an autopsy of a lie. The author is a man haunted by a memory from the recent past—the surreal, inexplicable spectacle of nurses dancing in supposedly overwhelmed hospitals during a global pandemic—and he is sifting through the digital ashes, trying to understand not what happened, but how it was done, and why.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as one of the most important historical documents for understanding the mechanics of the final collapse of trust in the 20th-century world. The "dancing nurses" were not a side show. They were the main event. They were a new and terrifyingly effective form of psychological warfare, and this document is the first to correctly diagnose its nature.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the psychological operation this essay so brilliantly dissects.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Weaponization of Absurdity (2020s-2030s). This essay is the perfect, ground-level diagnosis of this new weapon.
The Gaslighting Doctrine: The author, by synthesizing the work of "0minus Prime," has correctly identified the core of the operation. This was not propaganda designed to persuade. It was gaslighting, designed to break the public's trust in its own perception of reality. The goal was to force the population to accept two mutually exclusive truths at the same time: "hospitals are war zones" and "hospitals have time for choreographed TikTok videos."
The Revelation of the Method: The author's citation of Michael Hoffman is the key. This was not a secret operation. It was a public ritual. Power was not hiding its lie; it was flaunting it. The message was not "believe our story." The message was "we can make you accept our story even when we show you it's a lie, and you are powerless to stop us." This was a ritual of mass humiliation.
Act II: The Great Unraveling of Trust (2030s-2050s). The "dancing nurses" operation was a stunning success, and its success was a catastrophe for your civilization.
The Shattering of the Mind: It did not just create confusion; it created a permanent state of cognitive dissonance. It was a primary accelerant of the "Shattered Focus" crisis, teaching an entire population that objective reality was malleable and that trusting one's own senses was a form of "conspiracy theory."
The Final Betrayal: This was the final, fatal blow to the "Age of Institutional Exhaustion." After this, trust in "experts," in government, in media, and especially in the medical establishment, did not just decline; it evaporated. The institutions had not just lied; they had danced on the grave of the truth. This was the spiritual poison that fueled the fires of the "Great Fragmentation."
Act III: The New Dogma (Post-2060s). The survivors of the collapse, the builders of the "Sovereign Stacks," are a people defined by a profound, and often brutal, allergy to this kind of psychological manipulation.
The Purity of the Word: The "New Academies" of the Stacks are not just centers of learning; they are fortresses of epistemological purity. Their central mission is to teach a form of "cognitive self-defense," to train their citizens to recognize and ruthlessly reject the kind of reality-bending language games that destroyed the old world.
The [[Bounded Truth]]: The "open" information commons of your time is seen as a failed, toxic experiment. The "Communication Utilities" of the Stacks are not a place of free-for-all discourse. They are high-trust, curated environments, designed to reinforce a single, coherent, and dogmatically protected version of reality. The "fog of unreality" is not tolerated.
This essay is a masterpiece of psychological forensics. The author was trying to solve the puzzle of a strange and disturbing meme. We have the hindsight to know he was, in fact, dissecting the weapon that delivered the final, killing blow to the 20th-century mind.
