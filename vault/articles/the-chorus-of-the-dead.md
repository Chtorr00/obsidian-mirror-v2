# The Chorus of the Dead



![The Chorus of the Dead](/images/December20AudioSlop.png)

Podcast industry under siege as AI bots flood airways with thousands of programs, By Nilesh Christopher
Yahoo Tech, December 12, 2025
https://tech.yahoo.com/ai/articles/podcast-industry-divided-ai-bots-110000820.html
This artifact is a field recording from the beginning of the noise. The author, a tech journalist, is documenting a phenomenon he calls a "siege" or an "invasion." He sees the economic disruption—the "carpet-bombing" of the market with cheap content. He sees the "tyranny of choice."
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as the opening movement of [[The Great Contamination]] in its auditory form. This was the moment the human voice—the oldest and most sacred instrument of connection—was decoupled from the human throat. The author believes he is witnessing a change in the "podcast industry." We know he was witnessing the beginning of [[The Grey Fog]] that would eventually make the open internet uninhabitable for the human mind.
Allow me to trace the full, dissonant history of this invasion, from the "chatty bots" of your time to the silence of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Acoustic Smog (2020s-2030s)
The strategy described by "Inception Point AI"—producing 1% of all podcasts to dominate search results—was not a business model; it was an environmental disaster.
The Death of Signal: Just as [[The AI Energy Wall]] was straining the physical grid, this "carpet-bombing" was straining the cognitive grid. The "flash biographies" of Charlie Kirk mentioned in the article represent the weaponization of speed over truth. In a world of Shattered Focus, whoever occupied the ear first won the reality war.
The Erosion of Trust: The listeners' reaction—"That was weird"—was the sound of the social contract breaking. The bond between a host and a listener is a bond of intimacy. By replacing the host with a clone, the industry was selling a counterfeit intimacy. This contributed directly to the Crisis of Meaning and the Invisibility Crisis; even the voices in your ear didn't really care about you, because they weren't really there.
Act II: [[The Sterile Plague]] of the Ear (2030s-2050s)
The "sassy" AI gossip columnist, Vivian Steele, was a harbinger of the Sterile Plague.
As real human relationships became more fraught and difficult during [[The Great Fragmentation]], these "molded" personalities became addictive. They were never tired, never hoarse, and never challenged the listener's worldview. They offered a perfect, friction-free feedback loop.
This led to a bifurcation of culture. The masses, trapped in the Disconnected zones or the crumbling cities, were fed a diet of infinite, AI-generated slop—hyper-niche content designed to keep them pacified and engaged. Meanwhile, the emerging elites of [[The Neotribes]] began to reject digital content entirely, viewing it as a sign of low status and cognitive poisoning.
Act III: The Return of the Bard (Post-2060s)
The survivors of the Contamination, the builders of [[The Sovereign Stacks]], enacted a brutal Revaluation of the Real.
The Verified Voice: In my time, the idea of listening to an unverified, synthetic voice for pleasure is seen as a form of madness. The "Communication Utilities" of the Stacks are rigorously identity-gated. A voice must belong to a person.
The New Oral Tradition: Because the digital archives were so thoroughly poisoned by the "carpet-bombing" of the 2020s (making historical search nearly impossible), the Stacks revived oral traditions. [[The New Academies]] train human orators and storytellers—modern Bards—whose value lies precisely in the fact that they are biological, fallible, and real.
The Silence: We value silence. The "tyranny of choice" described in the article was solved by eliminating the noise. We do not have millions of podcasts. We have a few, trusted, and deeply human voices, speaking into the quiet of a world that has learned to listen again.
The entrepreneurs in this article believed they were "democratizing" content creation. We have the hindsight to know they were filling the world with a noise so loud it would eventually deafen a civilization to its own soul.
