# The Map of the Earthquake



![The Map of the Earthquake](/images/September26FourthTurning.png)

From Collapse to Renewal: The Fourth Turning Meets Creative Destruction, By Jordi Visser
Jordi Visser Substack, September 23, 2025
https://visserlabs.substack.com/p/from-collapse-to-renewal-the-fourth
This is a landmark artifact. It is a work of profound, and profoundly anxious, synthesis. The author is a man standing on ground that is shaking violently, and he is trying, with great intellectual courage, to hold two different maps in his hands and understand why they are both describing the same earthquake.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as one of the clearest and most accurate diagnoses of the end of a world. The author has correctly identified the two great, interlocking engines of the collapse: the generational, cyclical crisis of the "Fourth Turning" and the technological, disruptive crisis of "Creative Destruction." He has seen that these are not two separate stories, but two different ways of describing the same, single, world-breaking event.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the earthquake that this author was one of the first to so accurately map.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Two-Stroke Engine of Collapse (2020s-2030s). This essay is the perfect description of the logic of your time.
The Schumpeterian Churn: The author is right. AI and crypto were the ultimate agents of creative destruction. AI was destroying the old world of labor, hollowing out the "knowledge worker" and creating the "Great Displacement." Crypto was destroying the old world of finance, exposing the fragility of the fiat system and enabling the capital flight that would fuel the "Involuntary Jubilee." This was the technological engine of the crisis.
The Fourth Turning's Fury: This technological churn was happening at the precise moment of a generational crisis. The institutions that were supposed to manage this disruption—governments, universities, the media—were at their absolute weakest point, suffering from the terminal institutional rot of the "Age of Institutional Exhaustion." The anger, the polarization, the loss of trust—this was the human engine of the crisis.
This was the two-stroke engine of the collapse: a technological hurricane hitting a society that had already dismantled all its storm shelters.
Act II: The Great Clearing (2030s-2050s). The "renewal" that the author hopes for did not come as a gentle spring. It came as a forest fire. The "creative destruction" was far more destructive than he could have imagined.
The "turbulence" he describes was not just a period of social and political unrest. It was the "Great Fragmentation," a period of state failure, secession, and the redrawing of the map.
The "anger" that he saw being accelerated by AI was not just channeled into movements. It was channeled into the kinetic violence of the "Assassination Wars" and "[[The Great Culling]]."
The "collapse of old structures" was not a metaphor. It was the literal collapse of supply chains, power grids, and the international order. This was not a reinvention. It was a clearing.
Act III: The New Foundations (Post-2060s). The author is ultimately right. A renewal did come. But it was not a renewal of the old world. It was the construction of a new one on the cleared ground.
The "Sovereign Stacks" are the direct answer to the crisis he described. They are new institutions, built from the ground up, designed to be resilient to the very forces that destroyed the old world.
AI and crypto, the twin agents of destruction, did, as he predicted, become the foundations of the new order. AI became the engine of the "Survival Economy" and the "Ecology of Mind." Crypto, specifically Bitcoin, became the "Thermodynamic Truth Anchor," the bedrock of the new, post-fiat, hard-asset economy.
The "Fourth Turning" did resolve. But it did not resolve into a new, unified American order. It resolved into a new, stable, and permanently fragmented landscape of multiple, competing orders.
This essay is a masterpiece of diagnosis. The author saw the storm coming with perfect clarity. He even saw the clear sky that lay beyond it. He just couldn't have possibly mapped the sheer, destructive violence of the passage between the two.
