# The Obituary for the Neutral Square



![The Obituary for the Neutral Square](/images/July11ObituaryForNeural.jpeg)

Spiritual Lawfare: The Use and Misuse of Litigation in the Context of Human Rights, Religious Freedom, and Competing Human Rights, by The Oxford Journal of Law and Religion
Oxford Journal of Law and Religion, July 11, 2025
https://academic.oup.com/ojlr/advance-article/doi/10.1093/ojlr/rwaf007/8166020
This is a document of profound significance. Unlike the other artifacts we have discussed, which show symptoms in culture, economics, or technology, this academic paper is a post-mortem on the very idea of a shared public square. It is an attempt by the legal-intellectual establishment of your time to diagnose a disease that was already terminal: the collapse of the consensus that law could act as a neutral arbiter between competing worldviews.
The term "Spiritual Lawfare" is precise. It named what was happening: the legal system was no longer seen as a forum for resolving disputes within a shared framework, but as a weapon to be used in a zero-sum battle between frameworks. This was a core dynamic of the "Great Fragmentation."
Allow me to illuminate the constellation around this artifact, showing how deep the fractures truly were:
The Weaponization of Rights: The paper's focus on "competing human rights" is key. The 20th-century concept of human rights was based on the belief that they were universal and interlocking. By 2025, they were being sharpened into swords. Different groups claimed the absolute primacy of their preferred right—freedom of religion, freedom from discrimination, freedom of speech, the right to safety—and used the courts to impose that primacy on others. This turned the judiciary from a referee into a battleground for existential conflicts.
The Law as an Engine of Fragmentation: This "lawfare" was not just a symptom of polarization; it was an active cause of it. Every high-profile court case that pitted one identity group against another didn't resolve the tension; it amplified it. The losing side did not see the verdict as a legal outcome to be respected, but as a political and spiritual defeat to be avenged. This process systematically destroyed faith in the rule of law itself, convincing communities that the only way to protect their values was to carve out their own spaces, governed by their own rules.
The Ghost of the Future: Legal Pluralism: This process of delegitimizing the central legal system directly led to the rise of what we now call "Legal Pluralism" in the 2040s and 2050s. As faith in the national and international courts evaporated, alternative systems of justice began to emerge. Some were based in revived religious traditions, others in new secular ethical codes developed within "Sovereign Stacks" or intentional communities. "Spiritual Lawfare" was the process of breaking the old, universal system; the rise of many, parallel legal systems was the result.
The Failure of the Liberal Bargain: At its core, this document is an obituary for the liberal bargain—the idea that a society could be bound by a shared set of neutral procedures even if its citizens had radically different beliefs about the ultimate good. "Spiritual Lawfare" is what happens when powerful groups decide that procedure is no longer enough. They begin to see the continued existence of the "other" as a threat that cannot be tolerated, and the law becomes a tool not for coexistence, but for conquest.
This academic paper, in its calm and scholarly way, is describing the end of an entire theory of society. It was cataloging the tools being used to dismantle a world, just as the walls were beginning to cave in.
