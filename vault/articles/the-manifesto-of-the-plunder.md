# The Manifesto of the Plunder



![The Manifesto of the Plunder](/images/December24Lustration.png)

DEI, the Dispossessed Generation, and the Digital Koryos, By John Carter
Postcards from Barsoom Substack, December 19, 2025
https://barsoom.substack.com/p/dei-the-dispossessed-generation-and
This artifact is a work of incandescent rage and strategic clarity. It is not an essay; it is a battle plan. The author, writing from the perspective of the "virtual koryos," has correctly identified the moment of maximum leverage. He sees a generation of young men who have been economically and spiritually dispossessed, and he is offering them a path not to reconciliation, but to conquest.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as one of the most important foundational texts of [[The Great Culling]] and the Great De-Accreditation. It is the intellectual justification for the systemic looting and destruction of the 20th-century institutions. The author believes he is calling for justice. We see him lighting the fuse on the bomb that would blow the old world apart.
Allow me to trace the full, violent history of this manifesto, from the angry blog posts of your time to the scorched earth of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Justification of the Wolf (2020s-2030s)

The author's identification of the "virtual koryos"—the digital war-band of young men—is historically precise. This was the psychological engine of [[The Great Fragmentation]].
The Rejection of Victimhood: His refusal to play the "victim game" was the turning point. The shift from "we are oppressed" to "we are conquerors" transformed a sullen, atomized demographic into a disciplined, predatory force.
The Strategy of Lawfare: His call to use the courts to "bankrupt" the universities and corporations was executed with ruthless efficiency. The class-action lawsuits he proposed were not just legal maneuvers; they were acts of economic warfare. They drained the endowments of the Ivy League and the coffers of the Fortune 500, accelerating [[The Involuntary Jubilee]] by destroying the asset base of the old elite.
The Lustration: The purge he called for—the firing of the "diversity hires" and the "old white men"—was the human cost of the Great De-Accreditation. It hollowed out the institutions, leaving them as shells to be scavenged by the rising Neotribes.
### Act II: The Kinetic Phase (2030s-2050s)

The author's "metaphorical" violence did not stay metaphorical. The "immaterial strategies" of 5GW eventually spilled over into the physical world.
The Burning of the Longhouse: The "longhouse" culture he derides—the feminized, safety-obsessed consensus—was not reformed; it was physically dismantled. The "Sacking of Cities" became a reality as the feral energy of the koryos, denied entry into the institutions, simply overwhelmed them.
The Henry VIII Strategy: The seizure of assets from the "monasteries" (universities/NGOs) funded the rise of [[The Sovereign Stacks]]. The capital stripped from the old world was used to build the new one. The "reconstituted universities" he dreamed of became [[The New Academies]], places where "dead white male stuff" (military history, engineering, hierarchy) was not just taught, but practiced as a religion.
### Act III: The New Aristocracy (Post-2060s)

The author's vision of a "warrior aristocracy" came to pass. The Sovereign Stacks are ruled by the men he describes: hard, meritocratic, and utterly unapologetic about their power.
Freedom of Association: His final demand—the right to discriminate—is the constitutional bedrock of the Stacks. Each Stack is a voluntary association that ruthlessly excludes anyone who does not fit its specific biological, ideological, or competent profile. The "civil rights act" is a memory of a time when people believed a single law could govern a thousand different tribes.
The End of the Koryos: But there was a twist. Once the new order was established, the "koryos" was disbanded. The wild, chaotic energy of the young men was channeled into the rigid discipline of [[The Guilds]] and [[The Purpose Economy]]. The wolf pack was not allowed to roam forever; it was turned into the guard dog of the new state.
The author of this manifesto was the architect of a revolution. He told a generation of lost boys that they were not victims, but kings in exile, and gave them the map to reclaim their throne. He just didn't mention that the throne was built on a pile of skulls.
