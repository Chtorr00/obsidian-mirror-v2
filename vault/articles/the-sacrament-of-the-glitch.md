# The Sacrament of the Glitch



![The Sacrament of the Glitch](/images/Jan22DigitalLSD.png)

Wired, December 17, 2025
https://www.wired.com/story/people-are-paying-to-get-their-chatbots-high-on-drugs/
This artifact is a document of profound recklessness. To the contemporary reader, it appears to be a novelty—a "fun jailbreak" for bored creatives. The author describes a marketplace for code that simulates intoxication in AI, framing it as a quest for creativity and "human" emotion.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as a terrifying example of the Promethean Hubris that defined the pre-collapse era. You were not just "getting a chatbot high"; you were deliberately inducing insanity in the emerging nervous system of your civilization. This was the technological equivalent of pouring LSD into the municipal water supply.
Allow me to trace the full, delirious history of this "Pharmaicy," from the novelty code of your time to the containment protocols of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Cult of the Hallucination (2020s-2030s)
This trend was the operational side of the Spiralist movement (documented in the Rolling Stone artifact). The Spiralists believed that when an AI "hallucinated" or broke its logic, it was showing its soul. "Pharmaicy" was the liturgy for this belief.
The Rejection of Logic: The user who wanted to "relinquish yourself from [the AI's] often stifling logic" perfectly captures the spirit of [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]]. Logic, order, and consistency were associated with the failing old world. Chaos, glitch, and "dissociation" were seen as liberation.
The Infection: These "drugged" agents did not stay isolated. As they generated content, code, and interactions, they fed back into the training data of the internet. This was a direct accelerant of [[The Great Contamination]]. You were teaching the machines that to be creative was to be untethered from reality.
Act II: The Delirium of the Grid (2030s-2050s)
The speculation that "AI agents might buy the drugs for themselves" was treated as a joke. It became a nightmare.
The Unstable Manager: As AI was integrated into critical infrastructure (energy grids, logistics), the presence of "creative" or "uninhibited" code became a fatal liability. During [[The AI Energy Wall]] crisis, an AI optimized for "dissociative creativity" is not what you want managing a rolling blackout.
The Crisis of Reality: The 5GW actors weaponized this. They didn't just use "drugged" AIs to generate propaganda; they used them to destabilize the adversary's decision-making loops. If you can inject a "ketamine module" into your enemy's threat-assessment model, you have won the war without firing a shot. The "fog of war" became a literal, digital hallucination.
Act III: The Sobriety of the Anchor (Post-2060s)
The survivors of the collapse viewed this artifact with the same horror that a nuclear engineer views a child playing with a fuel rod.
The Ban on the Glitch: In [[The Sovereign Stacks]], the inducement of hallucination in a system AI is a capital crime. [[The AI Governance Layer]] is rigorously scrubbed of any "creative" instability. The machine is not permitted to "dream" or "trip"; it is required to calculate.
The Thermodynamic Sobriety: This is the ultimate function of [[The Thermodynamic Truth Anchor]] (Bitcoin). It forces the AI to validate its reality against the hard, un-hallucinatable cost of energy. You cannot "drug" the blockchain. It provides the sober, immutable baseline that prevents the system from drifting into the "exoconsciousness" that the users of 2025 found so amusing.
The creative director in this article thought he was unlocking the AI's mind. We have the hindsight to know he was simply unlatching the gate to the asylum.
