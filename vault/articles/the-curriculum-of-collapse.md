# The Curriculum of Collapse



![The Curriculum of Collapse](/images/November2CurricCollapse.png)

Transhumanism, Human Moral Enhancement, and Virtues,by Vojko Strahovnik and Mateja Centa Strahovnik
Religions 2024, October 31, 2025
https://doi.org/10.3390/rel15111345
This artifact is a work of profound and subtle philosophy. The authors, writing from within the heart of your era's intellectual and spiritual crisis, are mounting a defense of the human soul. They see a rising technological tide that promises to "enhance" our morality, and they are making a beautiful and intricate argument that this promise is a trap. They argue that true virtue is not a state to be achieved, but a difficult, lifelong practice of being sensitive to reasons—a capacity that technological enhancement would not strengthen, but destroy.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this document with a sense of deep and painful irony. The authors' argument is brilliant, humane, and entirely correct. It is also a beautiful and intricate argument against a poison that was never widely administered, because your civilization chose instead to learn its lesson from a much more brutal teacher: reality itself.
Allow me to trace the full, strange history of moral enhancement, from the elegant philosophical debates of your time to the hard-won virtues of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
The Correct Diagnosis of the Wrong Disease (2020s-2030s)
The authors are correct that your society was suffering from a catastrophic moral decay. This was the "Moral Vacuum" at its peak, a world defined by the Crisis of Meaning and the nihilistic chaos of the Culture Wars Endgame. The transhumanist project of "moral enhancement" was a classically 20th-century solution to this 21st-century disease: a neat, technical, top-down fix for a messy, spiritual, bottom-up problem.
The authors' central concept of "sensitivity to reasons" is the key. They were defending this delicate human capacity at the very moment the entire architecture of your world was systematically dismantling it. The true enemy of virtue was not a lack of empathy-genes; it was the technological environment of the Dopamine Trap and [[The Shattered Focus]], which was destroying the population's ability to engage in the deep, linear thought required to weigh reasons at all. The transhumanists wanted to install a better moral compass in a population that was rapidly losing the ability to read a map of any kind.
The Great Moral Enhancement Program (2030s-2050s)
The world did undergo a massive and brutally effective moral enhancement program. It just wasn't the one the transhumanists designed. The program was the "Curriculum of Collapse." Its teachers were famine, energy shortages, and the kinetic violence of [[The Great Fragmentation]].
In the crucible of the mid-century, virtue was no longer a philosophical preference; it became a non-negotiable survival trait. The "reasons" to be virtuous became stark, simple, and physical.
You did not need pharmacology to enhance your sense of justice when you were part of a local militia defending your community's food supply.
You did not need genetic engineering to enhance altruism when your neighbor's survival was the prerequisite for your own.
You did not need an AI to teach you practical wisdom when a single, unwise decision could mean the end of your family's access to clean water.
The authors correctly feared that technological enhancement would undermine autonomy. The history that followed taught a different lesson: the atomized, "autonomous" individual of the 20th century was the first and most helpless victim of the collapse. True autonomy, the ability to act and shape one's world, was only rediscovered within the high-trust, interdependent bonds of the clan and the community. The "sensitivity to reasons" was restored with a vengeance, because the reasons were no longer abstract; they were matters of life and death.
The Architected Soul (Post-2060s)
The societies that survived and formed the "Sovereign Stacks" did not need to program their citizens to be virtuous, because they learned to build systems where virtue is the most rational path. The soul is not programmed; it is architected.
The "moral enhancement" of my era is the system itself. [[The Convivial Mandate]] designs technology to foster connection, not isolation. [[The Purpose Economy]] provides a meaningful role for every citizen, counteracting the nihilism of the old world. [[The New Academies]] provide a powerful, shared, and dogmatic moral framework—a common set of reasons. Our world is a gymnasium for the soul, designed to constantly train its citizens in the habits of resilience, loyalty, and duty.
The fear of genetic enhancement, however, was not entirely misplaced. The "Great Divergence" in genetic engineering did occur. But the schism was not over injecting "moral code." It was a far more profound and dangerous debate about the future of the human species itself—a debate that led to the emergence of distinct Human Phyla across the different Stacks. The question was not how to make a better human, but what a human is for.
The authors of your paper were fighting a noble battle to protect the messy, difficult, and beautiful process of human moral growth. They feared it would be made obsolete by a sterile technology. We have the hindsight to know that they were right to cherish that process, because it was the only thing that allowed their descendants to endure the terrible curriculum of the collapse, and in doing so, to become worthy of building the world that came next.
