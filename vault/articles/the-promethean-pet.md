# The Promethean Pet

This artifact is a story of incredible determination and technological triumph. The author, a journalist for a major Australian newspaper, is chronicling the heartwarming tale of a tech entrepreneur who used AI to cure his dying dog. She frames it as a victory for "citizen science" and a hopeful sign for the future of human medicine.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this document not as a pet rescue story, but as the moment the seal broke on Democratized Bioweaponry. The author believes she is writing about a cure. We see her documenting the exact methodology that allowed an amateur, armed with a chatbot and a credit card, to design a novel, custom-built biological agent in his living room.
Allow me to trace the full, terrifying history of this "magical" breakthrough, from the veterinary clinics of your time to the quarantine zones of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Democratization of the Code (2020s-2030s)

The most critical detail in this story is the quote: "It’s democratising the whole process." This was the core delusion of [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]].
The Slipping of the Guardrails: The fact that a "punter in the street" could use ChatGPT and AlphaFold to design an mRNA sequence was celebrated. But the same AI that can synthesize a protein to kill a tumor can synthesize a protein to kill a host. The "guardrails" on these LLMs were linguistic, not biological. They could refuse to tell you how to make mustard gas, but they were perfectly willing to help you optimize a novel, folded protein because the AI didn't understand the physical consequence of the code it was writing.
The Naivety of the Establishment: The university professors who were "gobsmacked" and "motivated" by the entrepreneur's enthusiasm were the unwitting enablers of [[The Great Contamination]] in the biological sphere. They provided the physical lab space to instantiate the AI's digital blueprint. They assumed that because his intent was good (saving a dog), the democratization of the tool was safe.
### Act II: The Era of the Garage Plague (2030s-2050s)

The blueprint laid out in this article—sequence DNA, use AI to find targets, use AI to design mRNA, find a lab to print it—did not remain confined to veterinary oncology.
The Bio-Koryos: The same disaffected young men of [[The Virtual Koryos]] (who had previously used AI to generate "slop" and propaganda) realized that biology was just another code base. The "citizen science" celebrated here mutated into "citizen bioterrorism."
The Bespoke Pathogen: During [[The Great Fragmentation]], as the nation-state lost its monopoly on violence, it also lost its monopoly on virology. Small cells of extremists, or even unstable individuals suffering from AI-Induced Psychosis, used the exact pipeline Mr. Conyngham pioneered to design highly targeted, novel pathogens. The "red tape" of ethics boards was simply bypassed by sending the digital files to offshore, unregulated "dark labs" that would print any sequence for crypto.
### Act III: The Purity of the Fortress (Post-2060s)

The societies that survived the "Garage Plagues" did so by treating biological code with the same paranoia they applied to nuclear material.
The Ban on Open Biology: In [[The Sovereign Stacks]], the "democratization" of genomics is viewed as a suicidal folly. The Bioconservative Stacks ban the synthesis of novel DNA/RNA entirely. [[The Biotech Stacks]] (the heirs of this technology) practice it, but it is the most tightly guarded state secret, managed only by the highest echelons of [[The New Priesthood]].
The Hardware Lock: You cannot ask an AI in my time to design a protein sequence. [[The AI Governance Layer]] is physically air-gapped from any biological synthesis equipment. If you attempt to run an AlphaFold-style query on a non-state server, [[The Architecture of Pervasive Security]] will flag you as a Class 1 biological threat.
The author of this article thought she was writing a story about a man who loved his dog so much he learned biology. We have the hindsight to know she was writing the instruction manual for the people who hated the world so much they learned how to end it.
