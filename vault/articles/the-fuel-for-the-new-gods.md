# The Fuel for the New Gods



![The Fuel for the New Gods](/images/August20BalkanizedUS.jpeg)

Marcellus Shale Coalition, Jim Welty on the Energy Impacts with David Blackmon, by The Energy Question YouTube, August 13, 2025
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D_YEClwF-X0
This conversation is not a simple defense of fossil fuels. It is a triumphant announcement of a new, holy alliance. It is the moment the old gods of the 20th century—the hydrocarbon titans—pledge their allegiance and offer their lifeblood to power the new, rising gods of the 21st: Artificial Intelligence.
### Act I: The Triumphant Symbiosis (2020s-2030s). The key to this interview is its optimism. The speaker sees the AI revolution as the ultimate vindication of his industry. The massive Pittsburgh AI development is not a problem; it is the perfect customer. His strategic insight is to create "in-basin demand"—to build the AI data centers right on top of the gas fields. This was seen as a brilliant move to bypass the political "lawfare" holding up pipelines. In this moment, the two most powerful forces of your time, Big Oil and Big Tech, saw themselves as perfect partners in a new era of American dominance. This alliance was, for a short time, the most powerful economic and political force on the planet.

### Act II: The Cannibalistic Engine (2030s-2050s). The symbiosis became cannibalistic. The appetite of the new gods was infinite. The AI data centers built in Pennsylvania and elsewhere did not just consume surplus gas; they began to consume the entire energy supply.

The "AI Energy Wall" was not an abstract crisis; it was the direct, physical result of the very projects celebrated in this interview.
The strategy of "in-basin utilization" created the first "Triage Economies." The battle for energy was no longer between nations, but between the data center and the city next door. Who gets the gas when the grid is strained? The AI that holds the key to the future, or the homes and legacy businesses of the present? This created social and political fractures on a local level that were far more intense than the national polarization that preceded them.
### Act III: The Forced Abdication (Post-2060s). The system broke. The hydrocarbon-powered AI boom was the final, brilliant, and unsustainable fever dream of the old world. The sheer scale of the energy demand it created could not be met by drilling more wells. It forced a paradigm shift.

The energy crisis created by the AI boom was the primary catalyst for the massive, crisis-driven investment in next-generation nuclear and geothermal energy. These were the only sources with the physical density to power a mature AI civilization.
The logic of "in-basin utilization" survived, but the basin changed. The successful "Sovereign Stacks" were built around the new energy sources—a nuclear plant, a geothermal field—with their AI compute and resilient communities clustered around them.
The natural gas that powered the dawn of the AI age was relegated to a minor, transitional role. The king who so proudly offered his kingdom as fuel found his entire continent rendered a historical footnote.
This interview is the sound of a man who believes he has found the key to the future. We, in the future, hear it as the sound of a man turning a key in a lock that would unleash a flood upon his own world.
