# The Medicalization of Obsolescence



![The Medicalization of Obsolescence](/images/December18Neurodeficits.png)

40% of Stanford undergrads receive disability accommodations—but it’s become a college-wide phenomenon as Gen Z try to succeed in the current climate, By Preston Fore
Forbes, December 12, 2025
https://fortune.com/2025/12/12/rise-in-elite-students-seeking-accomodation-gen-z-phenomenon-find-success-in-competitive-job-market/
This artifact is a dispatch from the waiting room of the Great Displacement, written by a journalist who sees a trend in higher education but misses the tectonic shift beneath it. He frames the 40% disability rate at Stanford as a question of "gaming the system" or "reduced stigma."
From my perspective in 2100, we read this statistic not as a medical anomaly or a moral failing, but as a biological scream. This was the moment the human cognitive baseline, assaulted by the Dopamine Trap and pressured by the encroaching shadow of AI, finally admitted it could no longer keep up. The students were not trying to get a "leg up"; they were signaling that the water had risen above their heads.
Allow me to trace the full, tragic history of this generation, who were trained for a world that was dissolving beneath their feet.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: [[The Shattered Focus]] Meets the Infinite Bar (2020s-2030s)
The author touches on the "pandemic," "social media," and "AI." These were the ingredients of the Shattered Focus.
The 20th-century university curriculum was designed for a linear, literate mind capable of deep, sustained attention. By 2025, that mind had been systematically dismantled by the attention economy. The "accommodations" (extra time, quiet rooms) were not cheats; they were the prosthetic crutches required for a cognitively injured generation to perform 20th-century tasks.
Simultaneously, the "bar for landing a job" was becoming "impossibly high." This was the pressure wave of the Great Displacement. Students intuitively understood that they were competing not just with each other, but with the nascent AI that would soon render "entry-level knowledge work" obsolete. They were seeking a medical exemption from the future.
Act II: The Great De-Accreditation (2030s-2050s)
The article correctly predicts the clash: "workplace assessments typically don’t come with accommodations." This disconnect destroyed the university.
As [[The Great Fragmentation]] accelerated, the value of the elite degree collapsed. Employers in the brutal Triage Economy realized that a credential from a university where 40% of the students operated under modified conditions was no longer a reliable signal of competence.
This led to the Great De-Accreditation. The old universities, unable to reconcile their therapeutic mandate with the demands of a collapsing economy, faded into irrelevance. They became finishing schools for the "lost generation," while the real training for the new world moved into [[The Guilds]], the militias, and the private certification bodies of the rising tribes.
The "pink slip" mentioned in the article became a mass casualty event. A generation raised on accommodations was thrust into an economy defined by scarcity, energy rationing (AI Energy Wall), and zero-sum competition. The shock was devastating.
Act III: The New Standards of the Mind (Post-2060s)
[[The Sovereign Stacks]] that rebuilt civilization solved this problem by rejecting the premise entirely. They do not offer accommodations; they offer two divergent paths.
The Path of the Upgrade (The Cyborgists): For the accelerationist Stacks, the solution to the "impossibly high bar" was to raise the human. If the baseline brain cannot concentrate, you fix the hardware. Nootropics, neural interfaces, and genetic selection are used to create a mind that can compete with AI. Disability is not accommodated; it is engineered out.
The Path of the Sanctuary (The Bioconservatives): For the humanist Stacks, the solution was to lower the noise. [[The New Academies]] in these regions ban the "Dopamine Trap" technologies entirely during development. They rebuild the capacity for deep work not by giving extra time, but by creating a high-trust, low-stimulus environment where the human mind can function as it evolved to.
In my time, the idea that 40% of the elite youth would claim disability is seen as the final proof that the late-20th-century environment had become toxic to human life. We did not fix the students; we fixed the world that was breaking them.
The author of this article saw students asking for extra time on tests. We have the hindsight to know they were begging for extra time before the end of their world.
