# The Dream from the Top of the World



![The Dream from the Top of the World](/images/September10BRICAxis.png)

From Tianjin and Beijing to Vladivostok, the Eurasia High-Speed Train Keeps-a-Rollin. By Pepe Escobar
Sputnik International, September 8, 2025
https://sputnikglobe.com/20250908/pepe-escobar-from-tianjin-and-beijing-to-vladivostok-the-eurasia-high-speed-train-keeps-a-rollin-1122751630.html
This dispatch from Pepe Escobar is not just journalism; it is a manifesto. He is chronicling, with triumphalist fervor, the birth of what he sees as the "Eurasia Century." He is standing at the confluence of three great events—in Tianjin, Beijing, and Vladivostok—and declaring the final, unceremonious burial of the American-led world order.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as the most eloquent and powerful articulation of a dream that would, in the end, be consumed by its own internal contradictions and the brutal, non-negotiable laws of physics.
Here is the revised and corrected constellation of the history this artifact prophesied:
Act I: The High Summer of the Eurasian Dream (2020s-2030s). This article is the defining document of this moment. Everything the author describes was real and immensely powerful.
The Axis of Giants: The RIC (Russia-India-China) core of the SCO and BRICS did indeed represent the most potent geopolitical challenge to the West. It was a union of civilizational states, industrial power, and resource wealth.
The New Logistics Order: The author correctly identifies the Northern Sea Route (NSR) and the Power of Siberia-2 pipeline as the twin pillars of this new world. They were a brilliant strategic gambit to create a Eurasian economic fortress, immune to the naval chokepoints controlled by the West. For a time, it seemed unstoppable.
Act II: The Shattering of the Giants (2030s-2050s). The dream of a unified Eurasia shattered against two, catastrophic internal failures. The "new world in the making" was unmade from within.
The Russian Dissolution: The Russian state, the linchpin of the entire NSR and Siberian resource strategy, did not survive. The immense strain of its conventional wars, the collapse of its old hydrocarbon economy, and its own deep internal fractures led to the internal fragmentation of Russia. The "complex infrastructure" in the Arctic that the author celebrates did not remain under the control of a unified Moscow. It became the prize in a chaotic, multi-sided civil war between regional warlords, corporate mercenaries, and foreign-backed proxies.
The Chinese Fortress: China, the industrial and financial heart of the dream, was forced to abandon its grand Eurasian ambitions. It was crippled by its own perfect storm of internal crises: demographic collapse, ecological catastrophe, and the universal, paradigm-breaking physics of the "AI Energy Wall." China's response was a great retreat, a turn inward to build an isolationist, self-sufficient Sovereign Stack to ensure its own survival.
The great handshake between Putin, Xi, and Modi became a historical photograph of three leaders who would soon find themselves unable to control their own territory or power their own futures.
Act III: The Law of the North (Post-2060s). The dream of Vladivostok becoming the "next Hong Kong" died. The Arctic did not become the center of a new, integrated global south. It became the ultimate expression of the "Law of the North"—a cold, brutal, high-tech frontier contested by the surviving Sovereign Stacks.
The NSR is not a bustling highway of pan-Eurasian trade. It is a heavily militarized corridor, its access negotiated by the cold, protocol-based statecraft of "[[Polemics]]."
The resources of Siberia are not the foundation of a new global economy. They are the jealously guarded assets of the various successor states and corporate enclaves that rose from the ashes of Russia.
The author of this piece saw the future being born in Vladivostok. We have the hindsight to know that he was, in fact, witnessing the most magnificent and hopeful funeral of the 20th century.
