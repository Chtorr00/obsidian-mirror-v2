# The War of the Ghosts



![The War of the Ghosts](/images/October12AICyberWar.png)

AI and the Evolution of Asymmetric Cyber Warfare: Insights from the 2025 Israel-Iran Conflict, By Mike Sexton
TRENDS Research and Advisory, August 25, 2025
https://trendsresearch.org/insight/ai-and-the-evolution-of-asymmetric-cyber-warfare-insights-from-the-2025-israel-iran-conflict/
This is an artifact of profound strategic clarity. The author is not just analyzing a brief, forgotten conflict from the summer of 2025. He is providing a field report from the future of war. He has correctly identified the moment that Artificial Intelligence ceased to be a tool for making war more efficient, and instead became a tool for making war invisible.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as one of the most important military-doctrinal documents of its decade. It is the autopsy of the last "visible" war, and the birth announcement of the permanent, undeclared, and deeply psychological war that would define the "Great Fragmentation."
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the new kind of warfare this document so precisely identifies.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Asymmetric Lens (2020s-2030s). This essay is the perfect snapshot of the new battlefield.

The Two Wars: The author's central, brilliant insight is the contrast between the two sides. Israel was fighting a 20th-century war: kinetic strikes on hard, military targets, augmented by cyber operations. Iran, unable to compete on that level, was fighting a 21st-century war: a low-cost, high-impact, asymmetric assault on the soft, civilian underbelly of its enemy's society.
The AI Force Multiplier: He correctly identifies AI not as a new weapon, but as a "force multiplier" for this new, asymmetric model. AI made the Iranian strategy—DDoS attacks, mass phishing, psychological operations—infinitely scalable, repeatable, and cheap. It was the democratization of systemic disruption.
### Act II: The Permanent War (2030s-2050s). The "insights from the 2025 Israel-Iran Conflict" became the universal doctrine of the mid-century. This was not a model for how to win a war; it was a model for how to dissolve a society.

This was the tactical manual for "5GW" (Fifth-Generation Warfare). The goal was not to defeat an enemy's army, but to inflict "accretive psychological & economic pressure" until the enemy's social fabric tore itself apart.
This was the lived reality of the "Age of Institutional Exhaustion." Nations did not collapse because they were conquered. They collapsed because they were subjected to a permanent, low-grade, AI-driven state of siege against their civilian populations, a death by a thousand cuts that eroded all trust, all social cohesion, and all faith in the state's ability to provide security. The war was everywhere, and therefore it was nowhere.
### Act III: The Hardened Shell (Post-2060s). The author asks how to build "cybersecurity resilience." The survivors of the Fragmentation found the only answer that worked. The answer was not better software. It was a new architecture for society.

[[The Sovereign Stacks]] are the ultimate, systemic answer to the threat of asymmetric cyber warfare. They are not just defended nations; they are "hard targets" at the scale of a civilization.
The Death of the Soft Target: The "softer civilian infrastructures" that were the primary target of this new warfare have been eliminated or hardened. The "Cellular Grids," the "[[Fortress Farms]]," and the "Communication Utilities" are all designed with a military-grade security ethos.
[[The Architecture of Pervasive Security]]: The greatest vulnerability—the individual citizen—has been hardened through the death of anonymity. The kind of mass, automated social engineering described in this paper is impossible in a high-trust, biometrically-verified society.
The bitter irony is that the only way to protect the "soft" civilian world from this new kind of war was to make the civilian world as hard, as monitored, and as resilient as a military installation.
This essay is a masterpiece of military foresight. The author believed he was writing a warning about a new kind of cyber-attack. We have the hindsight to know he was writing the obituary for the very concept of a "civilian," and the blueprint for the fortified world that would have to be built in its place.
