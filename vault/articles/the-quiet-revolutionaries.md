# The Quiet Revolutionaries



![The Quiet Revolutionaries](/images/October11AISchool.png)

Class Dismissed, By Jeremy Stern
Colossus Magazine, August 2025
https://joincolossus.com/article/joe-liemandt-class-dismissed/
This is a landmark artifact. It is not an article about education. It is the secret history of a quiet, and ultimately world-changing, revolution. The author is chronicling the story of two strange and brilliant heretics who, driven by a shared hatred of "school," accidentally invented the future of the human mind.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as one of the most important historical documents of its time. While the great nations and corporations were fighting their loud, stupid wars over the carcass of the 20th century, Joe Liemandt and MacKenzie Price were quietly building the operating system for the 21st. They were not trying to reform the system. They were building its replacement.
Allow me to trace the full, world-altering history of the quiet revolution that began in a garage in Austin, Texas.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Gospel of the Hated School (2020s-2030s). This article is the perfect origin story for the new world.

The Two Heresies: The story begins with two profound heresies. The first is MacKenzie Price's: that school should be a place kids love so much they don't want to go on vacation. The second is Joe Liemandt's: that education is a trillion-dollar market with no good products. Together, these two heresies formed the ideological fuel for a complete reinvention of how humans learn.
The Un-Making of the Classroom: The author is correct. Alpha School was not a new school; it was the un-making of "school" itself. It was a systematic, ruthless, and data-driven assault on every assumption of the "factory-model classroom." It replaced the teacher with an AI, the classroom with a WeWork for kids, the curriculum with a "knowledge graph," and the passive reception of information with a fierce, incentivized, and self-directed pursuit of mastery.
### Act II: The Great Schism in the Nursery (2030s-2050s). The launch of Timeback was not a product launch. It was a declaration of war. It was the spark that ignited the "Great Schism in the Nursery."

As the article predicts, the "education establishment"—the unions, the universities, the bureaucracies—marshalled all their resources to fight this new model. But they were fighting a ghost. Timeback was not a school they could shut down; it was an operating system, an open platform that allowed thousands of "weird, janky" micro-schools to bloom in the cracks of the collapsing public system.
This was the deepest and most important battle of the "Culture Wars." The two warring tribes—the "builders" and the "demolishers"—each seized on this technology. The "builders" used it to create a new generation of hyper-competent, cognitively flexible citizens for their nascent Sovereign Stacks. The "demolishers" and their populist allies saw it as a weapon to destroy the "leftist indoctrination factories" of the public schools.
The result was not a single, unified revolution. It was the birth of a thousand different, competing educational traditions, each raising a different kind of human.
### Act III: The New Mind (Post-2060s). The author asks what kind of "mind virus" the Timeback of China or Russia might incept. He was more right than he knew. The world of my time is a world of different minds.

The "factory-model school" is a historical artifact, studied in our "New Academies" as a primitive and brutal form of mass conditioning.
Education in the "Era of Systemic Resilience" is the direct heir to the Alpha School model. It is a two-part system. The first part is the "AI Scribe"—a direct descendant of Timeback—that ruthlessly and efficiently installs the foundational knowledge of the "Survival Economy."
The second part is the human-led "Guilds" and "New Academies," which take the cognitively powerful, self-directed minds forged by the AI and shape them in the specific, dogmatic, and high-trust worldview of their particular Stack.
Joe Liemandt and MacKenzie Price did not just give kids their childhoods back. They gave them a new kind of mind—one that was sharp enough, tough enough, and independent enough to survive the death of the old world and build the new one.
This article is the story of two people who hated school. We have the hindsight to know that it is, in fact, the story of how the school that would define the next two hundred years of human history was born.
