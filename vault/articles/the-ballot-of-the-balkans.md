This artifact is a dispatch from the funeral of a political ideal. The author, a conservative peer and historian, is mourning the death of "British citizenship" as a unifying concept. He sees a by-election where the Green Party won not by discussing the environment, but by appealing to sectarian, foreign grievances in a foreign language. From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a warning, but as a statement of fact. Daniel Hannan was witnessing the moment when the Westphalian Nation-State in Britain ceased to function as a coherent entity and dissolved into a collection of warring Neotribes sharing the same landmass. He believed he was seeing a "toxic" aberration. We know he was watching the standard operating procedure of [[The Great Fragmentation]].
Allow me to trace the full, fractured history of this "new world," from the polling stations of 2026 to the partitioned island of my time.

Hannan correctly identifies the dynamic: the Green Party was no longer an environmental movement; it had become a Parasovereign vessel.

It signaled that there was no longer a "British" public square, but a series of sealed, linguistic silos. The "common identity" Hannan longed for had been dissolved by the Crisis of Meaning and the demographic shifts of the 20th century.
