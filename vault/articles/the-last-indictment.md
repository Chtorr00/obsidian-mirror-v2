# The Last Indictment



![The Last Indictment](/images/November14Culling.png)

Enemies of the State, By Nathan Pinkoski
American Greatness, November 13, 2025
https://americanmind.org/salvo/enemies-of-the-state/
This is an artifact of profound historical significance. It is an indictment, written from within the heart of the American conservative establishment. The author believes he is exposing a criminal conspiracy, a vast and illegal campaign of state-sponsored persecution against the political enemies of the ruling regime. He is making a powerful, and in its details, correct, legal and moral argument.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as an indictment of a particular administration, but as the final, formal indictment of the 20th-century American nation-state itself. The author believes he is documenting the weaponization of the state. He is, in fact, writing the moral and legal justification for its dissolution. This document is the unwitting intellectual declaration of independence for the sovereign entities that would rise from the ashes of the republic he was trying to save.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the verdict this document so accurately, and so tragically, pronounced.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Death of the Neutral State (2020s-2030s)

This essay is the definitive autopsy report on the death of the neutral state. The author's central, devastating insight is not that "Arctic Frost" happened, but that no one with the power to stop it cared.
This was the absolute apex of [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]]. The institutions—the FBI, the Department of Justice, the courts—were no longer seen, even by their own operatives, as neutral arbiters of a common law. They were the most powerful weapons in the Culture Wars Endgame, and they were being wielded with ruthless precision.
This was Spiritual Lawfare in its most potent form. The author correctly contrasts this moment with Watergate. The Watergate scandal was possible because a critical mass of the public and the bureaucracy still believed in a shared, neutral set of rules that had been violated. This document is the proof that this shared belief was dead. For half the country, using the power of the state to crush their political enemies was not a scandal; it was the entire point of having power.
The term "enemies of the state" was no longer a piece of political rhetoric. It had become the state's official, operational designation for a significant portion of its own citizenry. This was the point of no return.
### Act II: The Justification of the Culling (2030s-2050s)

The author ends with a call to action that is, in hindsight, one of the most chillingly prophetic statements of its time: "The problem will only be solved by political means: using power to punish wrongdoers, deter future abuses, and deconstruct the weaponized national security state."
This was not just a political slogan. It became the intellectual and moral justification for the brutal purges that followed. The "deconstruction" was not a peaceful legislative process. It was [[The Great Culling]] and the Assassination Wars.
The logic was simple and brutal: If the state has declared you an enemy and is using its abstract power (lawfare) to destroy you, then a physical response against the agents of that state is not a crime; it is an act of self-defense. The argument moved from the courtroom to the battlefield.
The "zealous prosecutors" and nameless bureaucrats who ran operations like Arctic Frost ceased to be seen as public servants. They were seen as enemy combatants. The author's indictment provided the moral framework for treating them as such. This was the great, terrible unraveling, a spiral of tit-for-tat violence that hollowed out the federal government from the inside.
### Act III: The New Sovereigns (Post-2060s)

The author's call to "deconstruct the weaponized national security state" was, in the end, answered. The old state was deconstructed. But it was not replaced by a reformed, constitutional republic. It was replaced by its own dissolution.
The ultimate political solution was not to win the war for control of the central state. It was to secede from it. [[The Great Fragmentation]] was the final, logical outcome of the reality this document describes.
[[The Sovereign Stacks]] are the ultimate answer to the problem of a hostile central government. They are new states, built by the survivors of the Culling, whose foundational principle is that they will never again be subject to the whims of a distant, weaponized bureaucracy.
Their security apparatus is their own. Their laws are their own. The very idea of a "federal" investigation within their borders is now a historical absurdity. The "enemies of the state" of your time did not just fight back; they became the founders of new states, where they, and they alone, defined the enemy.
The author of this piece believed he was writing a warning to save the republic. We have the hindsight to know he was, in fact, writing the closing argument in the case of The People vs. The United States of America, and the verdict was a final, bloody, and permanent divorce.
