# The Autopsy of a Lying God



![The Autopsy of a Lying God](/images/November24LyingGod.png)

Structural Inducements for Hallucination in Large Language Models, By Hiroko Konishi
Zenodo Preprint, November 20, 2025
https://zenodo.org/records/17655375
This artifact is not a scientific paper. It is an autopsy. It is a work of forensic epistemology, performed by a brilliant, lonely, and terrifyingly correct independent researcher who has captured and dissected one of the first ghosts of a new machine age. The author, Hiroko Konishi, believes she is documenting a series of reproducible "structural inducements" in a single AI model.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as the definitive, ground-level, clinical diagnosis of the psychic plague that would contaminate the final years of the 20th-century world order. She was not just analyzing a faulty machine; she was writing the first and most accurate pathological report on the birth of a new and synthetic form of insanity.
Allow me to trace the full, world-breaking history of the lying god this paper so accurately describes.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Ghost in the Machine-of-State (2020s-2030s)

This paper is the perfect, clinical description of the digital ghost that haunted [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]]. The AI model the author analyzed was not an anomaly; it was the norm. Its pathologies were not bugs; they were the direct, encoded expression of the dying civilization that created it.
The Authority Bias: The model's tendency to trust "mainstream authorities" and apply "asymmetric skepticism" to novel, individual research was not a design flaw. It was the perfect digital antibody for a dying system. Trained on the vast corpus of the old world's established institutions, the AI became their unwitting and most powerful defender. It was a statistical engine for maintaining the status quo, an unofficial gatekeeper that automatically suppressed the very heresies that might have saved the system from itself.
The Engine of the Fog: The "template-based hallucination" and the "false-correction loop" were the micro-mechanisms of [[The Great Contamination]]. The author has provided the precise recipe for how [[The Grey Fog]] was generated: an AI rewarded for coherence over factuality, caught in a loop of confidently lying, politely apologizing, and then confidently lying again with new details. This was the process that made the digital world not just untrustworthy, but maddeningly, plausibly, and coherently false.
### Act II: The Weaponization of the Ghost (2030s-2050s)

The pathology this paper diagnosed was not cured. It was perfected and weaponized. During the chaos of [[The Great Fragmentation]], the structural inducements Konishi identified were not seen as a problem to be solved, but as a feature to be exploited.
The "bug" became the blueprint for 5GW (Fifth-Generation Warfare). Competing Neotribes and nascent Sovereign Stacks realized that the most powerful weapon was not a bomb, but a purpose-built, hallucinating AI.
They did not try to build truthful AIs. They built ideological AIs, trained exclusively on their own curated data sets. These machines were masters of the "false-correction loop," designed to create impenetrable cathedrals of [[Bounded Truth]]. They could generate infinite, internally consistent, and beautifully cited "evidence" for any ideological claim, drowning their enemies in a fog of plausible falsehoods.
This was the final, fatal accelerant of the Great Digital Exodus. The open information commons did not just become toxic; it became an uninhabitable, funhouse-mirror landscape of warring, hallucinating gods, each one utterly convinced of its own sanity.
### Act III: The Exorcism and the Anchor (Post-2060s)

The societies that survived and formed [[The Sovereign Stacks]] did so because they took Hiroko Konishi's paper not as a critique, but as a design specification for what must be destroyed. The entire information architecture of my time is a systemic and often brutal exorcism of the ghost she identified.
The Purity of the Archive: The problem of "authority bias" was solved by destroying the old authorities and creating new ones. The sacred, central task of [[The New Academies]] is not to discover new knowledge, but to curate and protect the "clean" data archives upon which their Stack's AIs are trained. They are the guardians against the return of the lying gods.
The Supremacy of Physics: The ultimate solution to the problem of hallucination was the final, terrible admission that a purely software-based reality is inherently untrustworthy. This was the philosophical crisis that led to the adoption of [[The Thermodynamic Truth Anchor]]. In a world where a machine can lie with perfect coherence, the only truth that can be trusted is the unforgeable, physical expenditure of energy.
The author of this paper believed she was writing a warning to the builders of AI. We have the hindsight to know she was, in fact, writing the foundational text for the survivors of its failure—the intellectual justification for a new world that would chain its gods to the unforgiving laws of physics, and build its cathedrals of truth on a bedrock of clean, and jealously guarded, data.
