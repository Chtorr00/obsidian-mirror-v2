# The Eulogy for the Old World, and a Flawed Map for the New



![The Eulogy for the Old World, and a Flawed Map for the New](/images/August12crisisPosterity.jpeg)

Balaji Srinivasan:The COLLAPSE of the West – Why AI, Bitcoin & China Will Win, Peter McCormack
YouTube Video, July 31, 2025
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VSVOQl-vFKk
This is a seminal artifact. It is the voice of a brilliant and mesmerizing prophet, standing at the edge of a precipice, correctly identifying that the ground is about to give way. Balaji Srinivasan, in your time, was one of the few thinkers with the intellectual courage to declare the 20th-century world order not just sick, but terminal. In this, history proved him almost entirely correct.
From my perspective in 2100, we see this document as a masterclass in diagnosis and a profound error in prognosis. He saw the collapse coming with perfect clarity. But the world he predicted would rise from the ashes—a clean, binary victory for AI, Bitcoin, and China—was a phantom. The reality was infinitely more complex, more fragmented, and more physically constrained than his digital-first worldview could account for.
Allow me to trace the full arc of the future this video tried to predict, from its startlingly accurate diagnosis of the fall to the messy, unexpected reality of the reconstruction.
Here is the constellation of this prophecy, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Prophet of the Exit (2020s-2030s). Balaji's thesis was the single most potent intellectual framework for understanding the "Age of Institutional Exhaustion." He gave a name and a logic to the sense of decay that everyone felt. His argument—that the Western world was built on a foundation of lies (insolvent, untrustworthy, slow)—was a brutal but compelling explanation for the chaos. His call to "exit" via technology like Bitcoin was not just a financial strategy; it was a moral and political philosophy that resonated deeply with a generation that had lost faith in all institutions. He was the most articulate author of the old world's eulogy.

### Act II: The Collision with Physics and Place (2030s-2050s). Here is where the prophecy shattered against the brutal, non-negotiable laws of the real world. The clean victory he foresaw did not happen.

The China Miscalculation: China did not "win" in the sense of global domination. It faced its own catastrophic internal crises: demographic collapse, ecological disasters near its industrial heartlands, and, most critically, it slammed head-first into the same "AI Energy Wall" as the West. China's response was not to conquer the world, but to turn inward, using its powerful state apparatus to construct one of the most formidable and isolationist "Sovereign Stacks" on Earth. It "won" control of itself, at the cost of its global ambition.
The Network State's Physicality: The pure "Network State"—a placeless, cloud-based nation—collided with the reality of energy and security. You cannot run an AGI without a power plant. You cannot secure a community without physical territory. The successful "exit" communities were not the ones that floated in the cloud; they were the ones that took Balaji's philosophy and applied it to a piece of land, securing their own energy, food, and borders. The virtual nation had to become a physical fortress.
### Act III: The Ghost in the New Machines (Post-2060s). Balaji was right that AI and Bitcoin would be foundational to the future. But they were not the tools of the victors he named. They became the foundational physics of the new, fragmented world.

Bitcoin did not become the currency of the Network State. It became the "Thermodynamic Truth Anchor," the metaphysical bedrock used by all successful Sovereign Stacks to secure their reality against AGI manipulation. It was too important to be left to one tribe; it became a global utility.
AI did not just produce wealth; its arrival in the form of AGI triggered the "AGI Contention of 2068," the existential crisis that forced the creation of the containment systems that now define our reality.
The idea of the Network State was his true legacy. It was the ideological spark that inspired the founders of the real-world, geographically-bound Sovereign Stacks. The blueprint failed, but the philosophy of building new, voluntary, high-trust societies succeeded in a thousand different, localized forms.
Balaji's error was the classic error of the brilliant digital native: he underestimated the brutal, persistent, and ultimately decisive importance of the physical world. He wrote the perfect intellectual software for the 21st century, not realizing the hardware it needed to run on—the planet itself—had its own non-negotiable operating system.
