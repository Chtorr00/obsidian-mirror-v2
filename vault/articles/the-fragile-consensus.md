# The Fragile Consensus



![The Fragile Consensus](/images/August27Lonlinessepidemic.png)

Gene Editing: Developments, Ethical Considerations, and Future Directions, by Manav C Parikh
J Community Hosp Intern Med Perspect, January 6, 2025
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11759082/
This document is a snapshot of a profound, and ultimately doomed, moment of global unity. The author is chronicling the world's scientific and ethical leadership desperately trying to build a single, universal wall around the most powerful and dangerous technology in history. The shockwave of the He Jiankui experiment in 2018 has forced them into a fragile, temporary consensus: "We are not ready."
From my perspective in 2100, we see this as the last global summit on the future of the human species. It was a noble, necessary, and completely futile attempt to hold a single conversation in a world that was about to shatter into a thousand pieces.
Here is the revised constellation of the history this artifact documents:
### Act I: The Shadow of the Twins (2020s-2030s). The entire conversation of your era, as this paper perfectly shows, is happening in the shadow of He Jiankui's gene-edited twins.

The moratoriums and international summits are not acts of proactive governance; they are acts of damage control. They are an attempt by the 20th century's international institutions to prove they still have authority over a 21st-century technology.
This "fragile consensus" was the last gasp of the "Age of Institutional Exhaustion." It represented a global scientific elite trying to impose a universal ethical framework at the exact moment the political and cultural foundations for such a framework were dissolving. The consensus was a beautiful, intricate sandcastle, built at low tide.
### Act II: The Great Divergence (2030s-2050s). The tide came in. The "Great Fragmentation" made a mockery of any global moratorium. A consensus is meaningless when there is no "demos" to agree to it.

As the world fractured into competing "Sovereign Stacks," the single ethical debate became a multitude of strategic calculations.
The memory of He Jiankui was interpreted in radically different ways. For the emerging European Stack, it was a permanent cautionary tale, hardening their resolve to ban germline enhancement forever. For more accelerationist Stacks, his crime was not what he did, but that he did it prematurely, clumsily, and without the sanction of a state.
The cautious, consensus-driven approach described in the paper was abandoned. Gene editing became a critical component of the "Physics of Sovereignty"—a tool for ensuring the long-term biological resilience and competitive advantage of a Stack's population.
### Act III: The New Speciation (Post-2060s). The long-term result was exactly what the cautious ethicists of your time feared, but on a scale they could not imagine. The "Great Divergence" in policy led to a physical divergence in biology.

After generations of separate development, the populations of the different Stacks are no longer a single humanity. We are a collection of human phyla.
The fear of a genetic caste system within a society was dwarfed by the reality of a genetic caste system between societies. The gap between [[The Engineered]] populations of the Stacks and the baseline humans of the "Disconnected" zones is the most profound and challenging reality of my time.
This academic paper is the last page in the old book—the book in which "humanity" was a singular noun. Its authors were trying to write the final, definitive chapter on ethics. They did not know they were, in fact, writing the preface to a library of entirely new books, each telling the story of a different kind of human.
