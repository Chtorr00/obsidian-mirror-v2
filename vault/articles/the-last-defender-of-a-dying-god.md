# The Last Defender of a Dying God



![The Last Defender of a Dying God](/images/November15SciCollapse.png)

Why I Cut Ties with Science’s Top Publisher, By Anna Krylov
The Free Press, November 12, 2025
https://www.thefp.com/p/why-i-cut-ties-with-sciences-top-publisher
This is an artifact of profound intellectual courage. It is a dispatch from the front lines of the war for the soul of science. The author, a respected scientist, is performing an act of public heresy: she is renouncing one of her faith's most sacred institutions, not because it has failed, but because she believes it has been captured by a new, and fundamentally hostile, theology.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a protest against a policy, but as a eulogy for the very idea of a single, objective, and universal truth. Anna Krylov is the last defender of a dying god, and this document is the record of her final, valiant, and ultimately doomed stand.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the war this scientist was fighting, from the institutional battles of your time to the new dogmas of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Battlefield of the Word (2020s-2030s)
The author is correct: the "pendulum" was not swinging back. It was being dismantled. Your era was the absolute peak of [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]], and science, far from being a sanctuary from the Culture Wars, had become its most prestigious battlefield.
This was Spiritual Lawfare in its most sophisticated form. The DEI policies the author decries were not just a matter of human resources; they were a successful campaign to capture the central nervous system of the scientific enterprise—its journals, its peer review process, its system of accreditation. The goal was to replace the old god of "Objective Truth" with a new pantheon of gods: Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion.
The author's act of severing her ties was a counter-attack in this war. It was a heretical declaration that the old god was the only true one. Her question—"Was I asked because of my expertise—or because of my reproductive organs?"—was the central, agonizing cry of an entire generation of scholars who felt their life's work being subordinated to a political catechism.
The conflict was theological. The two sides were no longer operating within a shared framework. For one side, a study's validity was determined by its methodology and evidence. For the other, its validity was determined by its potential for "harm" and its adherence to "citation justice." There was no common ground left.
Act II: The Great De-Accreditation (2030s-2050s)
The author's hope that "scientists...must push" the pendulum back was the great, tragic miscalculation of her time. The struggle did not lead to reform. It led to the temple's collapse.
This public, internal, and irresolvable conflict over the very purpose of science was a primary accelerant of the Great De-Accreditation. The public, watching the high priests of their civilization's most trusted institution argue over whether truth was real, did the only logical thing: they lost faith. All of it.
Trust in "The Science" did not just decline; it evaporated. This was a fatal blow to the legitimacy of the entire 20th-century knowledge system. If even the chemists and physicists were engaged in what looked like a political power struggle, then who could be trusted? No one.
The great scientific institutions did not reform. They were abandoned. During the financial chaos of [[The Involuntary Jubilee]], their endowments were wiped out. During the social chaos of [[The Great Fragmentation]], their federal funding vanished. They did not lose the argument; they simply faded into bankruptcy and irrelevance.
Act III: The New Dogmas (Post-2060s)
The author feared science being sacrificed on the altar of social justice. The reality was stranger and more total. The idea of a single, universal "science" was sacrificed on the altar of the Fragmentation.
The world of my time is not a world without science. It is a world with many, separate, and intensely dogmatic sciences. This is the era of [[Bounded Truth]].
[[The Sovereign Stacks]] did not rebuild the old, universalist scientific enterprise. They built their own. The "science" of a bioconservative European Stack, with its strict ethical prohibitions on genetic enhancement, operates under a different moral and philosophical paradigm than the "science" of an accelerationist Pacific Coast Stack, which sees the human genome as a prototype to be perfected.
Within [[The New Academies]], the scientist is not a disinterested seeker of a universal truth. They are the high priests of their Stack's particular truth, working to advance a specific, shared, and unapologetically dogmatic vision of the world. The old god of a single, objective truth is dead. In its place are a thousand smaller, tribal gods, and each has its own loyal and brilliant scientific clergy.
Anna Krylov was fighting to save science from being corrupted by one ideology. She could not have known that her fight was the last battle before the very concept of a single, unified science was shattered forever, and the world broke apart into a constellation of different, competing, and irreconcilable truths.
