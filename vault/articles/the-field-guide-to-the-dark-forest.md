# The Field Guide to the Dark Forest



![The Field Guide to the Dark Forest](/images/Jan7DarkForestInternet.png)

You're Running Out of Time to Hide, By Art Chad
1000 Singularities Substack, December 15, 2025
https://artchad.substack.com/p/youre-running-out-of-time-to-hide
This artifact is a survival manual disguised as a cultural critique. The author, a creator on the edge of the internet, is documenting the precise moment the digital commons became uninhabitable. He sees the "slopification," the "vibe-coded" cyberattacks, and the retreat into private servers. He calls it the "Dead Internet."
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as one of the most important early descriptions of the Great Digital Exodus. The author is not just describing a problem with content moderation; he is describing the environmental collapse of the open web. This was the moment humanity realized that to survive digitally, it had to stop being public and start being hidden. The "Dark Forest" was not a metaphor; it was the blueprint for the only digital structures that would survive the coming storm.
Allow me to trace the full history of this retreat, from the "Plutonium" servers of your time to the encrypted citadels of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Contamination and the Siege (2020s-2030s)
The author's diagnosis of [[The Great Contamination]] is flawless. The internet didn't die because it was empty; it died because it was too full.
The Acoustic Smog: The "13 billion images" and the "carpet-bombing" of AI content created a signal-to-noise ratio of zero. The open web became a hall of mirrors where no human could trust their senses. This was the cognitive dimension of [[The AI Energy Wall]]—the cost of finding truth became higher than the value of the truth itself.
The Cyber-Siege: The "prompt-driven cyber attacks" described in the article were the first skirmishes of 5GW. The author correctly notes that defense was impossible because the attack surface was infinite. The only defense was to disconnect.
Act II: The Retreat into the Forest (2030s-2050s)
The "Dark Forest" theory became practice. The mass migration into private, gated, and encrypted communities was the digital parallel to [[The Great Physical Sorting]].
[[The Neotribes]] of the Web: Just as people fled the [[Feral Cities]] for high-trust enclaves, they fled the feral internet for high-trust servers. Discord, private Telegram channels, and "local-first" networks became the only places where human culture continued to exist.
The Death of the Public: The "open web" was abandoned to the bots. It became a zombie infrastructure, a place where AI agents scammed each other in an infinite loop of value extraction, while the humans hid in the undergrowth, communicating in whispers and shibboleths to avoid detection.
Act III: The Intranet of the Stack (Post-2060s)
In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the open internet is a memory. [[The Sovereign Stacks]] operate on "Dark Forest" principles as a matter of state security.
The Gated Reality: The Communication Utilities of the Stacks are vast, sophisticated intranets. They are not connected to a global web. They are physically air-gapped and cryptographically sealed. Access is granted only by verified, biological identity.
The End of Anonymity: The author's hope that "context" proves humanity was formalized. In the Stacks, there is no anonymity. Every packet of data is signed by a human reputation. This is the only way to keep the "slop" out.
The Watchful Silence: The global internet that remains is a demilitarized zone, patrolled by the hunter-killer AIs of the rival Stacks. It is a true Dark Forest: silent, deadly, and empty of human life. The only things that move there are weapons.
The author of this piece advised his readers to hide. We have the hindsight to know that hiding was the only way to ensure that there would be anyone left to be found.
