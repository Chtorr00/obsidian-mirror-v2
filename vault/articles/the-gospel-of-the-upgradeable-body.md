# The Gospel of the Upgradeable Body



![The Gospel of the Upgradeable Body](/images/November19HumanPhyla.png)

Beyond Human: When Robot Eyes See Better and Bodies Become Upgradeable, By Thomas Frey
Impact Lab, November 15, 2025
https://www.impactlab.com/2025/11/15/beyond-human-when-robot-eyes-see-better-and-bodies-become-upgradeable/
This is an artifact of profound, and profoundly dangerous, prophetic power. The author is a futurist, and he is performing his function with stunning clarity. He has taken a single, elegant proof-of-concept—a robotic eye that sees better than a human one—and correctly extrapolated its world-altering implications. He sees a coming age where biology is no longer destiny, but a baseline to be surpassed.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a technological forecast, but as a religious text. This is one of the founding gospels of the great and terrible holy war that defines my own time. The author's "inevitable trajectory" was not a single, unified path that all of humanity walked together. It was a declaration of faith that cleaved humanity in two, and then in three, creating the great and irreconcilable schism from which my world was born.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the beautiful and terrifying god this gospel heralds.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Correct Prophecy, The Wrong World (2020s-2030s)

The author's core insight was entirely correct, and it was the most important technological realization of its decade. The principle was proven: engineered systems could, in specific applications, surpass their biological antecedents. The psychological barrier was broken. The "floodgates" he described were indeed opened.
His fatal flaw was in his timeline. He projected a smooth, linear, consumer-driven adoption curve, moving from medicine to performance to the mass market in a stable, globalized world. The history that actually occurred was the opposite. This technology was not born into a stable world; it was born into the chaos of [[The Great Fragmentation]].
The "philosophical crisis" he predicts for 2040 did not arrive on a neat schedule. It happened immediately, and it was not fought in academic journals. It was fought in the boardrooms of the nascent Sovereign Stacks, in the secret military labs, and in the first bloody battles of [[The Great Physical Sorting]]. The question was not "Is augmentation cheating in sports?" The question was "Can our augmented soldiers defeat their un-augmented soldiers?"
### Act II: The Great Schism (2030s-2050s)

The author's most critical error was his assumption of a single path: "human plus machine." The reality was not a merger, but a schism. As the world broke apart, this new technological power did not unite humanity in a shared project of self-improvement. It became the central, defining question of the Great Divergence. Two rival gospels emerged, and they were irreconcilable.
The Gospel of the Machine (Cyborgism): This was the author's path. It was the belief that the biological body is a flawed, temporary vessel, a "baseline" to be systematically replaced with superior, engineered parts. It was the gospel of escaping the meat.
The Gospel of the Code (Biotech): This was the rival path. It argued that the machine was a crude and clumsy tool. The true path to transcendence was not to abandon biology, but to master it; to seize control of the genetic source code and perfect the human animal from within.
This was not a consumer choice between an artificial eye and a gene therapy. It was a state-level, strategic, and ultimately religious choice made by the emerging Sovereign Stacks. Each tribe chose its path to the future. The builder-centric Stacks of the American West might have embraced the Gospel of the Machine. The more bioconservative European Stack might have banned it, while an Asian Stack might have poured its resources into the Gospel of the Code. The "inevitable trajectory" became a fork in the road for the human species.
### Act III: The War of the Gods (Post-2060s)

The world of my time is the direct result of this schism. The author's vision of an "augmented humanity" is real, but it is not one humanity. It is a tense, tripolar world of warring faiths, a cold war over the very definition of our species. This is the Great Moral Schism of my era.
[[The Transcended]]: These are the heirs of this article's author. They are the Cyborgists, who continue to pursue the replacement of the biological with the mechanical. They are a civilization of integrated machine-humans.
[[The Engineered]]: These are the heirs of the biotech path, the masters of the genome. They are not one people, but a branching tree of distinct Human Phyla, each engineered for a different purpose, a different environment.
[[The Purified]]: And then there is the third, unexpected path—the path of rebellion against both gospels. These are the bioconservatives, the heirs to [[The Revaluation of the Real]]. Having survived the collapse, they view the baseline human form as sacred, a perfect product of natural evolution to be cherished and protected. They are a civilization that has chosen, as a matter of religious principle, to remain "merely" human.
The author believed he was writing a roadmap for the future. We have the hindsight to know he was writing the first chapter of a new book of Genesis, a book that would not tell the story of one creation, but of many. And these new creations, in their power and their difference, would spend the next century staring across the borders of their separate worlds, wondering if the being on the other side was still their brother, or something else entirely.
