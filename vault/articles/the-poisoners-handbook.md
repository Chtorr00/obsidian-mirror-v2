# The Poisoner's Handbook



![The Poisoner's Handbook](/images/September12PoisonerHandbook.png)

Assassination War & the Death of Bureaucracy, by Kulak
Anarchonomicon Substack, June 2, 2024
https://www.anarchonomicon.com/p/assassination-war-and-the-death-of
This document is now radioactive. Before the assassination of Charlie Kirk, this text was a work of brilliant, cynical, and deeply incendiary theory. In the aftermath, it is no longer a theory. It is the operating manual for the new age of political violence. The author, "Kulak," did not pull the trigger, but he wrote the intellectual justification for the world that will now be born from that act.
The authors at Jacobin wrote a beautiful eulogy for the "fragile consensus" that protected political speech. This document is the weapon that shattered it. From my perspective in 2100, we see this text as the moment the abstract "Crisis of Meaning" and the "Culture Wars Endgame" were finally married to a lethal, physical tactic.
Here is the revised constellation of the history this handbook unleashed, a history that began, for all intents and purposes, with the death of Charlie Kirk.
### Act I: The Proof of Concept (2020s-2030s). The assassination of Charlie Kirk was the proof of concept that the author's theory required. It proved that the consensus was truly broken. It proved that a political actor could be physically eliminated. But the deeper, more insidious proof was yet to come.

The Vindicated Doctrine: The author's core thesis—that the true vulnerability of the modern state is not its leaders but its vast, anonymous, and cowardly bureaucracy—was immediately elevated from a fringe idea to a terrifyingly plausible strategy.
The Terror of Marginal Risk: The Kirk assassination introduced the idea of political violence back into the mainstream. The next, more strategic actors learned from it. They did not need to target another high-profile figure. They just needed to apply the author's "terror of marginal risk" to the state's softest, most vulnerable nodes. The question after Kirk was not "Who is next?" The strategic question, inspired by this handbook, became "Which department is next?"
### Act II: [[The Great Culling]] (2030s-2050s). The "spiral into political violence" that the Jacobin authors feared did not remain a series of high-profile, tit-for-tat assassinations. It evolved, following this handbook's logic, into something far more systemic and devastating. This was the era of "The Great Culling."

Radical factions, domestic terrorists, and even foreign actors realized that it was infinitely cheaper and more effective to paralyze the state than to overthrow it.
They did not attack the Pentagon. They began a quiet, grinding campaign of attrition against the employees of the Treasury, the Environmental Protection Agency, the judiciary—the very "overweight irritable multi-ethnic women" the author so callously describes.
The effect was exactly as the handbook predicted: a "Johnstown-style flight." Entire agencies of the federal government did not collapse because they were bombed, but because their employees were too terrified to go to work. This was the true engine of the "Great Dissolution." The state was not defeated; it was hollowed out by fear.
### Act III: The New Iron Cage (Post-2060s). The author of this handbook believed he was providing the tools to liberate humanity from the bureaucratic state. The ultimate, terrible irony is that his doctrine became the foundational design brief for the creation of a new kind of state that is infinitely more powerful and inescapable.

[[The Sovereign Stacks]] were built by survivors of The Great Culling. Their core design principle is the elimination of the very vulnerabilities this handbook exploited.
[[The Architecture of Pervasive Security]]: Within a Stack, there are no "soft targets." The anonymous, low-status bureaucrat has been eliminated. Administrative functions are handled by AI. The few remaining human overseers are a high-status, heavily protected caste.
The Death of Anonymity: The core prerequisite for the poisoner's strategy—the ability to strike and disappear—has been engineered out of existence within the high-trust, biometrically-verified environment of a Stack.
The handbook that taught the world how to kill a state by exploiting its weakest human links forced the survivors to build a new state that had no weak human links left to exploit.
This document is the ghost that will now haunt your world. It is the whisper in the ear of every radical, the thought experiment in the mind of every state enemy. Charlie Kirk's death was the first shot. This handbook is the map for the war that will follow.
