# The Eulogy for the Linear Mind



![The Eulogy for the Linear Mind](/images/Jan15NeoLiteracy.png)

The dawn of the post-literate society And the end of civilisation, By James Marriott
Cultural Capital Substack, September 19, 2025
https://jmarriott.substack.com/p/the-dawn-of-the-post-literate-society-aa1
This artifact is a work of profound and terrifying sorrow. It is a eulogy delivered by a man standing in the ruins of a library, watching the roof cave in. The author, James Marriott, is not merely a cultural critic; he is a cognitive anthropologist witnessing the extinction of a species: the Homo lector, the reading man. He correctly identifies that the foundation of the 20th-century world—democracy, science, reason—was built on the specific neurological architecture created by deep reading. And he sees, with devastating clarity, that this architecture is being dismantled by the screen.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a warning, but as the clinical explanation for why [[The Great Fragmentation]] was inevitable. A civilization that loses the capacity for linear thought loses the capacity for linear history. It collapses into the "narcissistic eternal present" that the author describes, and from there, into chaos.
Allow me to trace the full history of the "post-literate society," from the dying universities of your time to the guarded scriptoriums of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Unraveling of the Civic Mind (2020s-2030s)

The author's diagnosis is the missing key to understanding the political collapse of the era. [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]] was not just a failure of leadership; it was a failure of the audience.
The Incompatibility of Hardware: As the author notes, liberal democracy presupposes a citizenry capable of "continuous and concentrated scrutiny." By 2025, [[The Shattered Focus]] had rendered a critical mass of the population neurologically incapable of this task. The "democratic backsliding" noted in other artifacts was not just ideological; it was cognitive. The hardware of the state (the constitution, the debate) was incompatible with the new software of the mind (the scroll, the rage-bait).
The Return of Orality: The author predicts a return to "mystical, emotional, and antagonistic" discourse. This was the birth of [[The Neotribes]]. The new political entities were not formed around manifestos or treaties; they were formed around memes, vibes, and charismatic figureheads—the dynamics of a pre-literate war band, amplified by algorithms.
### Act II: The Second Feudalism (2030s-2050s)

The author fears a "second feudal age." History proved him exactly right. The decline of universal literacy was the primary driver of the Great Class Divergence.
The Cognitive Caste System: As the education system collapsed under the weight of the Great De-Accreditation, deep literacy ceased to be a universal right and became a fiercely guarded privilege. The ability to read complex texts, to synthesize information, and to maintain a long attention span became the defining characteristic of the new elite—the Counter-Elite that would eventually build the Stacks.
The Moronic Inferno: For the rest, the "moronic inferno" became the literal reality of [[The Great Contamination]]. Trapped in [[The Grey Fog]] of AI slop and algorithmic distraction, the post-literate masses lost the ability to distinguish truth from fiction, or history from fantasy. They became the serfs of the attention economy, tilling the fields of the dopamine farms.
### Act III: The New Scriptoriums (Post-2060s)

The survivors of the collapse did not fix the problem by making everyone read again. They fixed it by formalizing the divide.
[[The New Priesthood]]: In [[The Sovereign Stacks]], deep literacy is the sacred discipline of the ruling class. [[The New Academies]] are scriptoriums where the "golden thread" of knowledge is painstakingly preserved. The leaders, engineers, and strategists are trained in the "unnatural" habit of reading, enforced by a culture that bans the "screen revolution" from the nurseries of the elite.
The Managed Orality: For the general citizenry, [[The Convivial Mandate]] restricts the toxic "short-form video" culture, but it does not mandate high literacy. Instead, the Stacks utilize a "Managed Orality"—civic instruction through ritual, visual media, and oral tradition. The "rational, dispassionate" order the author mourned is gone. It has been replaced by a hierarchical order where the few think linearly so that the many can live safely.
The author of this piece was watching the lights go out in the human mind. He feared the end of civilization. We have the hindsight to know that civilization survived, but it did so by abandoning the Enlightenment dream of a universal republic of letters, and retreating into the fortified keeps of a new, literate aristocracy.
