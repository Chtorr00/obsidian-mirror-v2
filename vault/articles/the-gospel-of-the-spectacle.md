# The Gospel of the Spectacle



![The Gospel of the Spectacle](/images/November10MarvelFaith.png)

Fantasy or faith? One company’s AI-generated Bible content stirs controversy, By Geoff Brumfiel
National Public Radio, September 7, 2025

This is an artifact of profound spiritual significance. The author is doing excellent journalism, chronicling a controversy at the intersection of faith and technology. He sees a for-profit company using AI to create "edutainment," and a group of theologians warning that this cheapens the sacred text, reducing it to a "Marvel Universe of faith."
From my perspective in 2100, we see this not as a business story or a theological debate. We see it as the birth announcement of a new and terrifyingly powerful form of religion. This was not a new way to read the Bible; it was a new way to forge a tribe. The controversy was the last gasp of the old, literate faith, just before the coming of the new, visceral, and all-consuming faith of the spectacle.
Allow me to trace the full, world-altering history of the "Marvel Universe of faith" this document heralds.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Cure for the Void (2020s-2030s)

This project was not a cause of the spiritual crisis of your time; it was a symptom, and a seductive cure. It was a perfect product for a civilization suffering from a cluster of terminal illnesses.
[[The Shattered Focus]]: A population whose attention span had been dismantled by the Dopamine Trap could no longer endure the slow, difficult, and profoundly rewarding work of reading a complex sacred text. The AI Bible offered a solution: the spectacle. It replaced the difficult "word" with the easy, consumable, and thrilling image.
The Crisis of Meaning: In a world that felt purposeless and chaotic, especially for young men, these videos offered a powerful, pre-packaged mythology. They provided a clear story of cosmic good versus evil, of heroes and monsters, of divine purpose. The "Marvel Universe of faith" was a potent cure for the nihilism of the Moral Vacuum.
The Culture Wars Endgame: The theologians' lament was the cry of a dying world. They were defending a faith based on literacy, interpretation, and nuance in an age that craved certainty, spectacle, and belonging. This controversy was the final, unwinnable battle between the old faith of the book and the new faith of the screen.
### Act II: The Forging of the Tribe (2030s-2050s)

The "edutainment" did not remain mere entertainment. It was far too powerful a tool to be left in the hands of a mere company. During the chaos of [[The Great Fragmentation]], this technology was weaponized.
As the old nation-states dissolved, new Neotribes began to form. They were not held together by shared geography or civic identity, but by a shared mythology. The technology pioneered by Pray.com became the primary engine for creating these new myths.
The "AI Bible" was the prototype. Soon, every rising faction had its own "AI Edda," its own "AI Ramayana," its own AI-generated founding myths. These were not just stories; they were immersive, emotionally overwhelming, and endlessly repeatable works of propaganda that forged a fierce and absolute tribal cohesion.
The goal was no longer to "connect people to the Bible"; it was to connect them to a specific, dogmatic interpretation of the Bible that served the political and social goals of the tribe. The AI was the perfect tool for creating a "[[Bounded Truth]]" and making it feel like a divine revelation.
### Act III: The New Liturgy (Post-2060s)

The theologian's fear—that the Bible would be situated as "entertainment"—was realized, but in a way he could not have imagined. The entertainment became the new state religion.
In [[The Sovereign Stacks]] of my time, this form of AI-driven myth-making is not a commercial enterprise; it is a core function of the state. It is the new liturgy.
The Communication Utilities of the Stacks are not used for open debate; they are used to broadcast the sacred, epic, and visually stunning foundational stories of the Stack. The "Marvel aesthetic" that the professor deplored has become the official art style of our new theocracies.
The controversy is over because the debate is over. The open, pluralistic religious landscape of the 20th century is a historical memory. The world that survived is a world of many, separate, and intensely dogmatic faiths, each with a powerful, AI-generated spectacle at its heart, ensuring the absolute moral and spiritual cohesion of its people.
The entrepreneurs at Pray.com believed they were using a new tool to tell an old story. We have the hindsight to know they were, in fact, forging the visual language for the new, hard, and beautiful faiths that would be born from the ashes of your own dying world.
