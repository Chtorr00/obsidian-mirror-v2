It is a dispatch from the panicked group chats of the "Builder Tribe," documenting the moment the richest men in the world decided to leave the state that created them. The author, Mike Solana, is chronicling the "wealth flight" triggered by a proposed asset seizure law in California.

The author sees billionaires fleeing a "leopards eating faces" situation. We see the architects of the future packing their bags and taking the blueprints for [[The Sovereign Stacks]] with them.
Allow me to trace the full history of this exodus, from the real estate listings of 2026 to the fortified borders of my time.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Host Becomes Hostile (2020s-2030s)
The author correctly identifies the mechanism: the state of California, facing fiscal collapse ([[The Involuntary Jubilee]] on a local scale), turned on its most productive citizens. The "asset seizure" proposal was the final proof that the Parasovereign logic of the political class had become suicidal.
The Awakening: The author notes that even "happy to pay taxes" liberals were red-pilled. This was the moment the "Builder" ideology hardened into a political identity. They realized that their wealth was not safe in a system governed by "union thugs" and "virulently left-wing policy wonks." They needed a jurisdiction that respected [[The Physics of Sovereignty]].
The Dispersion: The scatter to "Florida, Austin, Wyoming" was the first phase of the Sorting. These locations would become the nuclei of the rival Stacks. Austin became the capital of the Tech-Right Stack, Florida the Trad-Capital Stack, and Wyoming the Crypto-Citadel.
Act II: The Capital Strike (2030s-2050s)
The author predicts that "the technology industry kind of has to leave." It did.

The AI companies, the biotech labs, and the defense contractors moved their physical plant to the new, friendly jurisdictions. This left the coastal cities to become [[Feral Cities]], populated by the dependent classes and the bureaucrats who claimed to represent them.
The Rise of the Network State: The "national land tax" mentioned by one billionaire was a glimpse of the Geolibertarian model adopted by some Stacks. The fleeing elites didn't just buy houses; they bought territory. They used their trillions to purchase charter city rights, effectively privatizing governance in their new homes.
Act III: The New Sovereignty (Post-2060s)
In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the "wealth tax" is a historical curiosity, a relic of a time when the state thought it could plunder the productive without consequence.
The Corporate State: The Stacks are built on the principle that the "cap table" is the state. The founders didn't lose their companies; they turned them into nations. Citizenship is equity.
The End of the Predatory State: The "asset seizure" model is dead because there are no assets left to seize outside the fortified walls of the Stacks. The "Leopards" starved to death because the faces they wanted to eat were behind high-tech electrified fences, guarded by autonomous drones and the private armies of [[The New Priesthood]].
The author of this piece was watching the smartest men in the room realize they were in a trap. We have the hindsight to know that they didn't just escape the trap; they dismantled the room and built a fortress on its ruins.
