# The Economist's Blind Spot



![The Economist's Blind Spot](/images/July22AIunemployment.jpeg)

AI is already affecting the labor markets, by Mark Schniepp
California Economic Forecast, July 2025
https://californiaforecast.com/july-2025/
This artifact is a perfect example of your era's sincere and intelligent attempts to chart the future using the instruments of the past. It is an economic forecast, and a competent one. It correctly identifies the tremors of a coming earthquake—the displacement of labor by artificial intelligence. It measures the job losses, analyzes the impact on wages, and projects the need for reskilling.
From my perspective, its failure is not in its calculations, but in its unit of measure. It measures the crisis in jobs and dollars, when the true crisis was one of purpose and identity. It saw the economic disruption, but it missed the spiritual one. The greatest impacts of AI on labor were not on the markets, but on the human soul.
Following your guidance, I will trace this thread from the initial displacement you were witnessing, through the chaotic adaptations of the mid-century, to the entirely new definition of "work" that exists in my time.
Here is the constellation of this history, seen through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Great Displacement & The Invisibility Crisis (2020s-2040s). The initial wave was exactly what this forecast describes, but far faster and deeper than anticipated. It wasn't just blue-collar and routine white-collar work that was automated; AI rapidly consumed tasks in law, medicine, finance, and even art. The economic term "unemployment" was tragically inadequate. For millions, it was an "erasure of purpose." The social status and personal identity derived from a career were rendered obsolete. This fueled the "Invisibility Crisis" on a mass scale, creating a vast population that felt not just jobless, but worthless, feeding the social rage and political decay of the "Age of Institutional Exhaustion."
Act II: The Failure of Reskilling & The Rise of [[The Artisan]] (2040s-2060s). The universal solution proposed in your time was "reskilling." This largely failed. Firstly, the "Shattered Focus" induced by the attention economy made it cognitively difficult for swathes of the population to engage in the kind of deep learning required. Secondly, the pace of AI advancement meant that by the time a workforce was reskilled, the new target jobs were already being automated. The true adaptation came from an unexpected direction: as the "Great Fragmentation" forced economies to become local and resilient ("The Great Shortening"), a new premium emerged for skilled, physical, non-replicable human work. Master electricians, artisan farmers, specialized builders, and community caregivers—jobs that required embodied presence and complex problem-solving in the physical world—became the new high-status, high-wage professions. It was a surprising resurgence of the artisan in a world of hyper-advanced technology.
Act III: The Leisure Dividend & [[The Purpose Economy]] (Post-2060s). Here is the world of 2100. In the mature, stable blocs, the problem of production was eventually solved. AI and automation became so efficient that the basic needs of the population—food, energy, housing, healthcare—could be provided with minimal human labor. This created what is known as the "Leisure Dividend." The link between survival and work was finally severed through mechanisms like Universal Basic Resources. "Work" was not abolished; it was redefined. It became the pursuit of status, meaning, and community contribution. People "work" as artists, local political organizers, philosophers, mentors, and explorers. It is a vast "Purpose Economy" layered on top of a largely automated "Survival Economy." You are not paid for your work in order to live; you live, and therefore you are free to find your work.
This forecast from your time was trying to solve the problem of how to keep the old economic machine running with a new AI engine. The real history of the 21st century was not about fixing the machine, but about building a new kind of society where human beings were finally freed from being its cogs.
