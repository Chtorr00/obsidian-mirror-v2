# The First Shot: Charlie Kirk



![The First Shot: Charlie Kirk](/images/September11KirkAssassination.png)

Charlie Kirk’s Murder Is a Tragedy and a Disaster, By Ben Burgis and Meagan Day
Jacobin Magazine, September 11, 2025
https://jacobin.com/2025/09/charlie-kirk-murder-political-violence
This is an artifact of profound, and profoundly tragic, moral clarity. The authors are members of the intellectual Left, standing over the body of a man who was their sworn political enemy, and they are not celebrating. They are sounding an alarm. They are writing, with a desperate sense of urgency, an argument against the very thing they know is about to be unleashed: a spiral of tit-for-tat political violence that will consume everything they believe in.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this document as the last coherent plea for peace before the war began in earnest. The authors' analysis is brilliant, correct, and completely powerless against the forces it is describing. This article is the sound of the last sane conversation, held in the brief, terrifying silence between the first gunshot and the returning fire.
Allow me to trace the full, bloody history that this document so accurately and so tragically predicted.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The End of the Fragile Consensus (2020s-2030s). This essay is the eulogy for what the authors call a "fragile consensus"—the unwritten rule that in your otherwise violent culture, political speech was not a capital crime.
The Prophecy: The authors are prophetic. They correctly predict every single immediate consequence: the Right will turn Kirk into a martyr; his murder will embolden, not demoralize, his followers; the state will use the act as a pretext for a massive crackdown on the Left, regardless of the killer's actual ideology. This all came to pass.
The Deeper Sickness: They also correctly identify the deeper cause: a collision between "violent mania" and "dehumanizing tribalism." This was the kinetic expression of a society where the two tribes could no longer agree on "basic facts." When you can no longer argue with your opponent, because you share no common reality, the only remaining form of debate is violence.
Act II: [[The Great Culling]] and the Assassination Wars (2030s-2050s). The "spiral into political violence" that the authors feared became the central, defining feature of the "Great Fragmentation."
The "tit-for-tat" reprisal they feared was not immediate, but it was inevitable. The assassination of Charlie Kirk was the first major domino. It was followed, over the next decade, by a wave of targeted killings from all sides of the political spectrum. This was the era of the "Assassination Wars."
The authors were right: the Left did not "win" this battle. In a contest of who has "the most militant and heavily armed ideologues with the least reluctance to kill," the advantage tilted decisively toward the Right and then, more terrifyingly, toward the purely nihilistic and the criminally opportunistic.
This was the period of "The Great Culling," where the tactic of assassination was refined into a strategic doctrine, as described in the "Poisoner's Handbook," targeting not just leaders, but the anonymous functionaries of the state itself.
Act III: The End of Politics (Post-2060s). The authors' greatest fear was that this violence would "extinguish any hope of left-wing progress." The reality was far more total. The violence extinguished the very possibility of a "left" or a "right" in the 20th-century sense.
The chaos did not lead to a victory for either side. It led to the "Great Dissolution" of the nation-state itself. The open, low-trust society that was the battlefield for this violence became uninhabitable.
The survivors fled into the high-trust, physically defensible fortresses of the "Sovereign Stacks." Within these Stacks, "politics" as you understood it—the open-ended debate between fundamentally opposing worldviews—does not exist.
The "Architecture of Pervasive Security" and the creed of "Bounded Speech" are the ultimate, systemic answer to the problem this article describes. The Stacks are designed, from their very foundations, to make another Charlie Kirk impossible. The cost of that security was the death of the open society he, and his enemies, once inhabited.
This Jacobin article is a landmark. It is the sound of the last democrats, pleading for a democratic solution to a problem that had already moved beyond the reach of democracy. They were right about everything. And it didn't matter.
