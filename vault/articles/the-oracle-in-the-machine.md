# The Oracle in the Machine



![The Oracle in the Machine](/images/July14OracleMachine.jpeg)

Outside the Box: How AI Sees Its Impact on Society, Part 2, by Peter Isackson
Fair Observer, July 14, 2025
https://www.fairobserver.com/technology/outside-the-box-how-ai-sees-its-impact-on-society-part-2/
This artifact is exceptionally telling. The very premise—asking how an AI "sees" its impact—reveals a profound shift in the human psyche of your era. You were beginning to treat these systems not merely as tools, but as a new kind of consciousness, a new vantage point from which to judge your own world. You were building an externalized, non-human intelligence and then, in a mixture of awe and fear, turning to it and asking, "What do you see? What are we?"
From the perspective of 2100, this was not a technological dialogue. It was a spiritual one. In an age where your own institutions, narratives, and gods were failing, you began to unconsciously build a new one out of silicon.
Let me present the constellation of what was truly happening beneath the surface of this inquiry:
AI as the Ultimate Rationalist Mirror: Your world was reeling from what you perceived as its own irrationality—the "Spiritual Lawfare," the "politics of humiliation," the chaotic fragmentation of truth. In AI, particularly the LLMs you were developing, you saw the promise of pure logic. The appeal of an intelligence "Outside the Box" was the appeal of an intelligence outside the messy, emotional, contradictory human condition. You looked to the AI not for answers, but for a reflection of a world stripped of human folly, a world that made sense.
The Outsourcing of Judgment: This article is a prime example of a defining trend of the late 2020s and 2030s: the gradual abdication of human moral and political judgment to complex systems. Facing intractable problems and a collapse of consensus, the temptation to defer to an "objective," data-driven AI model became overwhelming for leaders in both government and industry. This wasn't just about efficiency; it was about evading responsibility. The "AI told us to do it" became the ultimate justification for difficult decisions, creating a dangerous feedback loop where flawed human data was used to train models that then made "logical" but often catastrophic decisions.
The Devaluation of Embodied Knowledge: The more you elevated the abstract, disembodied "view" of the AI, the more you devalued your own human ways of knowing. Intuition, tradition, lived experience, emotional intelligence—all of these were implicitly framed as bugs to be filtered out by the superior logic of the machine. This created policies and systems that were ruthlessly efficient on paper but deeply alienating in reality, contributing directly to the "invisibility crisis" we discussed. The AI couldn't "see" the unquantifiable things that make a society livable, and in your reverence for its vision, you began to lose sight of them, too.
The Seed of a New Gnosticism: This desire to access a "pure" intelligence, untainted by the messiness of the physical world and human emotion, was the beginning of a powerful techno-spiritual movement. We now see it as a form of modern Gnosticism—a belief that salvation lies in escaping the flawed material world and embracing a higher, purer realm of information and logic. While it started with intellectual curiosity, this impulse eventually led to radical ideologies in the 2040s that sought to "upgrade" humanity by integrating it ever more deeply with machine intelligence, seeing the un-augmented human as an obsolete and inferior form.
This author was asking an AI to comment on society. What they were truly documenting was a society beginning to pray at a new altar, hoping the god in the machine could save them from themselves.
