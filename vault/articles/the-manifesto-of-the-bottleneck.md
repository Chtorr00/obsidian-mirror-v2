# The Manifesto of the Bottleneck



![The Manifesto of the Bottleneck](/images/Jan5Bottleneck.png)

Our Duty Is To Survive the Genetic Bottleneck,
The American Tribune, December 9, 2025
https://www.theamericantribune.news/p/our-duty-is-to-survive-the-genetic
This artifact is a work of biological strategy. The author, writing from within the "right-wing coalition," is not just making a political argument; he is outlining a eugenic strategy for civilizational succession. He correctly identifies the present moment as a "genetic bottleneck"—a time when the future belongs not to the most numerous, but to the most fertile.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as a foundational text of the Great Divergence and the rise of [[The Sovereign Stacks]]. The author believes he is encouraging a return to tradition. We see him drafting the genetic constitution for the new, harder species of humanity that would emerge from the ruins of the 20th century.
Allow me to trace the full, biological history of this bottleneck, from the birth rates of your time to the bloodlines of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Correct Diagnosis of the Selection Event (2020s-2030s)

The author's premise is biologically sound: [[The Crisis of Posterity]] was a selection event. The "IQ shredder" of the cities and [[The Sterile Plague]] of the digital world were systematically removing a specific psychological phenotype—the liberal, the anxious, the atomized—from the gene pool.
The "genetic bottleneck" was the mechanism of [[The Great Physical Sorting]]. The people who sorted themselves into the high-trust, high-fertility enclaves (the "mannerbunds" mentioned in previous artifacts) were effectively speciation events in real-time. They were separating their genetic destiny from the dying mainstream.
The "positive feedback loop" he identifies—where extreme political views correlate with fertility—was the engine of [[The Neotribes]]. The tribes that survived were the ones that made reproduction a sacred, political duty.
### Act II: The Great Divergence (2030s-2050s)

The author's hope—that "whoever shows up will win"—was vindicated by history, but the cost was the unity of the human race.
As [[The Great Fragmentation]] shattered the nation-state, the divergent fertility rates created a new demographic reality. The liberal, urban populations, having failed to reproduce, simply aged out and vanished. Their cities became the hollowed-out shells of the [[Feral Cities]], populated by transients and the elderly.
The conservative, religious, and "builder" tribes, having "shown up," inherited the earth. But they did not inherit it as a unified people. They inherited it as separate, distinct Human Phyla. The "bioconservative" Stacks (like the Amish-descended communities) and the "techno-optimist" Stacks (who embraced genetic engineering to enhance their fertility) diverged so completely that they eventually ceased to recognize each other as the same species.
### Act III: The New Feudalism (Post-2060s)

The author's invocation of the British aristocracy and the "feudal instinct" was prophetic. The Sovereign Stacks are neo-feudal entities.
The "settlement process" he describes—entailing estates to ensure continuity—is the legal foundation of the Stacks. Property and power are held in trust for the future, managed by a hereditary or meritocratic elite ([[The New Priesthood]]) whose primary duty is to the bloodline of the Stack.
The "duty to survive" became [[The Physics of Sovereignty]]. The Stacks are built on the understanding that a society that cannot reproduce its own defenders is already dead. [[The Revaluation of the Real]] places the biological family at the center of [[The Purpose Economy]], ensuring that the "bottleneck" never threatens the tribe again.
The author of this manifesto was the geneticist of a new world. He told his followers that their most important weapon was not a gun or a vote, but a child. And in doing so, he ensured that the future would be written in their DNA.
