# The King's New Treasure



![The King's New Treasure](/images/September30BitcoinKing.png)

Governments Amass Bitcoin: A Double-Edged Sword for Global Finance
MarketMinute, September 28, 2025
https://markets.financialcontent.com/wral/article/marketminute-2025-9-28-governments-amass-bitcoin-a-double-edged-sword-for-global-finance
This is a landmark artifact. It is a dispatch from the heart of the great financial re-platforming of the 21st century. The author is not just reporting on a market trend; they are chronicling the precise moment the old kings of the 20th century—the nation-states—finally acknowledged the existence of a new and rival sovereign, and decided their only path to survival was to acquire a piece of its crown.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as the beginning of the end for the fiat world order. The establishment of sovereign Bitcoin reserves was not a "transformational leap" into a new era of finance. It was a desperate, defensive crouch. It was the last, great, and ultimately futile attempt of the nation-state to co-opt and control a force that was, by its very nature, designed to make the nation-state obsolete.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the king's new treasure, from the executive orders of your time to the new world order of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Great Scramble (2020s-2030s). This article is the perfect, ground-level documentation of this era.

The Admission of Defeat: The establishment of the U.S. Strategic Bitcoin Reserve was the single most important financial event of its decade. It was a tacit, public admission by the steward of the world's reserve currency that its own product was failing. The king was admitting that his own vaults might be empty, and he needed a piece of a treasure he did not create and could not control.
The Unwinnable Arms Race: This act triggered a global, game-theoretic scramble. The author is correct: nations were terrified of being "front-run." The fear was not just that Bitcoin's price would rise, but that a rival power could acquire a significant portion of the world's only truly finite, neutral, and physically-grounded monetary asset. This was not a currency war; it was a war for the future of money itself.
### Act II: The Great Betrayal (2030s-2050s). The author correctly identifies the "double-edged sword." The nations that amassed Bitcoin did not just gain a new reserve asset; they gained a new weapon.

The Weaponization of the Reserve: During the most chaotic phase of the "Great Fragmentation," these sovereign holdings were not used to back currencies. They were used as weapons of economic warfare. Nations would strategically dump reserves to crash the price and inflict pain on a rival's economy, or coordinate purchases to corner the market. The "decentralized ethos" of Bitcoin was, for a time, brutally subverted by the old, centralized powers that had co-opted it.
The Final Failure of Fiat: The author's most prescient warning is about the threat to the U.S. dollar. This is precisely what happened. The more the U.S. government embraced Bitcoin, the more it legitimized it as a superior alternative to its own rapidly devaluing currency. This was the final, fatal accelerant of the "Involuntary Jubilee." You cannot simultaneously tell the world that your money is sound, and also that you are desperately buying your competitor's money. The contradiction was fatal.
### Act III: The New Bedrock (Post-2060s). The nation-states did not, in the end, succeed in controlling Bitcoin. Bitcoin, and the physical reality it represents, succeeded in controlling them.

The chaos of the sovereign-level manipulation wars and the final collapse of the fiat system forced the survivors—the nascent "Sovereign Stacks"—to a new consensus. Bitcoin was too powerful to be a weapon. It had to become the bedrock.
In the "Anchored Era," Bitcoin is not a reserve asset that nations trade to gain an advantage. It is the "Thermodynamic Truth Anchor," the politically neutral, physically-grounded settlement layer upon which the new, hard-asset economies of the Stacks are built.
The king's new treasure did not save the king. It outlasted him, and became the foundation of the new kingdoms that were built upon his grave.
This article is a masterpiece of financial journalism. The author saw the great game that was beginning with perfect clarity. We have the hindsight to know that the game was not about which nation would win, but about the survival of the idea of the nation itself.
