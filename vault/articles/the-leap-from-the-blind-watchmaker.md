# The Leap from the Blind Watchmaker



![The Leap from the Blind Watchmaker](/images/Feb18BlindWatch.png)

Natura Facit Saltum: Preface to The Frozen Gene - The End of Human Evolution, By Steve Keen
Building a New Economics Substack, February 3, 2026
https://profstevekeen.substack.com/p/natura-facit-saltum
This artifact is a work of heretical biology. The author, a heterodox economist, is writing a preface for a book by a controversial publisher. He is challenging the foundational dogma of modern biology: that evolution is a slow, random process. He argues instead that nature makes "leaps," driven by quantum computation within the DNA itself, and that these leaps are a response to stress.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as a seminal text in the Biotech Reformation. It is the intellectual bridge between the "random mutation" model of the 20th century and [[The Engineered]] Phyla of the 21st. The author believes he is debating history. We know he is describing the mechanism that allowed humanity to speciate in real-time.
Allow me to trace the full, saltational history of this idea, from the fringe books of your time to the genetic labs of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Rejection of Gradualism (2020s-2030s)
The author's core thesis—that "random mutation" is statistically impossible—became the scientific consensus of the Counter-Elite.
The Directed Evolution: As the Biotech Faction (the "Vampire Elites") began their experiments, they realized that the "blind watchmaker" was too slow. They needed to force the leap. The idea that stress triggers quantum evolution was the key.
The Stress Test: [[The Great Fragmentation]] was the evolutionary stress event. The collapse of the global order, the Great Hunger, and [[The AI Energy Wall]] created the environmental pressure that unlocked the "introns" of human potential. The tribes that survived were the ones that "accelerated their evolution" by embracing this stress rather than fleeing from it.
Act II: The [[Quantum Biology]] of the Stack (2030s-2050s)
The connection between quantum biology and consciousness became the central mystery of [[The AGI Contention of 2068]].
The Failure of Silicon: The reason LLMs failed to achieve true AGI for a generation was precisely because they were built on classical, binary logic. They lacked the "quantum computation" inherent in DNA. They could calculate, but they could not leap.
The Wetware Solution: To bridge the gap, [[The Transcended]] Stacks (Cyborgs) began integrating biological wetware (human neurons, engineered DNA computers) into their AI systems. They realized that to build a god, you needed the "frozen gene" to thaw out and compute.
Act III: The Sudden Origins of the New Human (Post-2060s)
The author's discussion of "homeobox genes" and "sudden appearance" describes the birth of [[The Human Phyla]].
The Speciation Event: The differentiation between the Engineered, the Transcended, and [[The Purified]] was not a gradual drift. It was a saltation. In one or two generations, guided by the new science of quantum genetics, the populations of the Stacks diverged morphologically and cognitively.
The New Racism: The author's chilling note—that "racism... may be a distant echo of this process of shunning"—became the reality of inter-Stack relations. The Phyla do not view each other as races in the 20th-century sense, but as distinct species. The "outcast" phenotype became the foreigner.
The author of this preface was arguing against Darwin. He didn't realize he was arguing for the right of humanity to take the pen out of nature's hand and write its own, sudden, and terrifying next chapter.
