# The Midwives of a Harder Age



![The Midwives of a Harder Age](/images/December1Parasovereigns.png)

Tribal Future of the West, By Josh Neal and Mike Maxwell
Psychopolitics Podcast, November 20, 2025
https://jneal.substack.com/p/mike-maxwell-tribal-future-of-the
This is not a podcast. It is a council of war, held in the open, for all the world to hear. The speakers are not commentators; they are the intellectual midwives of a new and harder age. They believe they are chronicling the death of the 20th-century nation-state. From my perspective, they are doing something far more significant: they are writing, in real-time, the founding charter for the tribal fortresses that will replace it.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as one of the most important ideological documents of its decade. The author's thesis, which he calls the "tribal future," is a near-perfect, ground-level description of the process we now call [[The Great Fragmentation]]. He is not predicting the future; he is diagnosing the present with a terrifying and unflinching clarity, and in doing so, he is handing his followers the blueprint for what comes next.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the world these midwives were helping to deliver.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Correct Autopsy of the Nation-State (2020s-2030s)
The author, Mike Maxwell, has performed a perfect autopsy on the dying body of the Westphalian state. Every one of his "driving forces" is a correct and precise diagnosis of the terminal illnesses of [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]].
His "decline of hard material capacity" is the lived reality of [[The Supply Chain Wars]] and the first tremors of [[The AI Energy Wall]]. He correctly sees that the thermodynamic and industrial base of the old world is eroding.
His "epistemic divorce" is the perfect term for the social reality of a world where a shared truth has died, a direct consequence of [[The Great Contamination]] and the Moral Vacuum.
His "elite fragmentation" and the weaponization of "lawfare" is a textbook description of Spiritual Lawfare in the Culture Wars Endgame.
His identification of "parasovereigns"—cartels, gangs, ideological networks—is the discovery of the new political species that will thrive in the ruins of the old. These are the chaotic, larval forms of [[The Neotribes]].
Act II: The Inevitable Failure of the "Peaceful Option" (2030s-2050s)
The author's strategic prescription is the key to understanding the tragedy that followed. He frames nationalism—the "remigration" of Martin Sellner—as the "last peaceful option." He is correct. And the history of my time is the story of that option's catastrophic failure.
The nation-states of your era lacked the political will, the social cohesion, and the institutional capacity to implement such a radical reversal. Nationalism was not the cure that was administered; it was the final, violent fever that broke the patient. The attempt to reassert national sovereignty in a world that was already fragmenting only accelerated the collapse.
This failure led directly to the author's "backup plan" becoming the only plan. His call to action—to "build parallel structures," "intentional communities," and "Männerbunds"—was not a fringe activity. It became the single most important survival strategy of [[The Great Dissolution]]. The future did not belong to the people who tried to reform the dying state; it belonged to the people who took this author's advice and began building the lifeboats.
Act III: The New Sovereigns of the Fortress (Post-2060s)
The author's "tribal future" is the world of my time. The "parasovereigns" he identified did not remain parasites. They became the new sovereigns.
[[The Sovereign Stacks]] are the ultimate, scaled-up, and technologically sophisticated realization of the "Männerbund." They are the high-trust, defensible, and ideologically coherent tribes that survived the collapse.
The author's lament that "it's going to be a very precarious future" was a profound understatement. The transition was the Curriculum of Collapse, a brutal and violent education. But he is also correct about the outcome. The survivors rediscovered meaning not in abstract universalism, but in the hard, physical reality of defending their own tribe, their own fortress. The "problem of meaning doesn't arise when your house is on fire."
The author's sense of historical importance—that "people will look back in a century or so and we'll study what we're doing"—was not arrogance. It was a correct assessment. He and his intellectual circle were the first generation of [[The New Priesthood]], forging the "[[Bounded Truth]]" and the founding myths for the new, smaller, and harder worlds that were being born from the chaos.
The men in this conversation believed they were holding a wake for the nation-state. We have the hindsight to know they were, in fact, presiding over a baptism—the baptism, in fire and chaos, of the tribal sovereigns that would inherit the earth.
