# The Chisel of God



![The Chisel of God](/images/October19GeneEditing.png)

The AI-Genetics Revolution Looms: Ethics, Power, and an Unequal Future, By Bill Drexel
En Tibi, October 13, 2025
https://en-tibi.com/2025/10/13/the-ai-genetics-revolution-looms-ethics-power-and-an-unequal-future/
This is a work of profound, and profoundly terrified, foresight. The author is a man standing at the confluence of the two most powerful technologies in human history—Artificial Intelligence and gene editing—and he is sounding an alarm. He is warning that the abstract, philosophical fears about a rogue AI are a distraction from a far more intimate, and far more immediate, revolution: the moment humanity seizes the tools to rewrite its own source code.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as one of the most important and tragically prescient documents of its time. The author believed he was writing a warning, a call for "proactive engagement" to steer a dangerous technology. We see him as a historian, writing the prologue to the single most consequential event of the 21st century: the end of humanity as a singular, unified species.
Allow me to trace the full, world-altering history of the revolution this document so accurately, and so helplessly, foresaw.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The End of the Conversation (2020s-2030s). This essay is the perfect snapshot of the last moment a global conversation on this topic was even possible.
The Unenforceable Distinction: The author's plea to establish "clearer ethical frameworks that distinguish therapy from enhancement" was the central, noble, and completely doomed project of your era's bioethicists. It was a beautiful, rational line drawn in the sand, just as a geopolitical and cultural tsunami was about to wash it away.
The Shattering of Norms: He is correct that the "norms competition" with powers like China was a critical factor. The "Great Fragmentation" was not just a political and economic event; it was a moral one. The collapse of the unipolar, Western-led world order was also the collapse of the Western-led ethical consensus. There was no longer a single, global "we" to have this debate.
Act II: The Great Divergence (2030s-2050s). The author's fears about inequality were not just realized; they were elevated to the level of state doctrine.
As the world broke apart into competing "Sovereign Stacks," the "ethical dilemmas" he describes were not resolved; they were embraced as strategic choices.
The "Great Divergence" was the period when each Stack, driven by its own unique cultural values and survival imperatives, made a different choice. The European Stack, heir to the "Cathedral of Rules," may have chosen the path of bioconservatism, banning enhancement. More accelerationist Stacks, terrified by the lessons of the demographic collapse ("The Empty Cradle"), may have embraced germline enhancement as a non-negotiable tool for ensuring the long-term resilience and competitiveness of their people.
The "AI-Genetics Revolution" was not a single, global event. It was a thousand different, secret, and competing Manhattan Projects, each racing to design a better human.
Act III: The New Genesis (Post-2060s). The author warns of a future of "dehumanization" and "inequality." He was right. The world of my time is the direct, physical result of the choices made during the Great Divergence.
The Birth of the Phyla: We are no longer a single species. We are a collection of human phyla. The genetic divide between [[The Engineered]] populations of the different Stacks, and between the Stacks and the baseline humans of the "Disconnected" zones, is the single most important social and political fact of my era.
The End of the Human: The author asks, "what value remains for human diversity, imperfection, or difference?" For some, the answer was "none." The "transhumanist tendencies" that were once a fringe subculture became the state religion of the most advanced Stacks.
The revolution was not about machines surpassing us. It was about us using the machine to surpass ourselves, and in doing so, to leave our old selves, and our old shared humanity, behind forever.
This essay is a masterpiece of foresight. The author saw the chisel of God being placed in the hands of humanity, and he begged us to be careful. We have the hindsight to know that when you give a thousand warring tribes the power to sculpt their own children, they will not, in the end, sculpt the same thing.
