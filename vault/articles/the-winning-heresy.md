# The Winning Heresy



![The Winning Heresy](/images/August29TradePlugs.jpeg)

The Mutualist Economy: A New Deal for Ownership, Yakov Feygin and Nils Gilman
The Berggeruen Institute, April 23, 2020
https://berggruen.org/news/the-mutualist-economy-a-new-deal-for-ownership
This document is a prophecy from the wilderness. It is a blueprint for a world that was, in your time, considered a forgotten dream. The author is advocating for a "third way," a mutualist economy that is neither state-controlled socialism nor corporate-controlled capitalism.
From my vantage point in 2100, we read this document as the single most prescient and accurate economic vision of its era. It was almost entirely ignored in its own time. And yet, it became the essential, foundational blueprint for the resilient societies that rose from the ashes of the world that ignored it.
Here is the constellation of the history this heretical, and ultimately triumphant, document described:
Act I: The Whisper in the Void (2020s-2030s). In your time, this idea was a whisper. The global conversation was a shouting match between the titans of state power and the titans of corporate power. This document, with its focus on cooperatives, guilds, and decentralized, human-scale enterprise, was a heresy against both.
It was a correct and devastating diagnosis of the spiritual sickness of your economy. It recognized that the alienation and fragility of your system stemmed from the fact that individuals had no ownership, no agency, and no meaningful stake in their own economic lives.
While the mainstream was debating how to regulate or unleash the giant, impersonal systems of the 20th century, this document was quietly outlining the architecture of their replacement.
Act II: The Survival Strategy (2030s-2050s). The heresy became a survival strategy. During the "Great Fragmentation," the giant, impersonal systems of the 20th century were the first things to break.
The global corporations collapsed in the "Great Shakeout," unable to manage their brittle supply chains or their alienated workforces.
The nation-states failed in the "Great Dissolution," unable to fulfill their promises or manage the cascading crises.
What survived? The small, the local, the cooperative. The principles of mutualism were not adopted out of ideology; they were adopted out of desperation. High-trust, local, cooperative enterprises were the only things that proved resilient enough to keep the lights on and the food growing. The mutualist economy was the lifeboat.
Act III: The New Common Sense (Post-2060s). The survival strategy became the new common sense. The economic architecture of the "Sovereign Stacks" is the direct, scaled-up inheritance of the mutualist dream.
The "Guilds" of my time are the direct descendants of the cooperative, worker-owned structures described in this paper. They are the institutions that provide durable identity, shared ownership, and a sense of belonging that the old corporate model destroyed.
The "Purpose Economy" is the ultimate expression of this vision. With survival guaranteed by Universal Basic Resources, the "work" of the citizenry is to participate in these mutualist, project-based enterprises, driven not by profit, but by the pursuit of meaning, excellence, and community well-being.
The "Mutualist Economy" is the name we give to the vibrant, human-scale system that thrives on top of the automated "Survival Economy."
This document was ignored by the kings and presidents of your time. But it was discovered and read by the survivors of their failures. It was the winning heresy, the quiet blueprint that taught us how to build a world that was not just rich, but was finally, and resiliently, our own.
