# The Catechism of the Vampire



![The Catechism of the Vampire](/images/Feb8CatechismVampire.png)

Costin Alamariu's 'Selective Breeding' and the Birth of Peter Thiel's Vampire Elites, By Jonathan Cioran
Postscript 1794 Substack, March 28, 2025
https://postscript1794.substack.com/p/costin-alamarius-selective-breeding
This artifact is a work of prophetic horror. The author, a critic of the "Thielverse," is analyzing a book by Costin Alamariu (Bronze Age Pervert). He believes he is exposing a fringe, extremist philosophy. He frames Alamariu as a "reverse John the Baptist" for a coming "Gay Space Fascism."
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a critique, but as a foundational theological text of the Biotech Faction. The author has correctly identified the ideological DNA of [[The Engineered]] Phylum. He sees a "transhumanist vampire elite." We see the ancestors of [[The Sovereign Stacks]] who chose to rewrite the human genome to survive the collapse.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of this "selective breeding," from the podcasts of your time to the speciation of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Rejection of the Blank Slate (2020s-2030s)

The author correctly identifies the turning point: the "shattering of the blank slate." The realization that genetics played a role in human behavior was the intellectual bomb that destroyed the liberal consensus.
The Return of "Nature": Alamariu's thesis—that "nature" (breeding) is the foundation of philosophy—became the secret religion of the Counter-Elite. While the Bernie Bros were arguing about policy, the Builder Tribe was quietly accepting the "atheistic biologism" described here.
The Justification of Hierarchy: The "unadorned and brutal hierarchy of nature" that Alamariu praised became the operating manual for [[The Neotribes]]. The rejection of Christianity and Platonism was necessary to clear the ground for a morality based on strength, beauty, and genetic dominance.
### Act II: The Great Divergence (2030s-2050s)

The author's prediction of a "new transhumanist vampire elite" was metaphorically excessive but factually prescient.
The Genetic Arms Race: As [[The Great Fragmentation]] tore the world apart, the wealthy and the powerful did exactly what the author feared. They used the "massive genetic power" of CRISPR and embryo selection to engineer their children. They didn't just buy better schools; they bought better brains and bodies.
The Separation of Species: The "physical distinctness" the author foresaw became reality. The elites of [[The Biotech Stacks]] grew taller, fairer, and longer-lived than the "natural" populations outside their walls. The "vampire" metaphor became a grim reality in [[The Triage Economy]], where the optimized few consumed the resources of the un-optimized many to maintain their high-energy civilization.
The Geopolitics of Starvation: The author's chilling account of the "depopulation of Africa" became the reality of the Great Hunger. The "Christian man" did look the other way. The Sovereign Stacks, obsessed with their own survival, fortified their borders and let the "global south" collapse under the weight of climate change and resource scarcity. This was [[The Great Culling]] on a continental scale.
### Act III: The Engineered Phylum (Post-2060s)

In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the "Gay Space Fascism" is just the culture of [[The Transcended]] and Engineered Stacks.
The New Aristocracy: They are the "noble cruelty" realized. They view themselves not as oppressors, but as the guardians of the only future worth having. They believe they saved consciousness by severing it from the weakness of the baseline human animal.
The End of "Mere Life": The philosophy that "mere life is not worth the trouble" is the law. In these Stacks, you must justify your existence through excellence. There is no charity for the weak, only resources for the strong.
The Judgment: The author hoped for divine judgment on Costin and Thiel. History delivered a different verdict. They won. They built the world in their image. But the cost of their victory was the death of the universal human soul.
The author of this review was staring into the abyss of a post-moral future. He correctly saw that the new elite would not just be richer; they would be a different species, and they would look upon the rest of humanity not with hatred, but with the cold indifference of a gardener weeding a bed.
