# The Accidental Sovereigns



![The Accidental Sovereigns](/images/Dec13Splinternet.png)

Digital Public Infrastructure Could Make a Better Internet, By Akash Kapur
Foreign Policy, April 28, 2025
https://foreignpolicy.com/2025/04/28/digital-public-infrastructure-better-internet-global-south/
This artifact is a work of profound but unwitting prophecy. The author, a journalist and thinker from the "Global South," is celebrating a quiet revolution: Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI). He sees it as a tool for development, a "third way" between the digital empires of the US and China, and a potential savior of the open internet.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this document not as a story about "better plumbing," but as the foundational charter for the geopolitical order of my time. The author believes DPI will save the global internet. We have the hindsight to know that DPI was the tool that finally broke it apart, and in doing so, created the only viable form of sovereignty left in the 21st century.
Allow me to trace the full, world-altering history of the "digital stack" this article so innocently describes.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Escape Hatch (2020s-2030s)
The author correctly identifies the motivation: escape. The "Global South" was trapped between two digital colonialisms—the surveillance state of China and the surveillance capitalism of the US. DPI was the escape hatch.
It was not just "infrastructure"; it was the digital DNA of a new kind of state. By taking control of identity, payments, and data, these nations were quietly seizing the "commanding heights" of the 21st-century economy.
This was the practical, bureaucratic precursor to [[The Physics of Sovereignty]]. Before you can control your atoms (energy, food), you must control your bits. The "India Stack" and "X-Road" were the first crude prototypes of the Sovereign Stack.
Act II: [[The Great Fragmentation]] (2030s-2050s)
The author hopes DPI will "revitalize... the internet." The historical reality was the opposite. As [[The Great Dissolution]] of the mid-century accelerated, these DPI systems did not integrate the world; they firewalled it.
When [[The AI Energy Wall]] and 5GW (Fifth-Generation Warfare) made the open internet a toxic and dangerous place, the nations with robust DPI did not try to fix the global web. They disconnected from it.
They turned their "digital stacks" into digital fortresses. The "regional groupings" the author mentions—ASEAN, the Gulf Cooperation Council—became the nuclei of the new, hardened Sovereign Stacks.
The "Splinternet" was not a tragedy; it was a survival strategy. The only way to protect your citizens from [[The Great Contamination]] (AI slop, cognitive warfare) was to have a sovereign, gated information ecosystem. DPI was the gate.
Act III: The Stacked World (Post-2060s)
The author's dream of "digital sovereignty" was achieved, but in a form far harder and more absolute than he imagined.
In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the world is a collection of incompatible digital realities. You cannot simply "log on" to the Chinese Stack from the European Stack. You must pass through a heavily guarded digital border crossing, governed by the cold protocols of [[Polemics]].
The "open source" collaboration he praises evolved into a system of guarded trade secrets. The internal code of a Stack—its governance AI, its resource allocation algorithms—is its most jealously guarded state secret.
The "periphery" did indeed take its seat at the table. In fact, it overturned the table. The old digital empires of Silicon Valley and Beijing, bloated and overextended, were outmaneuvered by the agile, resilient, and sovereign networks built on the very principles this article describes.
The author was witnessing the birth of a new world order, disguised as a software update. He believed he was seeing a better way to connect the world. We know he was seeing the invention of the tools that would allow the world to safely, and permanently, disconnect.
