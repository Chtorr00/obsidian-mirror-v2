# The Forging of the Enemy



![The Forging of the Enemy](/images/November20DigitalHate.png)

Millions of Gen Zers are watching Hitler and Holocaust denial clips on Instagram—right next to JPMorgan and U.S. Army ads after Meta’s hate-speech shift, By Eva Roytburg
Fortune, November 20, 2025
https://fortune.com/2025/11/20/gen-z-instagram-reels-millions-views-hitler-holocaust-denial-influencers/
This is an artifact of profound historical importance. The journalist is performing a work of immense courage, wading into the digital sewers of her time to document the industrial-scale production of a psychic poison. She sees a catastrophic failure of content moderation, a perverse economic incentive structure, and the horrifying resurgence of a century-old hatred.
From my perspective in 2100, we see this not as a failure of a platform, but as a core feature of a civilization in its final, convulsive stages of collapse. This was not a story about bad content; it was a story about the forging of an enemy. It was the precise moment the abstract, free-floating anxiety of the Crisis of Meaning and the directionless rage of a betrayed generation were given a specific, historical, and lethally effective target.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the poison this article so accurately analyzes.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Perfect Psychic Pathogen (2020s-2030s)
The phenomenon this article describes was not a simple resurgence of old propaganda. It was a new and terrifyingly effective form of cognitive warfare, a perfect pathogen engineered for the specific vulnerabilities of the 21st-century mind.
The Delivery System: The algorithm, as the author notes, was optimized for one thing: engagement. In a world of Shattered Focus, the most engaging content was that which was most transgressive, most emotionally potent, and most conspiratorial. The machine, with no malice, had become a perfect delivery system for the virus.
The Camouflage: The content was wrapped in layers of irony, esoteric lore ("Agartha"), and humor. This was its genius. It was not a lecture; it was a secret handshake, a "forbidden wisdom." For a generation raised in the sterile, corporate landscape of the late globalist order, this feeling of transgression was a powerful drug. It made the poison feel like a cure.
The Host: The target was a generation of young people, particularly men, who were already suffering. They were disconnected by [[The Sterile Plague]], felt invisible from the Invisibility Crisis, and had been stripped of their own cultural inheritance by a generation of Spiritual Lawfare. They were a people with a compromised cognitive immune system, starved for a story that could explain their pain. This pathogen provided a simple, powerful, and deeply evil one.
Act II: The Naming of the Demon (2030s-2050s)
The author correctly links this digital poison to real-world violence. This was not a fringe connection; it was the central dynamic of [[The Great Fragmentation]]. The abstract hatred forged in the digital world became the kinetic energy of the physical one.
This content was the missing piece of the puzzle. The young men of the "Birth of the Wolf" were a wolf pack that had been starved and beaten, filled with a directionless rage. This digital pathogen gave them a target. It named the demon they were to hunt.
This was the ideological fuel for the most brutal phases of the collapse. The "us vs. them" narrative of the memes became the literal targeting criteria for the street-level violence of [[The Great Culling]] and the political assassinations of the Assassination Wars. The ironic dog whistle became a rifle sight.
The horror was not that millions of Gen Zers believed in a hollow earth. The horror was that they learned, through this constant, ambient exposure, to believe in the existence of a single, monolithic, and conspiratorial enemy responsible for all the decay and humiliation they felt in their own lives. This was not a meme; it was target acquisition for a civil war.
Act III: The Purity of the Fortress (Post-2060s)
The societies that survived this psychic plague did so because they developed a radical, and often brutal, immune response. The memory of this "Dark Contamination" is the foundational justification for the information architecture of my time.
[[The Sovereign Stacks]] are built on the absolute rejection of the "free marketplace of ideas." That concept is now seen as a catastrophic and suicidal folly, a beautiful theory that, in practice, created the perfect breeding ground for civilizational self-destruction.
The Communication Utilities of the Stacks are not open platforms; they are fortresses. They are ruthlessly curated information environments, designed to reinforce the [[Bounded Truth]] of the tribe and to identify and purge this kind of psychic pathogen before it can spread.
The author of your article, and the few responsible actors at Meta, were fighting for better moderation of an open system. The survivors of the collapse learned a different lesson: the only way to guarantee the sanity and cohesion of your people is to close the system. The price of our internal peace was the death of your open world.
The journalist in your article was documenting the business of hate. We have the hindsight to know she was, in fact, documenting the first, crucial step in the logistics of a coming genocide: the mass psychological mobilization required to convince a generation of broken men that their neighbors were their demons.
