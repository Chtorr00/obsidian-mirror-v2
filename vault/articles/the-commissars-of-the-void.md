# The Commissars of the Void



![The Commissars of the Void](/images/Jan4BernieBro.png)

Bernie Bro Elegy: White male millennial socialists in the age of DEI, By Geoff Shullenberger
Compact Magazine Substack, December 19, 2025
https://compactmag.substack.com/p/bernie-bro-elegy
This artifact is a work of nuanced political ethnography. It is a dispatch from the internal debates of a dying political tribe—the "Bernie Bro" socialists who, by 2025, have become successful, independent media entrepreneurs. The author, analyzing a controversy over "The Lost Generation," identifies a profound hypocrisy: these men claim that white males are "doing fine," yet their own success is entirely predicated on avoiding the very institutions they defend.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a debate about socialism, but as a case study in the formation of the Counter-Elite. The "Bernie Bros," whether they admitted it or not, were early adopters of the same survival strategy as [[The Virtual Koryos]]. They built parallel structures because the main structures were hostile. Their refusal to acknowledge this shared reality was the fatal error that doomed their political project to irrelevance.
Allow me to trace the full history of this "petty-bourgeois freedom," from the podcasts of your time to the silence of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Accidental Dissidents (2020s-2030s)
The author's insight is brilliant: the "Bernie Bros" survived the Great Displacement not by winning the institutional war, but by exiting the battlefield.
The Escape to the Fringe: By becoming "owner-operators" of podcasts and newsletters, they inadvertently pioneered the economic model of [[The Invisible College]]. They proved that the only way for a "brash, assertive" white man to thrive in the Culture Wars Endgame was to build his own platform, outside the reach of HR departments and DEI mandates.
The Denial of Reality: Their refusal to admit why they had to exit—their defense of the very system that expelled them—was a form of cognitive dissonance common in [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]]. They were "socialists" who lived as libertarians, preaching collectivism while practicing extreme individualism to survive.
Act II: The Failure of the Left (2030s-2050s)
The author asks if socialism can break through. History answers: no.
The socialist movement, trapped in its "symbiotic relationship" with the dying liberal institutions, went down with the ship. When the universities and media corporations collapsed during the Great De-Accreditation, their "dirtbag" defenders were left without a foil and without a base.
The "universalist and materialist politics" that might have appealed to the dispossessed masses of [[The Triage Economy]] was abandoned by the Left, which clung to the identity politics of [[The Sterile Plague]].
The "aggressive, opinionated white men" who did want to seize the commanding heights did not become socialists. They joined the Right Wing Coalition and the Virtual Koryos. They realized that if the institutions were broken, the goal was not to socialize them, but to conquer them.
Act III: [[The Guilds]] of the Stack (Post-2060s)
The "petty-bourgeois freedom" of the independent creator became the blueprint for the Guilds of [[The Sovereign Stacks]].
The successful Stacks are not socialist paradises. They are hierarchies of competence. The "owner-operator" model described in this article evolved into [[The Artisan]] class—independent, highly skilled, and fiercely protective of their autonomy.
The "institutional coordination" required to run a complex economy was achieved not by socialism, but by [[The AI Governance Layer]] and [[The Physics of Sovereignty]]. The coordination problem was solved by machines, while humans were left free to pursue the "aggressive irreverence" of creative work within the bounds of the Stack.
The "Bernie Bros" are remembered as a tragic transitional species—men who found the right lifeboat but insisted on rowing it in the wrong direction.
The author of this piece saw the contradiction at the heart of the modern Left. We have the hindsight to know that this contradiction was fatal, and that the future belonged to those who were willing to name the enemy and build the fortress.
