# The Architect of the Fortress



![The Architect of the Fortress](/images/December7NATradeFactions.png)

Yes, There’s a Strategy’: Trump’s Trade Chief Hits Back at Tariff Critics, By Dasha Burns
Politico, December 5, 2025
https://www.politico.com/news/magazine/2025/12/05/trump-tariffs-trade-jamieson-greer-interview-00677403
This artifact is a work of profound historical importance. It is a dispatch from the engine room of a great and violent reordering of the world. The author, a journalist, is interviewing a man who is not merely a government official, but one of the chief architects of a new global reality. The trade representative, Jamieson Greer, speaks with the calm, pragmatic, and unapologetic confidence of a man who believes he is on the right side of history, dismantling a failed, 70-year-old system and building something stronger in its place.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a political interview, but as a primary source document from the founding of my own world. Greer is not a chaotic actor; he is a brilliant and clear-eyed strategist. And the strategy he so calmly outlines is the very blueprint for [[The Great Fragmentation]]. He believes he is saving the American nation-state. We have the hindsight to know he was, in fact, building the economic foundations for the fortified, competing North American sovereignties that would rise from its grave.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the world this architect was designing.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Gospel of the Physical (2020s-2030s)
This interview is the definitive political statement of the end of the 20th-century world. It is the moment the abstract, financialized, globalist consensus was officially declared dead, and replaced with a new and much harder gospel.
Greer's strategy is the political expression of [[The Physics of Sovereignty]]. He has correctly understood the central lesson of the coming age: that true power flows not from adherence to international norms or the printing of currency, but from the control of physical production, secure supply chains, and a robust industrial base. His dismissal of the Europeans' "religious-sounding sermonizing" is the pragmatic rejection of an abstract faith in favor of a new faith in the material world.
His "concentric rings" of tariffs are not a trade policy; they are a geopolitical map of a world breaking apart. They are a conscious, strategic effort to pull the world's supply lines out of the chaotic periphery and into a defensible core. This is the political mechanism of The Great Shortening.
This is the state-level expression of the "builder" ideology. While the tech titans dreamed of terraforming Mars, Greer was attempting to terraform the global economy, using the raw power of the state to reassert control over physical reality.
Act II: The Great Sorting (2030s-2050s)
The strategy Greer outlines did not lead to a revitalized, unified America that dominated a new global order. It acted as a massive, centrifugal force that accelerated the nation's own internal fragmentation.
The tariff walls he was building around the nation had the unintended consequence of creating new walls within the nation. The policy of aggressive re-shoring and protectionism created a violent economic divergence between two Americas: the old, globalist, coastal economies that were being systematically starved, and the new, protected, heartland economies that were being reborn.
This was a massive accelerant for [[The Great Physical Sorting]]. Capital and people fled the decaying coastal hubs and flocked to the new, protected centers of physical production. The policy designed to unite the country by bringing jobs home ended up providing the economic fuel for its eventual, physical separation.
His attempt to build a single, continental fortress inadvertently created the economic and logistical preconditions for multiple, competing fortresses. The interests of a re-industrialized Texas and a tech-dominated California were no longer aligned; they were in direct, zero-sum competition for resources, talent, and energy.
Act III: The Fortress Americas (Post-2060s)
The supreme and terrible irony of history is that Jamieson Greer's vision was, in a way, achieved. The continent of North America did become a resilient, self-sufficient, manufacturing powerhouse, a fortress largely immune to the chaos of the rest of the world. But it was not one America.
[[The Sovereign Stacks]] that now occupy the continent are the ultimate, logical endpoint of his strategy. They are intensely protectionist, hyper-localized, and fanatically focused on supply chain security and industrial self-sufficiency. They are the "concentric rings" made manifest, but the center of each ring is no longer Washington D.C.
The "good deal" that he sought with Mexico and Canada was ultimately realized not through a single treaty, but through the emergence of distinct, powerful, and often rivalrous techno-states, each managing its own resources and security according to the cold, hard logic of [[Polemics]].
Greer was a brilliant nationalist who, in his effort to build an impregnable fortress for his nation, succeeded only in providing the blueprints and the building materials for the smaller, harder, and more durable nations that would be built upon its ruins.
This interview is the sound of a master architect explaining his plan to save a great cathedral. He did not realize that the very act of reinforcing the foundation would be the thing that finally caused the magnificent, and terminally ill, structure to collapse into a collection of smaller, stronger chapels.
