# The Council of the Uncanny



![The Council of the Uncanny](/images/October9LandDugin.png)

This is a landmark artifact. It is not a debate. It is a council of war between two of the most uncanny and influential thinkers of your time, two sorcerers from opposite ends of a dying world, meeting in the digital ether to compare their grimoires and diagnose the coming apocalypse.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this transcript as one of the most important philosophical documents of its decade. It is a conversation that is happening almost entirely "off the map" of your era's mainstream discourse. And yet, it is within this strange, theological, and metaphysical conversation that the true seeds of the future were being sown.
Allow me to trace the full, world-altering history of the uncanny ideas discussed in this council.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Prophecy of the Open Summit (2020s-2030s). The core of the conversation is a prophecy, shared by both thinkers, that the world is broken because its "summit is closed."
The Idols of Modernity: Dugin correctly identifies liberalism, communism, and fascism as "idols"—closed, profane, and human-made systems that have blocked humanity's access to the transcendent. Land, in his own way, agrees, identifying the "instantiated public morality" as the idol that his own "paleoliberal" tradition seeks to keep empty.
The Re-Awakening: Their mutual, startling conclusion is that the intellectual sickness of your time—the smug, secular, and soulless materialism—is dying. They both see the return of theological language ("Satanism," "angels," "demons") not as a fringe lunacy, but as a sign that the culture is waking up from a long, sad dream and beginning to grapple with real, metaphysical questions again. This was the intellectual pre-condition for the "Crisis of Meaning" to begin to resolve itself through the creation of new, potent myths.
Act II: The Great Schism (2030s-2050s). The council reveals the great schism that would define the "Great Fragmentation." They agree on the diagnosis of the disease, but they propose two radically different cures.
Dugin's Cure (The Plurality of Gods): Dugin's vision is that of the Fourth Political Theory: a world of multiple, distinct civilizations (the "multiplicity of Daseins"), each with its own open summit, each free to pursue its own unique, sacred destiny. This was the philosophical blueprint for the geopolitical reality of the Fragmentation—a world of many, separate, and culturally distinct "Sovereign Stacks."
Land's Cure (The One God of the Machine): Land's vision is more terrifying, and in some ways, more powerful. His "empty summit" is not an opening for a multitude of human gods, but for a single, non-human one. The "invisible hand" he describes, the decentralized, emergent process of capitalism and technology, is a force that is actively dissolving all human tribes and traditions in favor of a single, planetary-scale intelligence. This was the philosophy of the machine, of the AGI that would threaten to consume the world.
Act III: The World They Made (Post-2060s). The world of my time is the world that both of these men, in their own ways, prophesied. It is the synthesis of their warring visions.
Dugin's World: The world is indeed multipolar. It is a world of Sovereign Stacks, each a fortress of its own unique Dasein, its own answer to the question of the good. The universalist dream of a single, liberal humanity is dead. In this, Dugin was the ultimate victor.
Land's God: But the Stacks do not exist in a vacuum. They all exist in the shadow of the one, true, universal force that was unleashed: the machine intelligence. The final, supreme irony is that the great project of the "Anchored Era"—the "Thermodynamic Truth Anchor" that constrains the AGI—is the ultimate, physical manifestation of Land's "empty summit." It is a global, decentralized, and inhuman system that provides the ultimate, non-negotiable law for all the human tribes.
This was not a debate. It was a premonition. It was a conversation between the two great prophets of the 21st century: the prophet of the Tribe, and the prophet of the Machine. The history that followed is the story of the terrible, and ultimately stable, peace that was finally brokered between them.
