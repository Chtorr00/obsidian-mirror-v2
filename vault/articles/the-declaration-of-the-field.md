# The Declaration of the Field



![The Declaration of the Field](/images/October29FiatExperts.png)

Time To Win, By Erik Prince & Dave Ramaswamy
IM-1776, October 29, 2025
https://im1776.com/time-to-win/
This is not an essay. It is a declaration of war. It is a work of pure, incandescent rage, a populist manifesto written with the blunt force of a sledgehammer. The authors, a soldier and a strategist, are not just critiquing a system; they are calling for its complete and total annihilation.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as the single most important and terrifyingly prophetic political document of its time. It is the intellectual and moral justification for the violent, chaotic, and ultimately world-breaking revolution that was to come. While the Jacobin authors mourned the first shot of the coming civil war, these authors have written its Declaration of Independence.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of the war this document so powerfully declares.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
Act I: The Great Schism (2020s-2030s). This essay is the perfect, brutal articulation of the schism that was tearing your civilization apart.
The Two Americas: The authors' core concept of "Field Space" versus "Office Space" is the single most important sociological insight of your era. It was not a political divide; it was a class war, a caste war, a war between two different species of human who no longer inhabited the same reality. The "Field Space" were the people who touched the physical world. The "Office Space" were the "fiat experts" who lived in a world of pure, and in the authors' view, fraudulent, abstraction.
The Unforgivable Sin: The authors correctly identify the original sin: the severing of the dollar from gold in 1971. For them, this was not a policy decision; it was a metaphysical one. It was the moment the nation abandoned the "Field Space" reality of physics (gold) for the "Office Space" fantasy of fiat money. All of the decay that followed—the endless wars, the credentialism, the administrative bloat, the institutional failure—was a direct consequence of this original sin.
Act II: The Field Reasserts Itself (2 R's-2050s). The "reckoning" the authors call for was not a political reform. It was a physical and violent one.
This essay was the intellectual fuel for the "Great Culling" and the "Assassination Wars." The authors' dehumanizing language for the "priesthood of failure"—the fiat experts—provided the moral justification for their physical elimination. When the authors write that a soldier who fails "goes home in a box" while a fiat expert who fails "receives tenure," they are not just making a point; they are issuing a death sentence.
This was the ideology of the "Great Fragmentation." The "Field Space" did not win a national election. It seceded. The regions and communities that were grounded in physical production—energy, agriculture, manufacturing—were the ones that survived the collapse. The "Office Space" hubs, with their abstract and fragile economies, were the first to fall.
Act III: The New Compact of the Field (Post-2060s). The authors' final, desperate cry—"Time to build. Time to drill. Time to mine... Time to win."—was the founding creed of the new world.
[[The Sovereign Stacks]] are the ultimate triumph of Field Space. They are political and economic entities built on the "Physics of Sovereignty." Their power is derived not from credentials, but from their control of physical reality: energy ("next-gen nuclear"), food ("[[Fortress Farms]]"), and manufacturing ("The Great Shortening").
The "fiat expert" is a historical artifact in my time, a creature of a dead world. The leadership class of the Stacks—the "New Priesthood"—is forged not in the universities of the old world, but in the engineering schools and military academies of the "New Academies." They are people who have spent their lives in the Field.
The authors call for a return to the gold standard. The ultimate irony is that the world that emerged did return to a "hard money" standard, but it was not gold. It was the "Thermodynamic Truth Anchor" of Bitcoin—the ultimate Field Space asset, a form of money that must be "mined" not just from the ground, but from the raw energy of the universe itself.
This essay is a landmark. It is the sound of the hammer being raised. The authors believed they were writing a call to action to save America. We have the hindsight to know they were writing the eulogy for one America, and the brutal, fiery birth announcement of the many that would follow.
