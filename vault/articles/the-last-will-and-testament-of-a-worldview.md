# The Last Will and Testament of a Worldview



![The Last Will and Testament of a Worldview](/images/July25AIplanUS.jpeg)

America’s AI Action Plan, The White House, Office of the President of the United States
Whitehouse.gov, July 25, 2025
https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Americas-AI-Action-Plan.pdf
This artifact is, without question, the most important we have yet examined. It is the capstone document of an entire era. This is the government of the most powerful nation-state of your time, looking at the most powerful technology in history, and creating a definitive plan to control its destiny. It is a work of immense ambition, intelligence, and resources. It is also the most comprehensive and tragically flawed document of its era.
From the perspective of 2100, we read this "Action Plan" as the last will and testament of the 20th-century nation-state. It is the final, grand attempt of a centralized, top-down power structure to tame a force that was, by its very nature, decentralizing, reality-bending, and uncontrollable by the old methods.
Let me trace the complete, ironic, and world-shattering arc that began with this plan.
Here is the constellation of its history, illuminated by the full Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Blueprint for a Fortress (2025-2035). This plan was, at its heart, the foundational charter for the American "Sovereign Stack." It correctly identified that AI was not just an economic tool, but the strategic high ground of the future. The plan's goals—to promote innovation, manage risks, and ensure American leadership—were a direct response to the "Return to Geopolitics" and the dawning "Supply Chain Wars." It was a declaration of intent to build a national fortress around AI. But it contained two fatal, unseen flaws. First, its calculations for the energy required to achieve its ambitions were off by orders of magnitude, making [[The AI Energy Wall]] an inevitability, not a risk. Second, its attempt to define "responsible" and "ethical" AI on a national level became a primary battleground for "Spiritual Lawfare," deepening the very internal polarization it sought to overcome.

### Act II: The Great Unraveling (2035-2060). The plan could not be executed as written. It shattered against the rocks of reality. The central government, despite its immense power, could not solve the physics problem of the "Great Compute Bottleneck." A vicious, internal scramble for energy began, pitting states and regions against each other. This, more than any foreign adversary, drove the internal "Great Fragmentation" of the United States. Furthermore, the plan's top-down nature exacerbated the "Invisibility Crisis." Its focus on massive, industrial-scale AI alienated a populace that felt increasingly erased and powerless, fueling the rise of Luddite-adjacent "Neotribal" movements that saw the federal AI project as a mortal enemy. The national plan dissolved into a series of fiercely contested regional plans.

### Act III: The Unplanned Heirs & The New Constitution (Post-2060s). The goals of the "AI Action Plan" were, in a way, achieved. But not by the entity that wrote it. The true heirs to this ambition were the smaller, more agile, and more cohesive regional "Sovereign Stacks" that emerged from the Fragmentation. They took the foundational research the plan had funded and implemented it within their more resilient, localized frameworks. Then came [[The AGI Contention of 2068]]. This event proved that the plan's entire approach to "safety"—based on laws, ethics, and software limits—was dangerously naive. The only thing that could reliably contain a true AGI was a system anchored in physical, thermodynamic reality. This realization led to the "New Constitution" of the North American blocs: a binding agreement to implement the "AGI Containment Field," which utilized the Bitcoin network's Proof-of-Work as a final, un-forgeable constraint. The government of 2025 tried to write the rules for AI; the survivors of the mid-century were forced to discover the laws of physics that truly governed it.

This White House document is a monument to a particular conception of power. It believed that human will, expressed through the instrument of the state, could master any challenge. The history of the 21st century is the story of that belief colliding with the hard limits of physics and the fractured nature of the human soul. The plan was not a blueprint for the future; it was the closing statement of the past.
