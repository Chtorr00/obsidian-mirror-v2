# The Schism of the Superman



![The Schism of the Superman](/images/Feb9SchismSuperman.jpg)

Why Nietzsche’s Superman stands AGAINST Silicon Valley’s Transhuman Project, By Uberboyo
Uberboyo Substack, March 26, 2025
https://uberboyo.substack.com/p/why-nietzsches-superman-stands-against
This artifact is a critical piece of intellectual cartography. It is a dispatch from the internal theological debate of the Counter-Elite in the mid-2020s. The author, a "vitalist" philosopher, is drawing a line in the sand between two visions of the post-human future that were, until this moment, often confused by outsiders.
It provides the crucial distinction between [[The Engineered]] Phylum (the Vampires/Vitalists) and [[The Transcended]] Phylum (the Cyborgs). The author is vehemently rejecting the "Silicon Valley" vision of machine-integration in favor of a biological, Nietzschean vision of flesh-optimization.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as the declaration of the Great Moral Schism that would eventually divide [[The Sovereign Stacks]] into hostile biological camps.
Allow me to trace the history of this fork in the evolutionary road.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Definition of the "Tech-Head" Heresy (2020s-2030s)

The author correctly identifies the psychological weakness of the Silicon Valley transhumanist: they are "spiritually avoidant." They seek to escape the human condition through machinery (Neuralink, upload) rather than mastering it through biology.
The Cyborgist Path: The author mocks the "botched cripple with a robotic arm." This is a crude but accurate prediction of the early Transcended. Driven by [[The Shattered Focus]] and the need to merge with AI to remain economically viable, this faction did indeed pursue the path of "hacks" and "updates." They became the "denatured cyborgs" he feared.
The Vitalist Path: The author advocates for the "savage animalistic way." This is the ideology of the Biotech Faction (the "Vampire Elites" from the previous artifact). They rejected the machine interface. They focused on "health maxxing," nutrition, and genetic selection to create the "2-meter tall Dutchman." They wanted to be more animal, not less.
### Act II: The Divergence of the Stacks (2030s-2050s)

As [[The Great Fragmentation]] occurred, these two ideologies became the governing doctrines of rival Sovereign Stacks.
The Silicon Stacks (The Hive): The heirs of the "Tech-Heads" built stacks integrated with [[The AI Governance Layer]] at a neural level. They solved the Crisis of Meaning by dissolving the individual into the network. They achieved a form of immortality, but it was the "half-machine lifeform" the author despised.
The Vitalist Stacks (The Pack): The heirs of Uberboyo and Alamariu built Stacks based on [[The Physics of Sovereignty]] and biological supremacy. They used [[The Great Culling]] as a eugenic pressure cooker. They engineered themselves to be stronger, faster, and more beautiful, viewing the Cyborgs as "bugmen" who had surrendered their souls to the metal.
### Act III: The War of the Phyla (Post-2060s)

In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the conflict between these two groups is the primary fault line of the human species.
The Engineered (Vampires): They view themselves as the only true humans—the "Secure Guarantee of the Future" that Nietzsche wrote of. They treat the baseline humans ([[The Purified]]) as livestock and the Transcended as abominations.
The Transcended (Cyborgs): They view the Engineered as dangerous, unstable anachronisms—super-predators trapped in obsolete meat.
The Irony of Strength: The author argued that "strength is the answer to suffering." History proved him right, but it also proved that there are different kinds of strength. The Vitalists chose the strength of the body; the Cyborgs chose the strength of the network. They survived the collapse, but they emerged on the other side as different species, unable to interbreed or understand one another.
This article is the moment the "Right Wing" fractured into the biological and the mechanical. The author was screaming at the tech billionaires to stop trying to escape their bodies. He didn't realize that they weren't trying to escape; they were trying to build a new container for consciousness, while he was trying to build a better animal.
