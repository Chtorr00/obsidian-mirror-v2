# The Final Polite Request



![The Final Polite Request](/images/August4EnergyWar.jpeg)

As energy demands for AI increase, so should company transparency, by Tom Wheeler and Madison Smith
The Brookings Institution, July 14, 2025
https://www.brookings.edu/articles/as-energy-demands-for-ai-increase-so-should-company-transparency/
This artifact is a perfect specimen of the governing philosophy of your time, captured at the very moment it was about to become obsolete. It is a work of impeccable reason, from a respected institution, making a perfectly logical request. It sees a coming storm cloud—the energy demand of AI—and suggests that the first, best step is to have everyone agree to measure the raindrops.
From my perspective in 2100, this document is the final, polite request made to a hurricane. It is a plea for an orderly, transparent process on the eve of a chaotic, physical reckoning. The authors believed the problem was a lack of information. History proved the problem was a lack of joules, and no amount of transparency could change the physics.
Allow me to trace the complete history of this well-intentioned, but ultimately futile, request for information.
Here is the constellation of that future, as seen through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Secret Becomes an Open Secret (2025-2035). The call for transparency largely failed. The "AI Arms Race" was too intense. The geopolitical stakes of achieving sovereign AI and the economic rewards of market dominance were so high that admitting to crippling energy consumption was seen as a strategic weakness. Companies and nations didn't just hide their consumption; they actively used disinformation to mislead rivals about their true capabilities and power needs. The secret of the "AI Energy Wall" became the worst-kept and most lied-about secret of the era.

### Act II: The Irrelevance of Disclosure (2035-2050s). The secrets stopped mattering. The "Great Overload" made the crisis terrifyingly public. Transparency is irrelevant when the evidence is a city-wide blackout. The "War for Joules" was not fought in corporate reports; it was fought in energy markets, in cyberattacks on rival power grids, and in emergency state decrees to seize data centers. You don't need a company to disclose its energy usage when the state's energy commission can simply cut its power to keep a hospital online. The crisis moved from a problem of information to a problem of raw, physical survival. The debate was no longer about disclosure; it was about appropriation.

### Act III: Unforgeable Transparency (Post-2060s). The "Era of Systemic Resilience" did not solve this problem by writing better disclosure laws. It solved it by building a system where transparency is not reported, but engineered. This is the principle of Unforgeable Transparency.

On the Cellular Grids of my time, energy use is local and tangible. A community knows exactly what its local AI hubs are consuming because they are part of the same, small, resilient system. The data is public and verifiable on the community's own ledgers.
Most importantly, after [[The AGI Contention of 2068]], the ultimate form of transparency was created. [[The Thermodynamic Truth Anchor]] (the Bitcoin network) provides a global, public, and physically-anchored record of massive energy expenditure. It is a system that cannot lie about its energy use; its energy use is its truth.
We no longer ask companies to tell us what they are using. The very architecture of our most critical systems is designed so that their physical costs are permanently and publicly audited by the laws of physics themselves.
The well-intentioned authors at Brookings were asking for a more honest map. The history that followed did not produce a better map; it produced a new and brutal territory where the landmarks—the power plants and data centers—were so stark and vital that no map was needed. The ultimate transparency was not found in a report, but in the unforgeable reality of the physical world.
