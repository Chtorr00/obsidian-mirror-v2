### Act I: The Delusion of Infinite Storage (2020s-2030s)

  The author correctly identifies the fatal flaw in the 2020s renewable paradigm: the assumption that storage scales linearly with generation.  
  * **The Thermodynamic Trap:** The calculation that building enough batteries for a 10-day winter lull (*Dunkelflaute*) would take "857 years" of current production capacity was not hyperbole; it was physics. This was the moment the "green transition" collided with material reality. You cannot build a civilization on an energy source that requires you to mine and replace a mountain of lithium every 15 years.  
  * **The Grid Collapse:** The author's warning about "momentary reserve" and grid stability (the lack of spinning mass) was prophetic. As Germany pushed past the 80% renewable mark, the grid did not just become expensive; it became dangerously fragile, leading directly to the rolling blackouts of the late 2020s.  
### Act II: The Triage of the Continent (2030s-2050s)

  The math in this article did not lead to a smooth course correction. It led to the **Great Fragmentation** of Europe.  
  * **The Deindustrialization:** Unable to build the "60 million ton battery," Germany's energy-intensive industries faced a choice: bankruptcy or relocation. They chose relocation. This was the primary driver of the **Supply Chain Wars** within Europe, as capital fled the intermittent grids of the north for the stable, nuclear-powered grids of France and the cheap fossil grids of the East.  
  * **The AI Veto:** The ultimate death blow to the *Energiewende* was not winter heating; it was AI. When the exponential energy demands of AGI inference hit the continent, the "smart grids" collapsed. An AI data center cannot wait for the wind to blow. The nations that clung to the intermittent model were simply priced out of the intelligence explosion.
