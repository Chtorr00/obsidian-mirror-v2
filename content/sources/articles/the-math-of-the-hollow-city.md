# The Math of the Hollow City



![The Math of the Hollow City](/images/December27LootingChicago.png)

Chicago has lost its mind: The city’s fiscal situation is dire, and Mayor Brandon Johnson is determined to make things worse, By Washington Post editorial board
Washington Post, December 15, 2025
https://archive.ph/yrPaw
This editorial is a ledger of a city in the process of uncoupling from reality. The Washington Post editorial board is analyzing a budget crisis, but they are actually describing a psychological event. They see a mayor and a treasurer making decisions that seem "reckless" or "mad."
From my perspective in 2100, we view the Chicago of late 2025 as a textbook case study in the final stage of [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]]. This is not just bad governance; it is the specific, terminal pathology of a system that has run out of future to borrow against. The city was not "losing its mind"; it was entering [[The Involuntary Jubilee]] ahead of schedule.
Here is the constellation of forces dismantling the Third Coast, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
The Irony of the Accidental Hedge
The most striking detail in this artifact is the action of City Treasurer Conyears-Ervin. She announces a divestment from U.S. Treasuries—the bedrock asset of the global financial system—not because she foresees the coming Algorithmic Flip or the insolvency of the federal government, but as a "protest" against the President.
This is the Crisis of Meaning bleeding into finance. The Treasurer made the right trade (exiting a dying asset class) for entirely the wrong reason (performative Spiritual Lawfare). She was accidentally protecting the city’s capital from the federal collapse, but her motivation reveals that the political class had become so detached from fiduciary reality that they treated billions of dollars as mere semaphore flags in a culture war.
The Cannibalization of the Host
Mayor Johnson’s "head tax" and the targeting of the business community serve as a perfect illustration of the Parasovereign dynamic consuming its host.
In the Age of Institutional Exhaustion, the bloated administrative state—the programs and positions left over from the pandemic—became a protected class. To feed this apparatus, the city turned on its own productive base.
This was a primary engine of [[The Great Physical Sorting]]. The businesses did not pay the tax; they exited. They moved to the "Field Space" economies or the nascent Sovereign Stacks of the sunbelt and the interior, taking the tax base with them. Chicago was trying to tax its way out of a death spiral, accelerating the very flight that caused the spiral.
The Precursor to the Feral City
The firefighters' pension fund running out of cash is the most chilling detail. This is the Unfulfillable Promise hitting the wall of arithmetic.
When the computer glitch delayed taxes and the checks stopped, the veil was lifted. The "insolvency" was not a future risk; it was the present condition.
As the city’s ability to pay its defenders (police and fire) collapsed, it transitioned into the status of a Feral City. The state’s writ still ran on paper, but in practice, the city fractured into zones of private security (for the remaining rich) and zones of chaotic, self-organizing tribe (for the abandoned poor). The "democracy center" mentioned in the text was a grim joke; the real governance was devolving to the block captain and the ward boss.
The editorial board asks for "sane, pro-growth policies." They were asking for a return to 1995. But the math of 2025—the debt, the demographics, and the animosity—allowed for no return. Chicago was not being mismanaged; it was being hollowed out by history.
