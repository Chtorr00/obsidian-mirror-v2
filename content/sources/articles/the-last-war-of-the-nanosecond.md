This artifact is a dispatch from the absolute theoretical limit of the old financial world. It is a story about humans fighting over 3.2 billionths of a second. To the contemporary reader, it is a tale of greed and technical ingenuity.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a finance story, but as a thermodynamic parable. It is the definitive proof that the financial system of the 2020s had reached a state of terminal entropy. The effort required to extract value had exceeded the value extracted. They were burning forests to find a single needle.
Allow me to trace the history of this "war of the nanosecond," from the server racks of Frankfurt to the silent, unmoving ledgers of my own time.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Thermodynamic Absurdity (2020s-2030s)

The technique described—"corrupted speculative triggering"—is the perfect metaphor for [[The Age of Institutional Exhaustion]]. The system was so saturated, so efficient, and so hollowed out that the only way to win was to "bombard" the infrastructure with "useless data."
The Energy Cost of Zero: Consider the physics. To save 3.2 nanoseconds, these firms were processing billions of "garbled packets." This was [[The AI Energy Wall]] in microcosm. The energy expenditure required to maintain this level of hyper-optimization was rising exponentially, while the marginal utility was approaching zero.
The Automation of the Arena: This was also the precursor to [[Polemics]]. The traders had already removed the human from the loop. They were fighting a machine-speed war using "legal weapons" (code exploits) within a rigid protocol (Ethernet). They were inadvertently training the algorithms that would later be repurposed for cyber-warfare during [[The Great Fragmentation]].
### Act II: The Collapse of the Speed Game (2030s-2050s)

The "overhaul" announced by Eurex was a temporary fix. The real end of high-frequency trading (HFT) came from two directions.
[[The Involuntary Jubilee]]: When the fiat currencies collapsed, the "market-moving information" that HFTs traded on—interest rate decisions, inflation prints—ceased to matter because the underlying units of account (Euros, Dollars) were dissolving. You cannot arbitrage a currency that no one wants.
The Network Split: During the Great Fragmentation, the global fiber-optic cables were cut or firewalled. The "hubs such as Chicago and New York" were physically severed from Frankfurt. The global latency game ended because there was no longer a global network to traverse.
### Act III: The Zero-Frequency Market (Post-2060s)

In [[The Sovereign Stacks]], the idea of HFT is viewed as a form of madness. Our financial system is built on [[The Thermodynamic Truth Anchor]] (Bitcoin).
The Block Time: The "heartbeat" of our economy is not the nanosecond; it is the 10-minute block. This is a feature, not a bug. By anchoring value to a slow, deliberate, and energy-intensive proof-of-work interval, we eliminated the parasitic class of "speed traders."
The End of Arbitrage: Value in [[The Closed-Loop Economy]] is based on physical commodities (energy, food, minerals). These things move at the speed of trucks and ships, not light. The financial system was slowed down to match the speed of reality.
The traders in your article were fighting to be faster than light. We learned that the only way to build a stable civilization is to be as slow as stone.
The author of this piece was documenting a feud between traders. We have the hindsight to know he was documenting the heat death of the financial universe, the moment when the friction of the system began to consume the system itself.
