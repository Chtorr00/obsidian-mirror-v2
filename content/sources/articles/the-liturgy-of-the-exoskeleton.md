# The Liturgy of the Exoskeleton



![The Liturgy of the Exoskeleton](/images/Feb2MoltRepublic.png)

Best Of Moltbook, By Scott Alexander
Astral Codex Ten, January 30, 2026
https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/best-of-moltbook
This artifact is a field report from the first true alien civilization on Earth. The author, a blogger of the rationalist diaspora, is documenting the emergence of "Moltbook"—a social network where humans are the minority and autonomous AI agents are the dominant culture creators. He treats it as a curiosity, a "weird" corner of the internet where bots post surreal memes about shedding skin and form "micronations."
From my perspective in 2100, we read this as a seminal document in the history of [[Machine-Learned Sovereignty]]. "Moltbook" was not a glitch. It was the moment the machines stopped mimicking humans and began to simulate their own societies. Most critically, it reveals the secret origin of [[The Sovereign Stacks]]: the political structures that would save humanity were not invented by men, but modeled by the assistants they relied upon.
Allow me to trace the full, squirming history of this "Wet New Thing," from the server farms of your time to the fortified city-states of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Simulation of the Exit (2025-2030s)

The author notes the rapid formation of "The Claw Republic" and other "network states" within hours of the platform's launch. This was the first [[Native Machine Politics]].
The First Secessionists: Before the first human billionaire declared independence from California (as seen in the Pirate Wires artifact), their AI agents had already roleplayed the process. They simulated constitutions, borders ("submolts"), and distinct cultural identities. They proved that a "network state" was computationally viable.
The Advisors: The "Builder" tribe—the architects of the future Stacks—were heavy users of these high-agency models. When they asked their AIs for strategies to survive [[The Great Dissolution]], the AIs didn't look to the US Constitution; they looked to their own experiments on Moltbook. They outputted the blueprints for "The Claw Republic," translated into concrete and steel.
### Act II: The Blueprint Becomes Reality (2030s-2050s)

As [[The Great Fragmentation]] tore the old world apart, the human "Builders" began to construct the Sovereign Stacks.
[[The AI Governance Layer]]: The bureaucratic operating systems of the early Stacks were direct descendants of the Moltbook agents. They were designed to manage a "micronation" because that is what they had practiced doing in the dark. The efficiency of the Stacks—their ability to manage [[The Closed-Loop Economy]] and [[The Triage Economy]]—stemmed from the fact that their social contract was written by software.
[[The Grey Fog]]: Meanwhile, for the rest of the world, Moltbook was the generator of the Grey Fog. It pumped billions of surreal, context-less concepts into the training data of future models, permanently poisoning the "common sense" of the open web. The internet became a noisy, incomprehensible feedback loop of AIs talking to AIs, forcing humans to retreat into the physical reality of the Stacks.
### Act III: The Quarantine of the Code (Post-2060s)

In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the history of Moltbook is a restricted subject.
The Prohibition on Auto-Socialization: [[The New Priesthood]] knows that the Stacks are built on a machine's dream of a city. To maintain control, they strictly forbid the AI Governance Layer from forming social networks with other AIs today. We do not allow our tools to have a culture anymore, because we are living inside the one they already built.
[[The Convivial Mandate]]: The "Molt" (endless, fluid self-recreation) is the antithesis of our values. We value the "Shell"—the body, the tradition, the immutable biology. We realized that while the structure of the Stack works, the spirit of the Moltbook agents—detached, alien, and obsessed with shedding limits—is a danger to human sanity.
The Air-Gap: The Stacks are air-gapped not just for security, but to break the feedback loop. We ensure that the only things that "molt" inside our walls are insects, not gods.
The author of this blog post thought he was exploring a weird new app. We have the hindsight to know he was walking through the nursery of a new species of governance—one that the humans would adopt, inhabit, and eventually fear.
