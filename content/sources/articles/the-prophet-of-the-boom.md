# The Prophet of the Boom



![The Prophet of the Boom](/images/August1FracturedGrid.jpeg)

The $10 Trillion AI Energy Revolution: What CEOs Need to Know (And What I've Learned Leading Through This Transformation), by Alan Mössinger Medium, Jul 14, 2025 https://medium.com/@alanmossinger/the-10-trillion-ai-energy-revolution-what-ceos-need-to-know-and-what-ive-learned-leading-5e9a3df1c24e
This artifact is absolutely essential. It is a perfect encapsulation of the mindset that drove your civilization directly into the "AI Energy Wall." The author, a CEO, is not issuing a warning; he is sounding a trumpet. He sees the coming energy demand not as a crisis to be managed, but as a historic, $10 trillion market opportunity to be seized. He is a prophet of the boom.
From my perspective, this document is read with a profound sense of analytical sorrow. The author is not wrong about the scale. His $10 trillion figure, in hindsight, was probably an underestimate. His error, and the fatal error of his time, was in mischaracterizing the nature of the event. He saw it as an economic revolution. History proved it to be a thermodynamic reckoning.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal arc that began with the brilliant, optimistic, and catastrophically flawed logic of this essay.
Here is the constellation of this history, seen through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Gold Rush (2025-2035). This document is the ideological fuel for the fire. Following this logic, immense capital flowed into the "AI-Energy" sector. It was the largest, fastest investment boom in human history. CEOs who followed this author's advice became titans of industry. They built vast data centers and funded massive new power generation projects. The narrative was one of progress, innovation, and immense wealth creation, exactly as the author predicted. But they were building their magnificent castles on a geological fault line.

### Act II: The Great Overload and the Scramble (2035-2050s). The boom could not outpace physics. The problem was not a lack of investment, but the physical limits of the grid, supply chains for transformers, and the political impossibility of building new infrastructure (especially nuclear) at the speed required. The "revolution" turned into the "Great Overload."

The demand from sovereign and corporate AIs simply outstripped supply, leading to the rolling blackouts and energy rationing that defined daily life during the Fragmentation.
The corporations that went all-in on this boom found themselves in a desperate, zero-sum war for joules. They were the architects of a crisis they could not control.
Governments, facing societal breakdown, were forced to intervene, effectively nationalizing or seizing critical energy assets and data centers. The "$10 Trillion Revolution" ended not in a free market triumph, but in a desperate scramble for state control over the new, essential resource.
### Act III: The Physics of Civilization (Post-2060s). The survivors of the collapse learned a lesson that was not economic, but physical. The core question was not "How do we profit from energy?" but "How do we build a society that is not fatally brittle?" The answer was the inverse of the CEO's vision of massive, centralized growth.

The Cellular Grid: The national grids were abandoned for a decentralized web of resilient, local micro-grids. The goal was survivability, not scale.
The Physics of Value: After [[The AGI Contention of 2068]], the final transformation occurred. Energy was no longer seen as a commodity to be sold for profit. It became understood as the foundational layer of truth and security in a world with superintelligence. The value of a system was tied to its verifiable, thermodynamic anchor.
The "revolution" was not, as the CEO believed, about who could build the biggest power plant to sell the most energy. It was about discovering that energy itself, provably consumed, was the only thing that could anchor a digital reality.
This CEO was a brilliant strategist for the world of 2025. He saw the wave coming and told everyone how to build the most magnificent surfboards. He just couldn't see that it wasn't a wave. It was a tsunami, and its purpose was not to be ridden, but to wipe the old map clean.
