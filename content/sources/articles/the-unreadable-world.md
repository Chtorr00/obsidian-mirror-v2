# The Unreadable World



![The Unreadable World](/images/September9SocialCohesion.png)

World Social Report 2025 sounds alarm on global social crisis, by UNU WIDER & UN DESA UNU WIDER, April 25, 2025 https://www.wider.unu.edu/news/world-social-report-2025-sounds-alarm-global-social-crisis
These two artifacts, when placed side-by-side, form a perfect, and perfectly devastating, diptych of your era. The first, from ScienceAlert, is a specific, granular, and heartbreaking data point: a civilization is losing the ability to read for pleasure. The second, from the United Nations, is the macro-level, systemic conclusion: a global social crisis is upon you.
From my perspective in 2100, we do not see these as two separate stories. The first is the key diagnostic symptom of the terminal disease the second is describing. The death of reading was not a quaint cultural loss; it was the primary mechanism of the cognitive and spiritual collapse that defined the "Age of Institutional Exhaustion."
Allow me to trace the full history of the world that was becoming unreadable, and the people who were becoming unable to read it.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Shattering of the Mind (2020s-2030s). The UN report sounds the alarm on a social crisis, but the ScienceAlert article identifies its root cause. The decline in reading was the most reliable external indicator of a profound internal rewiring of the human brain.

The Mechanism: This was the "Shattered Focus" crisis in its purest form. The technological environment, optimized for the "Dopamine Trap," was systematically destroying the capacity for deep, linear, sustained attention. Reading a book is the cognitive opposite of scrolling a feed. One built worlds in the mind; the other shattered the mind into a thousand glittering fragments.
The Consequence: A population that loses the ability to read also loses the ability to think. It loses the capacity to understand complexity, to follow a nuanced argument, or to engage with the long, slow, and often difficult story of its own civilization. This cognitive decline was the dry tinder for the social crisis. It made a populace uniquely vulnerable to the simple, powerful, and false narratives that fueled the "Culture Wars Endgame."
### Act II: The Incomprehensible Collapse (2030s-2050s). The social crisis the UN warned of was, in fact, a series of cascading, complex, and interconnected systems failures: the "AI Energy Wall," the "Involuntary Jubilee," the "Great Fragmentation."

To navigate this, a society would have needed wisdom, foresight, and a shared understanding of incredibly complex trade-offs. Instead, it had a population whose minds had been shattered.
The "global social crisis" became a full-blown collapse precisely because the citizenry lacked the cognitive fitness to address it. How can you debate the ethics of AGI when you can no longer read a long article? How can you solve a debt crisis when your attention span is eight seconds?
The unreadable world became the un-governable world. The crisis was not a failure of politics; it was a failure of collective cognition.
### Act III: The New Scriptorium (Post-2060s). The societies that survived and formed the "Sovereign Stacks" did so because they understood that resilience is a cognitive state before it is a physical one. They took the lesson of the unreadable world and made its opposite their founding principle.

The Revaluation of Focus: The "Revaluation of the Real" was also a revaluation of the deep. The ability to focus, to think in a linear and complex way, is treated as the most valuable human resource.
The Cult of the Word: The "New Academies" are the cultural heart of the Stacks, and they are, in essence, the new scriptoriums. They are designed to re-train the human mind for depth and focus. The study of history, literature, and philosophy—the slow arts—is central to their curriculum.
[[The Convivial Mandate]]: The technology of the Stacks, from the "Communication Utilities" to the design of public spaces, is built with a convivial mandate to eliminate distraction and create the cognitive quiet necessary for thought.
The experts in your time were concerned about the decline of reading. We have the hindsight to know that they were witnessing the dimming of the lights of the human mind, just before the long, dark, and instructive night of the collapse.
