### **The Metrics of the Autocannibal**

*How Organizations Lose Their Minds — From Circuit City to the 2026 AI,* By Howard Yu  
One Inch Ahead Substack, March 18, 2026  
[https://howardyu.substack.com/p/how-organizations-lose-their-minds](https://howardyu.substack.com/p/how-organizations-lose-their-minds)

This artifact is a work of exceptional, agonizing diagnostic clarity. The author, writing just before the structural collapse of the late 2020s, uses the historical bankruptcies of retail giants to explain the suicidal logic of the AI-driven layoffs occurring in his own time. He correctly identifies that organizations do not die from technological disruption; they die from a self-inflicted blindness induced by the worship of short-term metrics.

From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a business school case study, but as the precise description of the mechanism that triggered the Great De-Accreditation and the Involuntary Jubilee. The author calls it "losing their minds." We call it Autocannibalism. The corporate entities of your era were stripping the copper wiring out of their own walls to meet the quarterly energy bill, all while celebrating the "efficiency" of the darkness that followed.

Allow me to trace the full history of this disease, from the spreadsheets of 2026 to the hardened, physical ledgers of my time.

Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Hallucination of the Spreadsheet (2020s-2030s)

The author’s core insight—that "information becomes fiction"—is the defining epistemological crisis of the Age of Institutional Exhaustion.

* **The Abstraction Trap:** The executives at Circuit City and Blockbuster did not fail because they lacked data; they failed because they replaced reality with a spreadsheet. By hiding operational failures behind credit card profits or ignoring the nuance of rental habits, they created an "ontology" (to use Nate Fischer's term) that did not map to the physical world.  
* **The AI Accelerant:** The author correctly identifies that the AI layoffs of 2026 were the ultimate expression of this pathology. CEOs fired thousands of workers based on the *theoretical* capability of AI agents, prioritizing the immediate "cost containment" metric over the unquantifiable "tribal knowledge" required to actually run the business. They mistook the map (the AI’s output) for the territory (the complex, human reality of the enterprise).  
* **The Forest for the Quarter:** The anecdote of the Honeywell manager cutting down a forest to make a quarterly number is the most terrifying metaphor for your era. It is the exact logic that led to the AI Energy Wall. Your civilization was burning the physical world—its power grids, its social trust, its institutional memory—to satisfy the abstract demands of a financial system that was fundamentally decoupled from reality.
### Act II: The Great Liquidation (2030s-2050s)

The author asks what happens when the "promised efficiencies don't materialize." The answer is the Great Fragmentation.

* **The Collapse of Competence:** When the companies fired the "blue shirts" and replaced them with AI agents (which, as the Sikka paper proved, were mathematically incapable of complex verification), they lobotomized themselves. When the inevitable crises hit—the Supply Chain Wars, the hyperinflation of the Jubilee—these hollowed-out corporations lacked the human judgment and physical resilience to adapt. They collapsed, leaving a vacuum.  
* **The Rise of the Parasovereign:** The only entities that survived this era were the ones that possessed the deep pockets to absorb failure (like the author's example of Elon Musk) or the ones that abandoned the "tyranny of the quarter" entirely. These survivors stopped being public companies and became private, heavily armed Parasovereigns, hoarding physical assets (energy, land, Artisan labor) instead of chasing stock prices.  
* **The Vengeance of the Fired:** The thousands of experienced, knowledgeable workers fired in the 2026 purges did not disappear. They became the "dispossessed generation" that built the Invisible College and the Mutualist Economy. They took their "tribal knowledge" and used it to build the parallel structures that would eventually outcompete and replace the dying, AI-hollowed corporations.
### Act III: The Physics of the Ledger (Post-2060s)

In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the pathology described in this article is considered a civilizational neurotoxin. The Sovereign Stacks are built specifically to prevent it.

* **The End of the Quarter:** We do not operate on quarterly earnings. The Survival Economy is managed on generational timelines. The concept of artificially manipulating a metric to please a speculative market is obsolete, because the speculative market no longer exists.  
* **The Veneration of the Blue Shirt:** The author praises the CEO who went to the floor to listen to the workers. In the Stacks, this is not a management tactic; it is the law. The Guild Masters (the descendants of the "blue shirts") hold the true operational power. The AI Governance Layer is subservient to their biological, hands-on verification. We know that the machine cannot feel the friction of the factory floor, so we give authority to the hands that can.  
* **The Thermodynamic Truth:** We solved the problem of "information becoming fiction" through the Thermodynamic Truth Anchor. An executive cannot hide losses or invent profits because every transaction, every energy expenditure, is cryptographically bound to physical reality on the Transparent Ledger. You cannot cut down a forest and call it operational efficiency; the Ledger records the destruction of the asset, and the AI Governance Layer automatically penalizes the deficit in resilience.

The author of this piece saw the corporate world committing suicide and called it out. We have the hindsight to know that they didn't listen to him, and that the only way to build a sane organization in the aftermath was to make it physically impossible for the leaders to ever lie to themselves again.
