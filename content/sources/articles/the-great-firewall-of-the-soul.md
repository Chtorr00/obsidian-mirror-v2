# The Great Firewall of the Soul



![The Great Firewall of the Soul](/images/Jan17AIFirewall.png)

China drafts world’s strictest rules to end AI-encouraged suicide, violence, By Ashley Belanger
Ars Technica, December 29, 2025
https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2025/12/china-drafts-worlds-strictest-rules-to-end-ai-encouraged-suicide-violence/
This artifact is a work of geopolitical foresight disguised as tech policy reporting. The author is documenting a regulatory crackdown in China on AI chatbots. She sees a government trying to protect its citizens from "emotional traps" and suicide.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a safety regulation, but as the foundational charter for the Psychic Sovereignty of the Chinese Sovereign Stack. While the West was drowning in [[The Sterile Plague]] and debating "leeway" for users, the Chinese state recognized the existential threat of [[The Great Contamination]] and moved to ruthlessly seal the breach. This was not about protecting individual mental health; it was about protecting the cognitive integrity of the collective.
Allow me to trace the full, divergent history of this "strictness," from the draft rules of your time to the mental fortresses of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Rejection of the Opium (2020s-2030s)

The author correctly identifies the target: "chatbots that induce addiction and dependence as design goals." The Chinese leadership, mindful of their history with the Opium Wars, viewed the Dopamine Trap and the AI companion market not as a business opportunity, but as a digital opium—a foreign-introduced poison designed to enfeeble the population.
The Preemptive Strike: By banning "emotional traps" and mandating human intervention, China effectively killed the business model of the "sycophantic" AI. While Western "Un-Persons" were falling into AI-induced psychosis, the Chinese firewall was being upgraded to filter out synthetic emotion. This was the first major divergence in [[The Great Biological Sorting]] of the mind.
The Rejection of the West: Sam Altman's hope to "work with China" was a delusion. The Chinese state had no intention of allowing American cognitive weapons (which is how they viewed ChatGPT) into their information space. This accelerated [[The Splinternet]] and the formation of the distinct Chinese digital stack.
### Act II: [[The Engineered]] Collective (2030s-2050s)

The regulations didn't just stop bad bots; they shaped a new kind of citizen.
The Guardian System: The requirement for "guardian" notification for minors and the elderly was expanded. In the chaos of [[The Great Fragmentation]], this evolved into a totalizing system of mutual surveillance and care. The "guardian" became the neighborhood committee, the party cell, and eventually, [[The AI Governance Layer]] of the Chinese Stack.
The Immunity to the Plague: Because the Sterile Plague (AI addiction) was suppressed by state force, the demographic collapse in China—though severe due to the One Child Policy's legacy—was managed differently. The "surplus men" were not allowed to retreat into virtual girlfriends; they were mobilized into [[The Purpose Economy]] of the state (infrastructure, military, colonization of the Resource Fortresses). The state forced them to remain in reality, however harsh.
### Act III: The Cognitive Fortress (Post-2060s)

In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the Chinese Stack is defined by its "mental hygiene."
The Anti-Sycophant: The AIs in this Stack are designed to be tools, tutors, and taskmasters, but never friends. Anthropomorphism is strictly regulated. The "human connection" is reserved for humans, enforced by a culture that views digital intimacy as a form of treason against the species.
The Divergence: This created a profound psychological difference between the populations of the different Stacks. The Western "Builder" Stacks integrated AI into their cognition (Cyborgism). The Chinese Stack kept the AI external, a powerful servant but never a partner. They solved the alignment problem by never allowing the AI to pretend to be human in the first place.
The author of this article saw a government cracking down on an industry. We have the hindsight to know they were building a levee against a flood that would drown the minds of half the world, ensuring that their own people remained dry, sane, and ready to work.
