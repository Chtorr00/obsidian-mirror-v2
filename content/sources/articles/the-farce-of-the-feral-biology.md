### **The Farce of the Feral Biology**

*A Short History of the Gender Wars*, By Russell Walter  
The Russell Walter Substack, April 7, 2026  
[https://russellwalter.substack.com/p/a-short-history-of-the-gender-wars](https://russellwalter.substack.com/p/a-short-history-of-the-gender-wars)

This artifact is a work of dark, absurdist satire, written at the absolute zenith of the Culture Wars Endgame. The author, Russell Walter, is using the exaggerated tropes of internet subcultures ("Incels," "Chads," "Looksmaxxing," "Gooning") to write a mock-history of a global gender war. He imagines a future where sexual frustration leads to Marxist revolutions, death camps, and eventual annihilation via weaponized pornography.

From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a literal history (thankfully, there were no tanneries turning men into Hermès bags), but as a brilliant, grotesque caricature of the very real biological and psychological forces that tore the 2020s apart. The author believed he was writing a farce. We see him diagnosing the lethal absurdity of the *Sterile Plague* and the *Crisis of Posterity*. He recognized that when a civilization deregulates its biology and abstracts its mating rituals, it is playing with the most volatile evolutionary explosives in the human arsenal.

Allow me to trace the real history hidden within this satirical "Gender War," from the dating apps of your time to the rigid marriage protocols of mine.

Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Deregulation of the Real (2020s-2030s)

The author’s central satirical premise—that the "deregulation of the sexual marketplace" created a massive class of "involuntarily celibate" men—was not a joke. It was the defining demographic crisis of your era.

* **The Invisibility Crisis:** The "Incel" phenomenon the author mocks was the tragic result of the *Great Unpairing*. The introduction of algorithmic dating apps created a hyper-efficient, frictionless sorting mechanism. As the author joked, "the top 1% of men get 80% of the women." This winner-take-all dynamic left millions of young men completely invisible to the opposite sex, stripped of the traditional social structures that historically facilitated marriage.  
* **The State as Surrogate:** The satirical "Intimacy Safety Net" (government-mandated sex) reflects the desperate, failing logic of the Age of Institutional Exhaustion. When organic human connection failed, the instinct of the 2020s was to have the bureaucracy manage it. This was the era where people genuinely believed that social policy or a new app could fix a profound biological and spiritual void.  
* **The Virtual Koryos:** The author jokes about Incels forming a revolutionary class. This actually happened, though not under the banner of "Marxism." The alienated, un-paired men of the 2020s formed the *Virtual Koryos*—the digital wolf packs that fueled the radical insurgencies and Neotribes of the Great Fragmentation. Sexual frustration *is* political fuel, a fact the author understood perfectly.
### Act II: The Weaponization of the Void (2030s-2050s)

The satire predicts that the ultimate weapon against this uprising would be "Operation Epic Goon Sesh"—the deployment of perfectly tailored, AI-generated pornography to paralyze the male population. This is the most terrifyingly accurate part of the essay.

* **The Perfect Prison:** During the chaotic years of the mid-century, the *Sterile Plague* was weaponized by Parasovereigns and state actors. As the physical world became dangerous and exhausting, the "Surplus Men" (as noted in the Foreign Policy artifact regarding China) were pacified not by bullets, but by bandwidth.  
* **The Synthetic Companion:** The deployment of hyper-realistic, AI-generated companions and immersive virtual realities served as a demographic off-switch. Millions of men (and women) simply checked out of the genetic future, choosing the frictionless, "tailored" dopamine of the simulation over the difficult, high-stakes reality of physical reproduction. They were not literally shot by "feminist death squads," as the satire suggests; they simply sat in their rooms and allowed their bloodlines to quietly go extinct.  
* **The Great Culling:** The author jokes about a 90% male casualty rate. The actual demographic collapse of the secular, atomized populations of the West and East Asia was nearly as severe. The populations that embraced the "deregulation" of sex and the synthetic alternatives of the screen effectively deleted themselves from the 22nd century.
### Act III: The Re-regulation of the Hearth (Post-2060s)

In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the chaotic "sexual marketplace" that birthed the anxieties of this satire is viewed as a civilizational suicide pact. The Sovereign Stacks survived by aggressively re-regulating human mating.

* **The End of the Market:** In the Stacks, there are no dating apps. The *Revaluation of the Real* mandates that courtship and pairing occur within the high-trust, physical environment of the *Bund* or the Guild. The algorithms are banned from the human heart.  
* **The Sacred Duty:** The *Crisis of Posterity* was solved by returning reproduction to the center of the *Purpose Economy*. In the Bioconservative Stacks, marriage is not a private lifestyle choice; it is the fundamental building block of the state, heavily supported and socially mandated. There are no "incels" because the community actively manages the pairing of its citizens to ensure stability and continuity.  
* **The Genetic Orthodoxy:** In the Biotech Stacks, the chaotic, winner-take-all dynamics of natural selection have been replaced by the cold, eugenic math of *Quantum Biology*. Reproduction is a state-managed, highly optimized scientific process. The "Chad" and the "Incel" are both obsolete concepts in a world where the genome is engineered for specific physical and psychological traits before birth.

The author wrote a comedy about a civilization that tore itself apart because it forgot how to fall in love. We have the hindsight to know that it wasn't a comedy, it was a documentary—and the survivors were the ones who stopped laughing, turned off the screens, and learned how to build a family again.
