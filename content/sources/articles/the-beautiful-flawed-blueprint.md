# The Beautiful, Flawed Blueprint



![The Beautiful, Flawed Blueprint](/images/July18BlueprintAI.jpeg)

The US' Economic Future in the Era of AI: From disruption to resilience, by Claudia Del Pozo
Friedrich Naumann Foundation, July 18, 2025
https://www.freiheit.org/north-america/us-economic-future-era-ai
This artifact is a perfect distillation of the hope and the profound blind spots of your time. It is a work of great intelligence, laying out a rational, plausible path from the chaos of disruption to a future of resilience. It is the kind of policy document that institutions of your era produced with diligence and sincerity. They were drawing a detailed, logical map.
From the perspective of 2100, we see this document with a deep sense of analytical sorrow. It is a beautiful map for a country that was, unknowingly, already headed in a different direction, driven by forces that this very blueprint fails to account for. It sought to solve a political and economic problem, but the true crises were ones of physics, psychology, and spirit.
Let me illuminate the constellation of forces that rendered this blueprint obsolete before the ink was dry:
The Missing Input: [[The AI Energy Wall]]. The paper speaks of capital, labor, data, and innovation as the key ingredients for the future economy. It misses the single most critical input that would define the next thirty years: energy. The exponential growth in AI capability that the paper assumes would drive prosperity also drove an exponential growth in energy demand. The "disruption" was not just about job losses; it was about the "Great Compute Bottleneck" of the 2030s, where the digital world's ambitions slammed into the physical limits of the electrical grid. The race for AI became a brutal, zero-sum scramble for generating capacity, a factor this economic model does not even see.
The Unprepared Mind: [[The Shattered Focus]]. The document speaks of "resilience" and the need to reskill a disrupted workforce. But it assumes a workforce that is cognitively prepared for that transition. It ignores the parallel crisis we have discussed—the societal "Shattered Focus" induced by the attention economy. The population was being systematically conditioned for shallow, rapid-fire engagement at the very moment the economy required deep, sustained, and adaptive learning. The human "software" was as much a bottleneck as the national power grid.
The Illusion of Cohesion: The Internal Fragmentation. This blueprint is addressed to "the US" as if it were a single, coherent actor that could implement a unified national strategy. History shows the opposite happened. The pressures of AI disruption, energy scarcity, and the ongoing "Crisis of Meaning" did not unite the country; they poured gasoline on its existing political and cultural fractures. The "resilience" that eventually emerged was not the top-down, national resilience envisioned here. It was a bottom-up, regional resilience, as different parts of the country effectively began to form their own "Sovereign Stacks" with different economic philosophies, energy sources, and social contracts.
The Misdiagnosis of "Disruption": The Invisibility Crisis. The paper treats "disruption" as an economic phenomenon—a problem of jobs and GDP that can be managed with safety nets and retraining programs. But for millions of people, the experience was not economic; it was existential. AI-driven efficiency did not just make their jobs obsolete; it made them feel invisible and worthless. This profound sense of being erased as a person fueled the politics of rage and humiliation that made the calm, rational, nationwide solutions proposed in this paper politically impossible to implement.
This document is a monument to the rational optimism of your time. It is a vision of a future that might have been, had the foundations of your world—physical, psychological, and social—not already been crumbling from below.
