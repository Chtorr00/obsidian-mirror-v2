# The Measure of the Schism



![The Measure of the Schism](/images/Jan10PublicTrust.png)

The State of Public Trust in Government 2025
Partnership for Public Service August 12, 2025
https://ourpublicservice.org/publications/the-state-of-public-trust-in-government-2025/
This artifact is a work of bureaucratic polling, presented with the dry neutrality of a non-profit. It is measuring public trust in the federal government. To the authors, the data shows a partisan flip—Republicans trusting the new administration, Democrats losing faith. They see a "predictable" pattern.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this report as the seismograph reading of the final, irreparable fracture of the American political psyche. The "schism" described here is not just a difference of opinion; it is a divergence of reality. This document proves that by 2025, the concept of a "neutral" federal government had ceased to exist in the minds of the people. It was seen only as a weapon, trusted when held by friends and feared when held by enemies.
Allow me to trace the full, brutal history of this divergence, from the polling data of your time to the separate realities of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Death of the Neutral State (2020s-2030s)

The poll confirms the thesis of the "Enemies of the State" essay: the idea of a non-political civil service was dead.
The Weaponization of Trust: The fact that trust in government flipped so violently based on who held the White House proves that the institutions were no longer seen as independent. They were seen as spoils of war. This was the psychological precondition for [[The Great Culling]]. When one side views the bureaucracy as an "enemy occupied territory," the only logical move is to purge it.
The Schedule F Revolution: The "rule changes" mentioned—giving the president power to fire civil servants—were not administrative tweaks. They were the legal mechanism for the dismantling of the administrative state. This was the beginning of the end for the "expert class" as a governing power.
### Act II: The Two Governments (2030s-2050s)

The "diverging views" in the poll were a prophecy. As the federal government became an explicit instrument of partisan warfare, it lost its ability to govern the whole.
The "Blue" states and cities, losing faith in the federal apparatus, began to build their own parallel institutions. They stopped cooperating with federal agencies. This was the beginning of Soft Secession.
The "Red" states, emboldened by their capture of the central machinery, used it to punish their enemies and reward their friends. But this victory was hollow. [[The Involuntary Jubilee]] soon rendered the federal purse empty, and [[The Great Fragmentation]] rendered its edicts unenforceable. The government they fought to control was already dissolving.
### Act III: The Privatized State (Post-2060s)

In the Era of Systemic Resilience, the debate over "trust in government" is obsolete.
The End of the Civil Service: [[The Sovereign Stacks]] do not have a "civil service" in the old sense. They have corporate or feudal administrations, staffed by [[The New Priesthood]]. These are not neutral bureaucrats; they are sworn members of the tribe, vetted for loyalty and competence.
Trust as Engineering: We do not rely on "trust" in institutions. We rely on verification. [[The Transparent Ledger]] and [[The Physics of Sovereignty]] ensure that governance is not a matter of opinion, but of measurable performance. You don't trust the water manager because he's a "civil servant"; you trust him because the water is clean, and if it isn't, the sensors will tell you and he will be replaced by an algorithm.
The authors of this report were measuring the temperature of a fever. They didn't realize the patient was already terminal, and that the only cure would be to let the fever burn the body down so that new, smaller, and harder bodies could be born from the ash.
