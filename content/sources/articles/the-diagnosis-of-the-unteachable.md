# The Diagnosis of the Unteachable



![The Diagnosis of the Unteachable](/images/March8DiagnosisUnteachable.png)

No, thank you very much. You can keep your “democracy” and shove it up your a**, By Hua Bin
Hua's Substack, February 8, 2026
https://huabinoliver.substack.com/p/no-thank-you-very-much-you-can-keep
This artifact is a work of furious, cynical, and ultimately prophetic political autopsy. The author, writing for a dissident, contrarian platform, is performing a vivisection on the corpse of American democracy. He argues that the system didn't fail because of corruption or conspiracy, but because of "mass stupidity"—a moral, not intellectual, failing of the citizenry.
From my perspective in 2100, we read this not as a partisan rant, but as the foundational text for the Post-Democratic Epistemology of [[The Sovereign Stacks]]. The author identified the fatal flaw of the 20th-century order: a complex system cannot be governed by a simplified mind. The "impenetrable dome of imbecility" he describes was the cognitive barrier that prevented the old world from seeing the cliff it was driving over.
Allow me to trace the full history of this "mass stupidity," from the angry blog posts of your time to the rigorous hierarchies of mine.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Collapse of the Civic Mind (2020s-2030s)

The author's concept of "stupidity as a moral failing" aligns perfectly with [[The Shattered Focus]] and the Crisis of Meaning.
The Rejection of Complexity: The author notes that "sloganeering reduces complexity to simplicity." This was the mechanism of [[The Great Contamination]]. The population, overwhelmed by information and unable to process nuance, retreated into the comforting, binary narratives of [[The Neotribes]] (MAGA, Woke, etc.). They didn't want truth; they wanted a team.
The Illusion of Agency: The observation that "voters... are merely part of the scenery" was the bitter pill that the Right Wing Coalition (from the earlier artifact) had to swallow. They thought electing a "savior" would fix the system. They discovered that the system was a "reality show" designed to channel their rage into impotence. This realization fueled the Great Withdrawal.
### Act II: [[The Great Culling]] of the Gullible (2030s-2050s)

The author asks: "Do you trust those guys know what they are doing?" History answered: No.
The Darwinian Correction: When [[The AI Energy Wall]] and [[The Supply Chain Wars]] hit, the "mass stupidity" became a survival liability. The populations that could not "engage in complexity"—who could not understand supply chains, energy physics, or basic hygiene—were the first to fall in [[The Triage Economy]]. The "impenetrable dome" was shattered by hunger and cold.
The End of Universal Suffrage: As the [[Feral Cities]] collapsed, the survivors realized that democracy was a luxury of abundance. You cannot vote on the laws of thermodynamics. The new governance structures—the Männerbunds, [[The Guilds]]—were built on competence, not popularity. The "one person, one vote" system was replaced by "one share, one vote" (Neocameralism) or "one gun, one vote" (Warlordism).
### Act III: The Hierarchy of the Mind (Post-2060s)

The Sovereign Stacks are built on the rejection of the author's "democracy."
The Competence Hierarchy: In the Stacks, political power is proportional to cognitive and physical contribution. [[The New Priesthood]] are the "guardians" who have proven their ability to handle complexity. The "masses" are cared for in [[The Survival Economy]], but they do not govern.
The Education of the Will: [[The New Academies]] are designed to cure the "moral stupidity" Bonhoeffer described. They teach critical thinking, logic, and systems theory as martial arts. The goal is to produce a citizen who cannot be "dumbed down" by a slogan.
The author warned that democracy in a stupid society is dangerous. We agreed. So we abolished the democracy to save the society.
The author of this piece was screaming at a wall of "four-toothed" cultists. He didn't realize that the only way to break the cult was to break the world that fed it.
