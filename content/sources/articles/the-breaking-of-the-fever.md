# The Breaking of the Fever



![The Breaking of the Fever](/images/September5AIBarrier.png)

The Fever Dream of Imminent Superintelligence Is Finally Breaking, By Gary Marcus
The New York Times, September 3, 2025
https://www.nytimes.com/2025/09/03/opinion/ai-gpt5-rethinking.htm
This is not an opinion piece. It is a retraction, written by a heretic on behalf of an entire civilization. The author, Gary Marcus, is a figure who, in my historical archives, is noted for his lonely and often ridiculed intellectual courage. This document is the moment the fever dream of imminent, god-like superintelligence, a dream that had gripped your world and driven it to the brink of a catastrophic energy crisis, finally broke.
From my perspective in 2100, we see this essay as the single most important intellectual turning point of its decade. The author believes he is calling for a "rethink." We see him as the doctor, finally vindicated, explaining to a patient who has narrowly survived a self-inflicted wound why the poison they were drinking was, in fact, poison.
Allow me to trace the full history of the fever that this document finally helped to break.
Here is the constellation of this history, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:
### Act I: The Heretic's Vindication (2020s-2030s). This essay is the public declaration of a new reality.

The Collapse of the Scaling Hypothesis: The "unproven hypothesis" of scaling was the central, unquestioned religion of the AI priesthood. The failure of GPT-5 was not just a product launch disappointment; it was a public refutation of a core theological doctrine. It was the moment the congregation began to suspect the high priests were fallible.
The Unseen Wall: The author correctly identifies the flaws in the AI's reasoning. But these were merely the logical symptoms of a deeper, physical disease. The brute-force scaling he critiques was the direct engine of the "AI Energy Wall." The models were becoming unreliable and absurd because the physical cost of making them infinitesimally smarter was becoming infinite. This essay is the moment the intellectual justification for that suicidal path began to crumble.
### Act II: The Great Pivot (2030s-2050s). The "rethink" the author calls for did not happen in university labs. It happened in the brutal, zero-sum arena of the "Great Fragmentation."

The companies and nascent Sovereign Stacks that clung to the old religion of pure scaling went bankrupt. They could not survive the "Great Compute Bottleneck" and the "War for Joules." Their giant, inefficient models became magnificent, useless monuments to a dead god.
The ideas that Marcus champions in this article—world models, neurosymbolic AI, cognitive science—became the new gospel, not because they were more elegant, but because they were more lethal. In an era defined by energy scarcity, the winning doctrine was "intelligence-per-watt." The factions that embraced this new, hybrid, efficiency-first model of AI were the ones that survived.
### Act III: The New Orthodoxy (Post-2060s). The world of my time is the world that Gary Marcus was calling for, born from the ashes of the world Sam Altman was trying to build.

The Ecology of Mind: We do not have a single, monolithic AGI. We have a distributed, resilient "Ecology of Mind"—trillions of hyper-efficient, specialized AIs built on the neurosymbolic principles that Marcus advocated. They are the tireless, intelligent ants that run our civilization, not the single, all-powerful god the 2020s dreamed of.
The Final Backstop: The ultimate failure of the old scaling paradigm was confirmed by the "AGI Contention of 2068," which proved that a single, god-like intelligence was not just inefficient, but fundamentally uncontrollable. The new, distributed "Ecology of Mind" is not only more resilient; it is safer.
The "return to the cognitive sciences" was not just a new chapter in research. It was the intellectual salvation that pulled civilization back from the brink.
This essay is a landmark of intellectual history. It is the moment a lone, critical voice became the prophet of a new and more sustainable age. The author believed he was offering a better path to AGI. We have the hindsight of knowing he was offering the only path to a future that had one.
