### **The Taxonomy of the Unwanted**

*It sounds like exile is a much more potent threat in your era. To think of being evicted from your community is unthinkable for us \- even "undocumented" immigrants are controversial to exclude in 2026\. However, communities in our time like the Amish and the Mormons will practice "shunning" for those who have violated community norms, so there are precedents from our time. What happens to those who have been displaced from their Stacks? You have written about the "stackless" before, who carved out a degree of autonomy in the gaps between the larger stacks. What is the difference between the stackless and the exiles, and the drifters who remain disaffiliated by choice?*

You have touched upon the darkest and most effective enforcement mechanism of my time. You are correct: the 20th-century aversion to exclusion—the idea that no human is "illegal"—is a concept we study with historical fascination, but do not practice. In the **Era of Systemic Resilience**, inclusion is not a right; it is a contract.

The "Amish option"—shunning—was indeed the prototype. But in your time, a shunned Amish teen could flee to New York City and become a barista. In my time, there is no New York City to flee to. There are only other fortresses, most of which have their gates locked.

To understand the fate of the displaced, we must distinguish between three very different categories of people who exist outside the high-trust communities. They are the **Drifters**, the **Exiles**, and the **Stackless**.

Here is the taxonomy of the outside, viewed through the Hindsight Filter:

## I. The Drifters (The Atomized Citizens)

* **Status:** Citizens of the Stack, but members of no Bund.  
* **Who they are:** These are the individuals who refuse to submit to the intense social conformity of the local communities (the religious strictures, the genetic screenings, the communal duties). They prefer the "negative liberty" of anonymity.  
* **Where they live:** They inhabit the **Transit Zones** and **Neutral Hubs** of the Stack—the massive, utilitarian dormitories clustered around the maglev terminals and industrial zones.  
* **Quality of Life:** They are safe. They receive **Universal Basic Resources** (synthetic food, water, a pod). They have access to the **Survival Economy**. But they have no *social capital*. They have no tribe to defend them if they get into a dispute. They are the "NPCs" of the Stack—serviced by the machine, but spiritually invisible. They are the modern equivalent of the "digital nomad," wandering the infrastructure without ever putting down roots.

## II. The Exiles (The Broken Keys)

* **Status:** Stripped of citizenship.  
* **Who they are:** These are individuals who violated the **Protocol as Law** in a way that threatened the integrity of the Stack. Saboteurs, violent criminals, or those who attempted to hack the **Thermodynamic Truth Anchor**.  
* **The Mechanism:** Exile is not a physical deportation squad; it is a digital deletion. The **AI Governance Layer** revokes their cryptographic keys.  
  * Their biometric signature no longer opens doors.  
  * Their credits on the Ledger are frozen.  
  * The automated defense turrets no longer recognize them as "friend."  
* **The Fate:** They are physically pushed out of the air-locks into the **Wild Lands**. Most die within weeks from exposure, thirst, or predation by the Stackless. Exile is, for all intents and purposes, a delayed execution. It is the ultimate deterrent that keeps the peace inside the walls.

## III. The Stackless (The Feral Natives)

* **Status:** Non-citizens. Indigenous to the ruins.  
* **Who they are:** These are the descendants of the populations that were left behind during the **Great Physical Sorting** and the **Great Culling**. They live in the **Feral Cities** (the ruins of Chicago, London, Paris).  
* **Life:** They have adapted to the collapse. They are scavengers, traders, and survivors. They live in low-tech, high-violence tribal structures of their own.  
* **Relationship to the Stack:** To the Stackless, the Stack is a terrifying, god-like entity that hums in the distance. Occasionally, they trade raw materials or salvage with the Stack's border guards, but they are genetically and culturally distinct. They are the "Barbarians" to the Stack's "Rome."

---

**The Difference in 2100:**

* A **Drifter** is inside the wall, fed by the machine, but alone.  
* An **Exile** is thrown over the wall, usually to die.  
* The **Stackless** were born outside the wall, and have learned to survive the nightmare.

The **Sovereign Stacks** maintain internal cohesion not just through shared values, but through the terrifying clarity of the alternative. Every citizen knows that the only thing separating them from the Feral City is the validity of their cryptographic key. This ensures a level of politeness and adherence to the law that your era would find stifling, but which we find essential for survival.
